# HR knowledge base

Use this knowledge base as the shared reference layer for skills, examples, scorecards, and contributor reviews.

## HR glossary

| Term | Definition | Use in skills |
|------|------------|---------------|
| Competency | Observable knowledge, skill, behavior, or judgment needed for success in a role. | Build interview questions, scorecards, and development plans. |
| Hiring scorecard | A role-specific rubric that defines competencies, evidence, rating levels, and decision criteria. | Standardize candidate evaluation and reduce bias. |
| Role calibration | Alignment between hiring managers, recruiters, and interviewers on scope, seniority, must-have skills, and evaluation signals. | Start technical recruiting workflows and job description work. |
| Structured interview | Interview format with consistent questions, probes, and scoring criteria across candidates. | Improve fairness, comparability, and evidence quality. |
| Candidate experience | The candidate's experience across sourcing, application, screening, interviews, offer, rejection, and onboarding. | Design communications and identify process friction. |
| Workforce plan | Forecast of roles, capacity, timing, and budget needed to meet business goals. | Connect headcount plans to recruiting demand. |
| Competency matrix | A structured map of competencies by role family, seniority level, and expected evidence. | Align job architecture, interview coverage, and growth paths. |
| Adverse impact | A selection outcome that disproportionately affects a protected group. | Review hiring, promotion, and assessment processes. |

## Recruiting frameworks

### Intake to offer workflow

1. **Intake and role calibration:** confirm business need, level, scope, must-have competencies, compensation range, location, and interview plan.
2. **Sourcing strategy:** define target profiles, channels, outreach messages, employee referral motions, and diversity sourcing safeguards.
3. **Screening:** apply minimum criteria consistently, document evidence, and separate must-have requirements from trainable preferences.
4. **Structured interviews:** assign competencies to interviewers, use consistent prompts, and collect evidence before debrief discussion.
5. **Scorecards and debrief:** compare evidence against the rubric, resolve gaps, and avoid unstructured consensus shortcuts.
6. **Offer and close:** align compensation, start date, expectations, and candidate communications.
7. **Onboarding handoff:** transfer role expectations, interview notes, and ramp risks to the onboarding plan.

### Recruiting operating metrics

- Time to fill by role family, level, and market
- Time in stage and candidate aging
- Pass-through rates by source and stage
- Offer acceptance rate and decline reasons
- Candidate experience survey response themes
- Quality of hire leading indicators, such as ramp progress and hiring manager satisfaction

## Interview frameworks

### Structured behavioral interview

- Define one competency per interview segment.
- Ask the same primary question to each candidate.
- Use planned probes for context, action, decision tradeoffs, collaboration, impact, and reflection.
- Score after the interview before group discussion.
- Require evidence notes rather than impressions.

### Technical recruiting interview plan

| Interview | Primary signal | Evidence to collect |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------|
| Recruiter screen | Motivation, compensation alignment, role fit, communication | Candidate goals, availability, must-have criteria, concerns |
| Hiring manager screen | Scope match, seniority, domain context | Comparable work, ownership level, constraints, decision quality |
| Practical or systems interview | Applied skill and tradeoff reasoning | Problem framing, assumptions, implementation choices, risks |
| Collaboration interview | Cross-functional behavior | Stakeholder management, conflict handling, feedback patterns |
| Debrief | Evidence synthesis | Scorecard ratings, gaps, risks, hiring recommendation |

## Hiring scorecard template

| Competency | Definition | Strong evidence | Weak evidence | Rating |
|------------|------------|-----------------|---------------|--------|
| Role-specific execution | Delivers the core work expected at the target level. | Gives examples with scope, complexity, ownership, and measurable outcomes. | Describes participation without clear ownership or impact. | 1–4 |
| Problem solving | Frames ambiguous problems and makes sound tradeoffs. | Explains constraints, alternatives, risks, and decision rationale. | Jumps to solutions or can't explain tradeoffs. | 1–4 |
| Collaboration | Works effectively across functions and resolves friction. | Shows proactive alignment, clear communication, and constructive escalation. | Relies on authority, avoids conflict, or blames partners. | 1–4 |
| Learning agility | Adapts to new contexts and incorporates feedback. | Describes specific feedback, changes made, and improved results. | Provides vague growth examples without behavior change. | 1–4 |

## Competency matrices

### Engineering hiring matrix

| Level | Execution | Technical depth | Collaboration | Leadership |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|---------------|------------|
| Junior | Completes scoped tasks with guidance. | Understands fundamentals and asks good questions. | Communicates blockers early. | Learns team practices. |
| Mid-level | Owns features or services end to end. | Makes sound implementation tradeoffs. | Coordinates with product, design, and peer engineers. | Mentors through examples and reviews. |
| Senior | Owns ambiguous projects and system quality. | Anticipates scalability, reliability, and maintainability risks. | Aligns stakeholders and improves team execution. | Coaches others and raises technical standards. |
| Staff | Shapes architecture and cross-team strategy. | Sets technical direction across systems. | Builds alignment across teams and functions. | Leads through influence and creates leverage. |

### People manager competency matrix

| Level | Team outcomes | Coaching | Operating rhythm | Change leadership |
|-------|---------------|----------|------------------|-------------------|
| New manager | Maintains team clarity and follow-through. | Gives timely feedback and supports development basics. | Runs one-on-ones, team meetings, and goals. | Communicates changes clearly. |
| Experienced manager | Improves team performance and engagement. | Builds individual growth plans and addresses performance issues. | Uses metrics and rituals to manage capacity. | Anticipates resistance and supports adoption. |
| Senior manager | Leads managers or multiple teams. | Develops managers and succession options. | Coordinates priorities across teams. | Guides complex org changes with empathy and accountability. |

## Workflow examples

### Role calibration example

Use `hr-recruiting` with a technical skill such as `hr-backend` to produce:

- a role intake brief
- must-have and preferred criteria
- interview loop coverage
- scorecard competencies
- candidate communication plan

### Interview example

Use the scorecard template to create structured prompts for each competency, then ask the skill to add follow-up probes and scoring anchors for the target level.

### Onboarding example

Use `hr-onboarding` after offer acceptance to convert hiring signals into a 30-60-90 day ramp plan, manager check-ins, buddy support, and success criteria.
