# Role family knowledge base

Use this reference to calibrate HR roles, connect work to repository skills, and create consistent deliverables across recruiting, people operations, rewards, learning, employer brand, and analytics.

Each role family follows the repository taxonomy in [taxonomy.md](./taxonomy.md). Recommended skills point to source skill files in `skills/hr-*/SKILL.md`.

## Recruiter

**Taxonomy fit:** recruiting and technical-recruiting categories; recruiting workflow stages from role calibration through offer management.

### Responsibilities

- Run intake meetings and clarify role scope, must-have criteria, compensation range, interview plan, and hiring timeline.
- Source, screen, and nurture candidates using consistent qualification criteria.
- Manage candidate communications, interview logistics, feedback follow-up, and offer coordination.
- Maintain accurate pipeline data and surface process risks early.
- Partner with hiring managers to improve role clarity, scorecards, and close strategy.

### Success metrics

- Time to slate, time to fill, and time in stage by role family.
- Qualified candidate conversion from screen to hiring manager review.
- Offer acceptance rate and documented decline reasons.
- Candidate experience ratings and response themes.
- Hiring manager satisfaction with slate quality and communication.

### Core competencies

- Role calibration and requirement translation.
- Structured screening and evidence-based note taking.
- Sourcing channel selection and outreach personalization.
- Candidate relationship management.
- Hiring process coordination and stakeholder follow-through.

### Common workflows

- Role intake, job description review, and scorecard alignment.
- Candidate sourcing, screening, shortlisting, and interview scheduling.
- Weekly pipeline review with hiring managers.
- Offer handoff, close plan, and candidate disposition.
- Onboarding handoff with role expectations and risk notes.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-recruiting](../skills/hr-recruiting/SKILL.md) for intake, sourcing, interviews, scorecards, and offer workflows.
- [hr-frontend](../skills/hr-frontend/SKILL.md), [hr-backend](../skills/hr-backend/SKILL.md), [hr-fullstack](../skills/hr-fullstack/SKILL.md), and other technical recruiting skills for role-specific calibration.
- [hr-onboarding](../skills/hr-onboarding/SKILL.md) for post-offer handoffs.
- [hr-vietnam-context](../skills/hr-vietnam-context/SKILL.md) for Vietnam-specific hiring context.

### Example deliverables

- Role intake brief with must-have and preferred criteria.
- Candidate screening guide with knockout criteria and probes.
- Sourcing plan by channel, market, and diversity safeguard.
- Hiring manager pipeline update.
- Offer close plan and candidate handoff summary.

### Common interview questions

- Tell me about a difficult role you filled. How did you recalibrate the search?
- How do you separate must-have requirements from preferences during intake?
- Describe how you handle a hiring manager who gives vague or late feedback.
- What pipeline metrics do you review weekly, and what actions do they trigger?
- How do you document candidate evidence without relying on impressions?

### Career progression

- Coordinator or sourcer: owns scheduling, research, outreach support, and candidate care.
- Recruiter: owns end-to-end requisitions and hiring manager partnership.
- Senior recruiter: handles complex searches, advises on market tradeoffs, and improves process quality.
- Principal recruiter or recruiting manager: sets hiring strategy, coaches recruiters, and owns portfolio-level outcomes.

## Talent acquisition lead

**Taxonomy fit:** recruiting, technical-recruiting, and workforce-strategy categories; full intake-to-offer operating model ownership.

### Responsibilities

- Translate workforce plans into hiring capacity, prioritization, and recruiting operating plans.
- Set recruiting process standards, scorecard quality expectations, and interviewer enablement.
- Manage recruiter workload, agency usage, budget, and hiring manager escalation paths.
- Monitor funnel health, source mix, quality signals, and candidate experience.
- Partner with leadership on hiring tradeoffs, market constraints, and offer strategy.

### Success metrics

- Hiring plan attainment by priority role and quarter.
- Recruiter capacity, requisition load, and aging requisitions.
- Funnel conversion, source quality, and pass-through rates.
- Offer acceptance rate by level, function, and location.
- Interview process compliance and scorecard completion quality.

### Core competencies

- Recruiting strategy and operating rhythm design.
- Workforce planning translation.
- Data-informed prioritization and capacity management.
- Stakeholder influence with executives and hiring managers.
- Recruiter coaching and process governance.

### Common workflows

- Quarterly hiring plan review and recruiter capacity planning.
- Weekly recruiting business review with funnel risks and actions.
- Interview process audit and scorecard calibration.
- Offer strategy review for priority candidates.
- Recruiting process retrospectives and hiring manager enablement.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-workforce-planning](../skills/hr-workforce-planning/SKILL.md) for headcount planning and scenario tradeoffs.
- [hr-recruiting](../skills/hr-recruiting/SKILL.md) for recruiting workflows and scorecards.
- [hr-analytics](../skills/hr-analytics/SKILL.md) for funnel dashboards and operating metrics.
- [hr-compensation-benefits](../skills/hr-compensation-benefits/SKILL.md) for offer range and market alignment.
- Technical role skills such as [hr-ai](../skills/hr-ai/SKILL.md), [hr-data](../skills/hr-data/SKILL.md), [hr-devops](../skills/hr-devops/SKILL.md), and [hr-security](../skills/hr-security/SKILL.md) for specialized hiring portfolios.

### Example deliverables

- Quarterly recruiting operating plan.
- Hiring capacity model by recruiter and role complexity.
- Funnel health dashboard with risk actions.
- Interview process standard and scorecard quality checklist.
- Executive hiring update with tradeoffs and decisions needed.

### Common interview questions

- How do you decide which roles receive recruiting priority when capacity is constrained?
- Describe a time you improved a recruiting funnel. What changed and how did you measure it?
- How do you coach recruiters on intake quality and hiring manager pushback?
- What signals tell you an interview process is producing low-quality evidence?
- How do you balance speed, candidate experience, and quality of hire?

### Career progression

- Senior recruiter: owns complex roles and mentors peers.
- Lead recruiter: coordinates a pod, improves process, and handles escalations.
- TA manager: owns team performance, recruiter capacity, and hiring manager relationships.
- Head of TA: owns recruiting strategy, budget, employer brand alignment, and executive hiring outcomes.

## HRBP

**Taxonomy fit:** people-operations, workforce-strategy, performance, and employee-relations categories; supports manager effectiveness and organization health.

### Responsibilities

- Partner with leaders on workforce planning, organization design, performance, engagement, and employee relations.
- Diagnose people risks using manager input, employee feedback, and HR data.
- Coach managers on feedback, performance documentation, role clarity, and team changes.
- Connect business priorities to people programs and change plans.
- Escalate compliance, conduct, and employee relations issues through the right channels.

### Success metrics

- Engagement, retention, and regretted attrition trends by business unit.
- Performance process completion and calibration quality.
- Manager effectiveness indicators from surveys, feedback, and case trends.
- Workforce plan accuracy and role clarity for priority teams.
- Resolution quality and cycle time for employee relations cases.

### Core competencies

- Business acumen and organization diagnosis.
- Manager coaching and conflict navigation.
- Performance management and talent review facilitation.
- Change management and communication planning.
- HR data interpretation and risk escalation.

### Common workflows

- Monthly business review with people metrics and risks.
- Talent review, succession planning, and performance calibration.
- Manager coaching on feedback, documentation, and team norms.
- Engagement survey interpretation and action planning.
- Employee relations triage and investigation partnership.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-employee-relations](../skills/hr-employee-relations/SKILL.md) for stay interviews, exit interviews, and employee relations matters.
- [hr-performance-management](../skills/hr-performance-management/SKILL.md) for reviews, feedback, and performance improvement plans.
- [hr-conflict-resolution](../skills/hr-conflict-resolution/SKILL.md) for mediation and difficult conversations.
- [hr-workforce-planning](../skills/hr-workforce-planning/SKILL.md) for headcount and capacity planning.
- [hr-analytics](../skills/hr-analytics/SKILL.md) for people metrics and dashboards.

### Example deliverables

- Business unit people plan.
- Talent review summary with succession and retention risks.
- Manager coaching guide for a performance issue.
- Engagement action plan with owners and measures.
- Employee relations case summary and next-step recommendation.

### Common interview questions

- Tell me about a time you influenced a leader who disagreed with your people recommendation.
- How do you diagnose whether a team issue is manager behavior, workload, role clarity, or culture?
- Describe your approach to performance calibration.
- How do you balance employee advocacy with business needs?
- What HR data do you trust, and what data do you treat cautiously?

### Career progression

- HR generalist: handles employee questions, policy support, and program execution.
- HRBP: owns business unit partnership and manager coaching.
- Senior HRBP: advises senior leaders and handles complex organization changes.
- People partner leader: sets business partnership model, develops HRBPs, and shapes talent strategy.

## People operations

**Taxonomy fit:** people-operations, compliance, onboarding, and HR technology categories; owns reliable employee lifecycle operations.

### Responsibilities

- Operate employee lifecycle processes from onboarding through offboarding.
- Maintain HRIS data quality, process documentation, service levels, and employee support channels.
- Coordinate policy implementation, approvals, letters, records, and compliance documentation.
- Improve workflows through automation, templates, and cross-functional handoffs.
- Monitor operational risks and employee experience friction points.

### Success metrics

- Employee request response and resolution time.
- HRIS data accuracy and audit completion rate.
- Onboarding completion, time to productivity signals, and new hire satisfaction.
- Process error rate, rework volume, and policy exception trends.
- Employee self-service adoption and knowledge-base usefulness.

### Core competencies

- Process design and service delivery.
- HRIS administration and data stewardship.
- Compliance-aware documentation.
- Employee communication and case handling.
- Continuous improvement and automation mindset.

### Common workflows

- New hire onboarding setup, document collection, and first-week coordination.
- Employee data changes, contract updates, and approvals.
- Policy rollout, acknowledgement tracking, and employee support.
- Offboarding checklist, access coordination, and exit documentation.
- HR process audit and standard operating procedure refresh.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-onboarding](../skills/hr-onboarding/SKILL.md) for onboarding and offboarding design.
- [hr-technology](../skills/hr-technology/SKILL.md) for HRIS selection, implementation, and workflow automation.
- [hr-compliance](../skills/hr-compliance/SKILL.md) for policy and documentation controls.
- [hr-employee-relations](../skills/hr-employee-relations/SKILL.md) for employee lifecycle feedback.
- [hr-vietnam-context](../skills/hr-vietnam-context/SKILL.md) for Vietnam-specific employment administration.

### Example deliverables

- Employee lifecycle process map.
- Onboarding and offboarding checklist.
- HRIS data audit report.
- Employee support knowledge-base article.
- Policy rollout plan with acknowledgement tracking.

### Common interview questions

- Describe a people process you improved. What was the measurable impact?
- How do you maintain data accuracy across HR systems and manual handoffs?
- Tell me about a time you handled a sensitive employee request.
- How do you prioritize employee service requests when everything feels urgent?
- What controls do you use before launching a new HR workflow?

### Career progression

- People operations coordinator: executes lifecycle tasks and employee support.
- People operations specialist: owns processes, documentation, and HRIS updates.
- People operations manager: manages service delivery, automation, and operational controls.
- Director of people operations: owns operating model, systems roadmap, compliance operations, and employee service strategy.

## Compensation and benefits

**Taxonomy fit:** core-hr, compensation, benefits, and compliance categories; owns rewards design and governance.

### Responsibilities

- Design, maintain, and communicate compensation structures, benefits programs, and rewards policies.
- Conduct market pricing, job evaluation, pay equity review, and salary range governance.
- Support offers, promotions, merit cycles, bonus programs, and benefits renewals.
- Partner with finance, legal, HRBPs, and leaders on cost, fairness, and compliance.
- Explain rewards decisions clearly to managers and employees.

### Success metrics

- Pay range coverage, compa-ratio distribution, and pay equity indicators.
- Offer acceptance and decline reasons tied to compensation.
- Benefits participation, utilization, and employee satisfaction.
- Merit cycle completion quality and exception rate.
- Rewards cost accuracy against budget and forecast.

### Core competencies

- Market pricing and job evaluation.
- Compensation analytics and pay equity review.
- Benefits design and vendor management.
- Rewards governance and policy writing.
- Clear manager enablement for sensitive pay topics.

### Common workflows

- Salary benchmark refresh and range update.
- Offer compensation review and exception approval.
- Annual merit, bonus, and promotion cycle support.
- Benefits renewal, enrollment communication, and vendor review.
- Pay equity audit and remediation planning.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-compensation-benefits](../skills/hr-compensation-benefits/SKILL.md) for pay structures, benefits, rewards analysis, and market benchmarking.
- [hr-analytics](../skills/hr-analytics/SKILL.md) for compensation dashboards and workforce metrics.
- [hr-compliance](../skills/hr-compliance/SKILL.md) for policy, audit, and equal opportunity considerations.
- [hr-recruiting](../skills/hr-recruiting/SKILL.md) for offer strategy alignment.
- [hr-vietnam-context](../skills/hr-vietnam-context/SKILL.md) for Vietnam-specific social insurance, tax, and labor context.

### Example deliverables

- Compensation philosophy and salary range framework.
- Market pricing report for priority roles.
- Pay equity analysis summary.
- Benefits renewal recommendation.
- Manager talking points for merit or offer decisions.

### Common interview questions

- How do you build a salary range when market data is limited?
- Describe a time you found a pay equity concern. What did you recommend?
- How do you balance cost control with competitive offers?
- What information should managers receive before a merit cycle?
- How do you explain a compensation decision that an employee may not like?

### Career progression

- Compensation or benefits analyst: prepares data, benchmarks, reports, and employee support materials.
- Total rewards specialist: owns program operations and manager enablement.
- Compensation and benefits manager: designs programs, governs exceptions, and leads cycles.
- Total rewards leader: sets rewards philosophy, budget strategy, vendor ecosystem, and executive governance.

## Learning and development

**Taxonomy fit:** learning, performance, leadership-development, and employee-engagement categories; owns capability building and growth systems.

### Responsibilities

- Identify capability gaps from business goals, performance data, engagement feedback, and manager input.
- Design learning programs, manager enablement, onboarding learning, and leadership development paths.
- Measure learning transfer, behavior change, and business impact.
- Maintain learning content, facilitation standards, and vendor or platform quality.
- Partner with HRBPs and leaders to connect learning to career progression and performance expectations.

### Success metrics

- Program completion, attendance, and learner satisfaction.
- Skill assessment improvement and behavior-change evidence.
- Manager effectiveness and leadership pipeline indicators.
- Internal mobility, promotion readiness, and succession coverage.
- Learning program impact on performance, engagement, or retention goals.

### Core competencies

- Learning needs analysis and instructional design.
- Facilitation and manager enablement.
- Capability framework and career path design.
- Evaluation design beyond attendance metrics.
- Stakeholder management and program adoption.

### Common workflows

- Training needs analysis for a team, role family, or business priority.
- Learning path design with objectives, practice, and reinforcement.
- Manager training rollout with toolkit and follow-up.
- Leadership development cohort planning.
- Program evaluation and iteration based on outcomes.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-training-development](../skills/hr-training-development/SKILL.md) for training needs, learning design, and program evaluation.
- [hr-leadership-development](../skills/hr-leadership-development/SKILL.md) for manager and leader capability building.
- [hr-performance-management](../skills/hr-performance-management/SKILL.md) for linking growth to performance expectations.
- [hr-onboarding](../skills/hr-onboarding/SKILL.md) for new hire learning paths.
- [hr-employee-engagement](../skills/hr-employee-engagement/SKILL.md) for engagement-informed development priorities.

### Example deliverables

- Training needs analysis summary.
- Role-based learning path.
- Manager enablement toolkit.
- Leadership cohort curriculum.
- Learning impact report with behavior and business indicators.

### Common interview questions

- How do you decide whether training is the right solution to a performance gap?
- Describe a learning program you built from needs analysis through measurement.
- How do you measure behavior change after training?
- What makes manager training stick after the workshop ends?
- How do you adapt learning for different seniority levels or functions?

### Career progression

- L&D coordinator: supports logistics, content updates, and learner communications.
- L&D specialist: designs sessions, facilitates programs, and tracks outcomes.
- L&D manager: owns learning portfolio, vendors, and capability priorities.
- Head of learning: sets enterprise learning strategy, leadership pipeline, and capability architecture.

## Employer branding

**Taxonomy fit:** recruiting, employee-engagement, diversity-inclusion, and technical-recruiting categories; connects employee value proposition to hiring markets.

### Responsibilities

- Define and maintain employee value proposition, talent messaging, and candidate-facing content.
- Partner with TA, marketing, communications, DEI, and hiring teams on campaigns.
- Turn employee stories, role insights, and culture signals into credible market content.
- Measure brand reach, engagement, conversion, and candidate perception.
- Ensure messaging is accurate, inclusive, and aligned with the real employee experience.

### Success metrics

- Career site conversion and source attribution from brand campaigns.
- Candidate awareness, engagement, and nurture conversion.
- Application quality by campaign or content source.
- Social engagement, referral participation, and talent community growth.
- Candidate perception and employee advocacy signals.

### Core competencies

- Talent messaging and content strategy.
- Candidate persona research.
- Campaign planning and channel measurement.
- Inclusive storytelling and brand governance.
- Cross-functional partnership with TA and marketing.

### Common workflows

- Employee value proposition research and message testing.
- Role family campaign plan for priority hiring.
- Career site content update and candidate journey review.
- Employee story collection and approval workflow.
- Campaign performance review with recruiting funnel outcomes.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-recruiting](../skills/hr-recruiting/SKILL.md) for candidate personas, sourcing messages, and hiring funnel alignment.
- [hr-diversity-inclusion](../skills/hr-diversity-inclusion/SKILL.md) for inclusive messaging and representation safeguards.
- [hr-employee-engagement](../skills/hr-employee-engagement/SKILL.md) for employee sentiment and advocacy inputs.
- [hr-frontend](../skills/hr-frontend/SKILL.md), [hr-mobile](../skills/hr-mobile/SKILL.md), [hr-ai](../skills/hr-ai/SKILL.md), and other technical skills for credible role-family content.
- [hr-analytics](../skills/hr-analytics/SKILL.md) for campaign and funnel measurement.

### Example deliverables

- Employee value proposition brief.
- Candidate persona and messaging matrix.
- Role-family campaign plan.
- Career site content audit.
- Employer brand performance dashboard.

### Common interview questions

- How do you make employer brand messaging credible rather than generic?
- Tell me about a campaign that improved recruiting outcomes.
- How do you partner with recruiters and hiring managers on technical hiring content?
- What metrics show whether employer brand work is affecting hiring?
- How do you avoid overstating culture or benefits in candidate-facing materials?

### Career progression

- Employer brand coordinator: supports content calendar, events, and campaign logistics.
- Employer brand specialist: owns content production, employee stories, and channel reporting.
- Employer brand manager: sets campaign strategy and partners with TA leadership.
- Talent brand leader: owns employee value proposition, talent marketing strategy, and global brand governance.

## HR analytics

**Taxonomy fit:** workforce-strategy, analytics, recruiting, performance, and employee-engagement categories; turns people data into decisions and governance.

### Responsibilities

- Define HR metrics, data definitions, dashboard standards, and reporting cadence.
- Analyze workforce, recruiting, retention, engagement, performance, and compensation patterns.
- Partner with HR leaders to turn findings into decisions, actions, and follow-up measures.
- Improve data quality, privacy-aware access, and interpretation discipline.
- Build self-service reporting and explain limitations in plain language.

### Success metrics

- Dashboard adoption and stakeholder decision usage.
- Data accuracy, definition consistency, and report refresh reliability.
- Time to answer priority people questions.
- Action completion and outcome tracking from analytics insights.
- Reduction in duplicate reporting and manual spreadsheet work.

### Core competencies

- HR data modeling and metric definition.
- Data visualization and executive storytelling.
- Statistical reasoning and bias-aware interpretation.
- Data governance, privacy, and access controls.
- Consulting with HRBPs, TA, rewards, and leaders.

### Common workflows

- Monthly people dashboard refresh and insight review.
- Recruiting funnel analysis by source, stage, role family, and market.
- Retention and turnover analysis with segmentation and action planning.
- Engagement survey analysis and driver prioritization.
- Data quality audit and metric definition governance.

### Recommended skills

- [hr-analytics](../skills/hr-analytics/SKILL.md) for HR metrics, dashboards, reporting, and data governance.
- [hr-data](../skills/hr-data/SKILL.md) for data role context, pipelines, and analytics workflows.
- [hr-workforce-planning](../skills/hr-workforce-planning/SKILL.md) for headcount forecasting and scenario planning.
- [hr-recruiting](../skills/hr-recruiting/SKILL.md) for funnel and source analysis.
- [hr-compensation-benefits](../skills/hr-compensation-benefits/SKILL.md) for compensation analysis and pay equity reporting.

### Example deliverables

- HR metrics dictionary.
- Executive people dashboard.
- Recruiting funnel diagnostic.
- Retention risk analysis.
- Engagement insights report with recommended actions.

### Common interview questions

- How do you define a metric so different HR teams calculate it the same way?
- Describe an analytics project that changed a people decision.
- How do you explain uncertainty or data limitations to executives?
- What checks do you run before publishing a dashboard?
- How do you balance self-service reporting with privacy and governance?

### Career progression

- HR reporting analyst: prepares recurring reports and data checks.
- People analyst: builds dashboards, analyzes trends, and supports HR partners.
- Senior people analyst: owns complex studies, metric governance, and executive storytelling.
- People analytics leader: sets analytics strategy, data governance, platform roadmap, and decision science standards.
