# HR recognition and rewards

Design and operate employee recognition programs that reinforce desired behaviors, strengthen culture, and make appreciation a consistent management practice — from peer recognition platforms to formal award programs and service milestones.

## Supported tasks

- Designing end-to-end employee recognition program frameworks
- Creating peer-to-peer recognition systems and platforms
- Developing manager recognition toolkits and training
- Building service anniversary and milestone award programs
- Writing recognition criteria aligned to company values and behaviors
- Designing nomination and award processes for formal recognition
- Creating recognition communication and launch campaigns
- Measuring recognition program effectiveness and participation rates
- Building recognition budgeting models and ROI frameworks
- Developing inclusive recognition practices for distributed and diverse teams

## Key prompts

### Program design

1. "Design a recognition program for a [company size] organization with [budget range]."
2. "Create a peer-to-peer recognition framework aligned to our [company values]."
3. "Build a tiered recognition model covering spot awards, team recognition, and annual awards."
4. "Design a values-based recognition program with nomination criteria and reward tiers."
5. "Develop a recognition strategy that works for both in-office and remote employees."

### Manager and leader enablement

1. "Create a manager recognition toolkit with scripts, examples, and timing guidance."
2. "Write a guide for managers on giving specific, meaningful recognition."
3. "Develop a recognition training module for new managers."
4. "Create examples of value-aligned recognition messages for [company values]."
5. "Write a leadership recognition playbook for public and private appreciation moments."

### Formal awards and programs

1. "Design an annual employee awards program with categories, criteria, and process."
2. "Create a service anniversary program for [5/10/15/20]-year milestones."
3. "Develop a team achievement award process for project-based recognition."
4. "Write nomination guidelines and evaluation criteria for [employee of the year/quarter]."
5. "Design a CEO spotlight recognition program for extraordinary contributions."

### Measurement and culture

1. "Create a recognition program metrics framework tracking participation, frequency, and sentiment."
2. "Develop a recognition culture assessment survey."
3. "Build a recognition ROI model connecting program investment to engagement and retention."
4. "Design a recognition equity audit to ensure fair distribution across teams and demographics."
5. "Create a recognition calendar aligning appreciation moments with company milestones."

## Tips

- Recognition is most impactful when it is timely, specific, and tied to observable behaviors or outcomes.
- Peer recognition programs often have higher engagement than top-down programs—design for horizontal appreciation.
- Train managers to recognize in the style the employee prefers—some value public acknowledgment, others prefer private appreciation.
- Audit recognition distribution data to identify underthanked teams or underrepresented employee groups.
- Separate recognition (non-monetary appreciation) from rewards (monetary or tangible) to prevent recognition becoming purely transactional.
- Build recognition into regular team rituals rather than relying solely on formal programs.
