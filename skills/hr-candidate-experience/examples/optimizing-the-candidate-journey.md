# Optimizing the candidate journey

## Context

A growing organization receives feedback that candidates feel uninformed during long interview intervals. The recruiting team needs to redesign candidate touchpoints to improve candidate satisfaction and conversion.

## Step 1: Map touchpoints and identify friction

Sample prompt: "Draft a candidate journey map highlighting key communication touchpoints from initial application to onboarding."

Expected response: A detailed stage-by-stage journey map highlighting application acknowledgment, screen follow-up, interview prep, post-interview updates, and offer delivery.

## Step 2: Create candidate preparation and status guides

Sample prompt: "Write an interview prep guide template for candidates detailing technical topics, team overview, and interview tips."

Expected response: A professional candidate welcome guide providing clear expectations, panel member backgrounds, and tips for virtual interview setup.

## Step 3: Implement feedback loops and measure CSAT

Sample prompt: "Create a candidate Net Promoter Score (cNPS) survey strategy and questionnaire for post-interview feedback."

Expected response: A short 3-question survey measuring communication timeliness, interviewer professionalism, and overall recommendation score, sent automatically after process completion.

## Workflow summary

The recruiter establishes automated stage updates, delivers preparation guides prior to interview panels, and collects candidate feedback to continuously refine the candidate experience.
