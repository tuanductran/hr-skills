# Enterprise learning strategy

Design and execute enterprise learning strategies that connect capability development to business outcomes — from learning ecosystem architecture and governance to skills-based learning design, vendor management, and learning culture activation.

## Supported tasks

- Designing enterprise learning strategy and multi-year roadmaps
- Building and assessing learning ecosystems and tech stacks
- Aligning learning investment with business and talent priorities
- Designing skills-based learning pathways and curricula
- Evaluating learning program effectiveness and ROI
- Building learning governance and prioritization frameworks
- Designing learning culture activation programs
- Managing learning vendors, content libraries, and partnerships
- Building learning measurement frameworks (Kirkpatrick, ROI)
- Designing learning for specific talent segments (leaders, new hires, high potentials)
- Connecting learning strategy to workforce capability planning
- Building the business case for L&D investment

## Key prompts

### Learning strategy design

1. "Design a 3-year enterprise learning strategy for [company] aligned to [business priorities]."
2. "What learning capabilities does [company] need to build to support [strategic initiative]?"
3. "How do we shift our learning strategy from a program-based to a skills-based model?"
4. "Design a learning strategy for [specific talent segment, for example, new managers, technical specialists, high potentials]."
5. "What are the critical learning priorities for [company] given [business context, for example, AI adoption, rapid growth, transformation]?"
6. "How do we build a learning strategy that is responsive to changing business priorities rather than tied to a fixed annual plan?"

### Learning ecosystem design

1. "Design a learning ecosystem architecture for [company] covering formal, social, and experiential learning."
2. "What learning technology stack serves [company size and context] best and how should it integrate?"
3. "How do we evaluate and select a learning management system for [organization]?"
4. "Design a content strategy that balances build, buy, curate, and AI-generated learning for [company]."
5. "What is the right balance between structured courses, on-the-job learning, coaching, and community for [skill type]?"

### Skills-based learning

1. "Design a skills-based learning pathway for [skill domain] that maps from foundational to advanced levels."
2. "How do we connect our skills taxonomy to learning content so employees can find relevant development resources?"
3. "What assessment approach helps employees identify skills gaps and receive personalized learning recommendations?"
4. "Design a critical skills accelerator program for [skill gap area] that delivers capability at business speed."
5. "How do we build skills-based learning pathways for roles that are changing rapidly due to AI?"

### Learning measurement

1. "Design a learning measurement framework for [company] based on Kirkpatrick's four levels."
2. "How do we calculate the ROI of our learning investment in [program] for a [CFO / CHRO] audience?"
3. "What leading indicators tell us our learning programs are building real capability before we see business results?"
4. "Design a learning analytics dashboard for [L&D director / business leader] that connects learning data to performance outcomes."
5. "How do we evaluate the effectiveness of our external learning vendor partnerships?"

### Learning culture

1. "Design a learning culture activation program for [company] that goes beyond training compliance."
2. "What manager behaviors most enable or inhibit a learning culture and how do we change them?"
3. "How do we create dedicated learning time in a high-workload environment?"
4. "What recognition and social practices reinforce learning as a valued behavior, not just a required activity?"
5. "How do we measure learning culture strength at an organizational level?"

## Tips

- Learning strategy fails when it is divorced from talent strategy; tie every learning priority to a specific capability gap that limits business performance.
- The 70-20-10 model (experience, social, formal) is a useful reminder that classroom and e-learning represent a small fraction of how adults actually build skills — design the full learning ecosystem accordingly.
- Measurement is the most underinvested area in L&D; without it, learning is treated as a cost rather than an investment, making budgets vulnerable during downturns.
- AI is rapidly expanding the menu of scalable, personalized learning options — include AI-powered content curation and adaptive learning in your ecosystem strategy.
- Learning culture is built by leaders modeling curiosity and growth, not by L&D programs alone — get executive sponsors to visibly learn and share what they're learning.
