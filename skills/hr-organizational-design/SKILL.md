# HR organizational design

Design, restructure, and optimize organizational structures and operating models — from spans and layers analysis and RACI charting to restructuring plans and job architecture frameworks that align the organization to its strategy.

## Supported tasks

- Designing organizational structures aligned to business strategy
- Developing operating model frameworks for functions and business units
- Conducting spans and layers analysis to optimize management ratios
- Creating RACI and DACI charts for role clarity and decision rights
- Planning and managing restructuring and workforce reductions
- Designing team structures for agile, matrix, and hybrid models
- Building job architecture frameworks with leveling guides
- Facilitating org design workshops with leadership teams
- Analyzing organizational effectiveness and efficiency gaps
- Developing change management plans for structural transitions

## Key prompts

### Structure design

1. "Design an organizational structure for a [function/business unit] of [headcount] people."
2. "Compare functional, divisional, and matrix org structures for [company context]."
3. "Recommend an optimal spans and layers model for [organization stage/size]."
4. "Design a shared services model for [HR/Finance/Legal] in a multi-entity organization."
5. "Create an org design for a new [product/market/geography] expansion."

### Operating model

1. "Build an operating model canvas for [function] aligned to [business strategy]."
2. "Define decision rights and governance for a [centralized/decentralized] model."
3. "Create a RACI matrix for [process/function] to clarify accountability."
4. "Design a center of excellence model for [HR/Technology/Finance]."
5. "Develop a business partnering model that balances strategic and operational support."

### Restructuring

1. "Create a restructuring project plan for [scope] with milestones and communication steps."
2. "Write a workforce reduction planning framework covering selection criteria and risk controls."
3. "Develop a communication plan for announcing organizational changes to employees."
4. "Design a role transition support plan for employees affected by restructuring."
5. "Build a post-restructuring effectiveness review process."

### Job architecture

1. "Create a job leveling framework for [function] from entry level to executive."
2. "Develop job family definitions and career bands for [industry]."
3. "Write a job architecture governance process for managing role creation and changes."
4. "Build a job title standardization framework to reduce proliferation."
5. "Design a role evaluation methodology aligned to [Hay/Mercer/internal] standards."

## Tips

- Start org design from strategy, not from the current org chart—structure should follow purpose.
- Validate design decisions with workload data, not just intuition about headcount needs.
- Minimize management layers to reduce decision latency; target 5-8 direct reports for most manager roles.
- Communicate the rationale for structural changes clearly—ambiguity drives attrition during transitions.
- Separate org design decisions from people placement decisions to reduce bias and politics.
- Review organizational effectiveness 6-12 months after a major redesign to assess whether the structure is delivering intended outcomes.
