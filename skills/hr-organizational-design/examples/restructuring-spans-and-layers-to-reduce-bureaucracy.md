# Restructuring Spans and Layers to Reduce Bureaucracy

## Context

A 3,000-employee manufacturing firm has grown to 9 management layers, resulting in slow decision-making, diluted accountability, and high administrative overhead. Executive leadership mandates reducing management layers to a maximum of 5 and increasing average manager spans of control from 1:4 to 1:8.

## Step 1: Conduct spans and layers audit

Sample prompt: "Build a spans and layers audit guideline to evaluate manager-to-individual-contributor ratios across business functions."

Expected response: A detailed audit framework analyzing current reporting hierarchies, identifying single-point managers (1:1 or 1:2 reporting relationships), and pinpointing redundant management layers across corporate functions.

## Step 2: Formulate target organizational structure

Sample prompt: "Draft an organizational design proposal for transitioning from a functional structure to a product-line matrix organization."

Expected response: A target org design proposal outlining flattened reporting structures, reclassified manager roles, consolidated supervisory positions, and defined spans targets customized by function (e.g. 1:10 for operations, 1:7 for specialized engineering).

## Step 3: Define decision rights and transition plan

Sample prompt: "Write a decision rights framework using RAPID or RACI for resolving accountability overlaps between centralized marketing and regional sales teams."

Expected response: A RAPID decision framework clarifying who recommends, approves, performs, and inputs into key business decisions, paired with a change impact analysis for affected leaders.

## Workflow summary

The Organizational Design Lead audits current reporting depth, designs a flattened target structure with broader spans of control, and codifies clear decision rights to eliminate managerial bureaucracy.
