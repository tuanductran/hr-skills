# Building AI fluency as an organizational capability

## Context

You are the Chief People Officer at a 1,200-person financial services company. The CEO has announced that AI will be embedded into every business function within 18 months. Currently, less than 10% of the non-technical workforce has meaningfully used AI tools in their work.

The board expects the company to demonstrate measurable AI capability progress at the 12-month mark. Your mandate: build AI fluency as an organizational capability across all 950 non-technical employees within 12 months.

## Step 1 — Define what AI fluency means for this organization

**Sample prompt:**

> "Define AI fluency as an organizational capability for a financial services company. Break it into three levels — foundational, practitioner, and advanced — with observable behaviors at each level. Focus on practical work application, not technical depth."

**Generated AI fluency proficiency levels:**

| Level | Label | Description | Target population |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Foundational | Understands what AI tools can and cannot do. Uses approved AI tools for defined tasks with guidance. Recognizes AI-generated errors and knows when to escalate. | All 950 non-technical employees |
| 2 | Practitioner | Uses AI tools independently across regular work tasks. Designs effective prompts for their domain. Identifies new use cases within their function. Shares learnings with the team. | ~400 employees (team leads, analysts, specialists) |
| 3 | Advanced | Configures and adapts AI tools for function-specific workflows. Coaches peers and evaluates AI tool quality. Partners with technical teams on use case development. | ~80 employees (function leads, senior contributors) |

## Step 2 — Assess the current capability state

**Sample prompt:**

> "Design a rapid AI fluency self-assessment for 950 non-technical employees that gives us directional capability data in under 10 minutes. Include questions that assess practical usage, not just awareness."

**Generated assessment design:**

```text
AI Fluency Pulse Survey — 8 questions, ~8 minutes

Section 1: Current usage (3 questions)
1. How often do you currently use AI tools (e.g. ChatGPT, Copilot, Claude) in your work?
   [Daily / Weekly / Occasionally / Never]

2. Which tasks have you used AI tools for in the past month? (select all that apply)
   [Drafting communications / Summarizing documents / Analyzing data /
    Generating reports / Research / None]

3. When you use AI tools, how confident are you in reviewing and correcting the output?
   [Very confident / Somewhat confident / Unsure / I don't yet use AI tools]

Section 2: Capability self-rating (3 questions)
4. Rate your current ability to write effective prompts for AI tools:
   [1 — No experience / 2 — Basic / 3 — Developing / 4 — Confident]

5. Rate your understanding of where AI tools produce unreliable outputs
   and how to identify errors:
   [1 — No understanding / 2 — Basic / 3 — Developing / 4 — Confident]

6. Rate your ability to identify new use cases for AI tools in your own work:
   [1 — Cannot identify any / 2 — Have a few ideas / 3 — Actively identify and test / 4 — Coach others]

Section 3: Readiness and barriers (2 questions)
7. What is the biggest barrier preventing you from using AI tools more in your work?
   [Don't know which tools to use / Not sure if it's allowed / Concerned about accuracy /
    No time to learn / Haven't found a useful application / Other]

8. How much time per week could you realistically invest in building AI skills over the next 3 months?
   [< 30 minutes / 30–60 minutes / 1–2 hours / > 2 hours]
```

**Baseline results (hypothetical):**

- 68% of employees have never used an AI tool in a work context.
- 22% use AI occasionally, primarily for email drafting.
- 9% use AI weekly across multiple task types.
- Top barriers: "Not sure if it's allowed" (41%), "Haven't found a useful application" (33%).

## Step 3 — Design the capability building program

**Sample prompt:**

> "Design a 12-month AI fluency capability building program for 950 non-technical financial services employees. It must reach Foundational level for all employees and Practitioner level for ~400 by Month 12. Address the specific barriers we identified: policy uncertainty and unclear practical applications."

**Generated program structure:**

```text
Month 1–2: Foundation — "AI at Work" launch

Objectives: Eliminate policy uncertainty. Build awareness and baseline usage.

Components:
- CEO-launched AI policy and usage guide: what tools are approved, what is encouraged,
  what is off-limits for financial data.
- 90-minute live "AI at Work" session for all 950 employees in cohorts of ~50.
  Content: approved tools demo, 3 use cases for your function, first prompt exercise.
- AI Starter Guide: function-specific one-pagers showing the top 5 AI use cases
  for each department (Operations, Finance, Compliance, HR, Sales, Customer Service).

Target: 100% of employees complete the Foundation session by end of Month 2.
Metric: Usage rate rises from 32% to 55% of employees using AI at least monthly.

Month 3–6: Practitioner — "AI in Your Work" activation

Objectives: Embed AI into regular workflows. Build prompt skills. Create peer momentum.

Components:
- 4-week self-paced AI practitioner pathway: prompt engineering, output review,
  use case identification. Available in Workday Learning; ~3 hours total.
- Function-specific AI sprints (Month 4): each function runs a 2-week focused experiment
  applying AI to one high-value workflow. Teams share outcomes in a company-wide forum.
- AI Champions network: identify 1–2 volunteers per function as peer coaches.
  Provide Champions with advanced training and a monthly learning community session.

Target: 400 employees reach Practitioner level by Month 6.
Metric: 40% of employees using AI tools weekly; AI Champions active in all 8 functions.

Month 7–12: Advanced and Embed — "AI as Default" scaling

Objectives: Make AI the default approach for eligible tasks. Build Advanced cohort.
Sustain momentum. Integrate AI capability into performance conversations.

Components:
- Advanced AI cohort program (Month 7–9): 80 employees nominated by function leads.
  12-week program: advanced prompting, use case design, AI evaluation, peer teaching.
- AI use case library: company-wide library of tested AI workflows contributed by
  employees, curated by AI Champions, searchable by function and task type.
- Performance review integration (Month 10): HRBP team adds AI fluency as a
  development conversation topic in year-end reviews for all managers.
- Month 12 capability reassessment: repeat baseline assessment to measure
  proficiency score movement against targets.

Target: All 950 at Foundational; 400 at Practitioner; 80 at Advanced by Month 12.
Metric: Weekly AI usage at 65%+; business impact cases from 3+ functions.
```

## Step 4 — Build the business case for investment

**Sample prompt:**

> "Build a business case for the 12-month AI fluency program covering: investment required, expected capability outcome, and how we will demonstrate business impact at the 12-month board review."

**Generated business case summary:**

```text
AI Fluency Capability Program — Investment and Impact Summary

Investment
- Program design and content development: 120K USD (one-time)
- AI Champion training and community: 30K USD/year
- Technology (LMS, AI tool licenses): 180K USD/year (shared with IT budget)
- People Ops facilitation time: 0.5 FTE equivalent
Total Year 1 investment: ~350K USD

Expected capability outcomes (Month 12)
- 950 employees at Foundational level (baseline: ~300)
- 400 employees at Practitioner level (baseline: ~100)
- 80 employees at Advanced level (baseline: ~15)

Business impact indicators (to measure at Month 12)
- Weekly AI usage rate: target 65% (baseline: 22%)
- Time saved per employee per week: target 45 minutes (to be validated in function sprints)
- Number of documented AI use cases generating measurable productivity gain: target 15+
- Board-facing outcome: demonstrate 3 function-specific business impact cases
  (e.g. Compliance: AI-assisted contract review reduces review time by 40%;
   Operations: AI-assisted report generation saves 2 hours/week per analyst)
```

## Step 5 — Monitor and adapt

**Sample prompt:**

> "Design a quarterly capability review process for the AI fluency program that tracks progress, identifies at-risk functions, and makes investment adjustments before the 12-month board review."

**Generated quarterly review structure:**

```text
Q1 Review (Month 3)
- Foundation session completion rate by function.
- Barrier resolution: has policy uncertainty decreased? (re-pulse 3 questions)
- Usage rate movement.
- AI Champions in place: all 8 functions? Any gaps?

Q2 Review (Month 6)
- Practitioner pathway completion rate.
- Function sprint outcomes: which functions produced strong use cases?
- Usage rate: on track for 55% weekly target?
- At-risk functions: flag any function below 60% Foundation completion for intervention.

Q3 Review (Month 9)
- Advanced cohort progress.
- AI use case library: number of contributions and usage.
- Performance review integration: manager readiness for AI fluency conversations.

Q4 Review (Month 12)
- Capability reassessment results vs. baseline.
- Business impact case documentation.
- Board presentation preparation: 3 function-specific impact stories.
- Year 2 investment recommendation: sustain, scale, or pivot.
```
