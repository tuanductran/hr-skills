# Conducting an Organizational Effectiveness Diagnostic After Rapid Scaling

## Context

Following two years of rapid headcount growth from 200 to 750 employees, a technology company is experiencing declining delivery velocity, increased cross-functional friction between Product and Sales, and rising attrition among senior engineers. The VP of People needs to conduct an organizational effectiveness diagnostic to identify root causes and recommend systemic interventions.

## Step 1: Design the diagnostic framework

Sample prompt: "Build an organizational effectiveness diagnostic framework evaluating leadership alignment, structural efficiency, process execution, and cultural capability."

Expected response: A comprehensive diagnostic framework assessing strategic clarity, role ambiguity, decision bottlenecks, handoff friction, and employee sentiment across key operating divisions.

## Step 2: Survey and audit team dynamics

Sample prompt: "Design a team effectiveness survey instrument assessing role clarity, decision rights, psychological safety, and cross-functional friction."

Expected response: A targeted quantitative and qualitative survey instrument measuring alignment on priorities, clarity of ownership, communication flow, and operational barriers between functional silos.

## Step 3: Prioritize interventions and report findings

Sample prompt: "Create an intervention prioritization matrix to categorize organizational improvement initiatives by effort versus business impact."

Expected response: A structured matrix categorizing short-term quick wins (clarifying decision rights for product launches) and long-term systemic fixes (restructuring product-engineering pods and establishing cross-functional SLAs).

## Workflow summary

The VP of People deploys a structured diagnostic framework, audits cross-departmental friction through targeted instruments, and presents prioritized, effort-impact ranked interventions to executive leadership.
