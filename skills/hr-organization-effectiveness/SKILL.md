# Organization effectiveness

Assess and improve organizational effectiveness at team, function, and enterprise levels — from diagnosing performance blockers and capability gaps to designing interventions, measuring org health, and building operating models that sustain high performance.

## Supported tasks

- Designing and running organizational effectiveness diagnostics
- Assessing team and function performance and health
- Identifying organizational capability gaps limiting business performance
- Designing org effectiveness interventions and improvement plans
- Measuring organization health through surveys and data analysis
- Evaluating operating model fitness for current business strategy
- Facilitating organizational effectiveness reviews with leadership
- Designing team effectiveness improvement programs
- Connecting org effectiveness data to talent and HR strategy
- Building org effectiveness governance and review cadences
- Assessing cross-functional collaboration and decision-making quality
- Reporting org effectiveness insights to business and HR leadership

## Key prompts

### OE diagnostics and assessment

1. "Design an organizational effectiveness diagnostic for [business unit / function] covering [performance, health, capability, culture]."
2. "What diagnostic questions reveal whether a team's performance issues are structural, capability-related, or leadership-driven?"
3. "Run an org effectiveness assessment for [department] and identify the top three improvement priorities."
4. "What data sources should we combine to build a comprehensive picture of org effectiveness in [company]?"
5. "Design a 30-60-90 day organizational health diagnostic for a newly appointed [leader]."
6. "What are the most important organizational effectiveness dimensions to assess during [M&A / restructuring / rapid growth]?"

### Operating model effectiveness

1. "Assess whether our current operating model supports [strategic goal] or is creating friction."
2. "What signs indicate that an operating model is no longer fit for purpose and needs redesign?"
3. "How do we evaluate whether our governance and decision-making model is fast enough for [business context]?"
4. "Design a cross-functional collaboration assessment that identifies where work gets stuck between teams."
5. "What span-of-control, layer count, and role clarity issues are limiting our organizational effectiveness?"

### Team effectiveness

1. "Design a team effectiveness assessment for [leadership team / project team / function] covering [dimensions]."
2. "What are the most reliable indicators of a high-performing versus a struggling team in [context]?"
3. "Design a team effectiveness workshop agenda that helps [team] identify and commit to priority improvements."
4. "How do we distinguish between a team effectiveness problem caused by the team itself versus its structural context?"
5. "Write a team effectiveness improvement plan for [team] addressing [identified gaps]."

### Capability and culture assessment

1. "Assess the organizational capability of [function] against what [business strategy] requires."
2. "What critical capabilities does [company] need to develop to succeed in [strategic context]?"
3. "Design a culture assessment that identifies whether our stated values match our actual organizational behaviors."
4. "How do we measure organizational agility and identify what is slowing decision-making and execution?"

### Org effectiveness governance

1. "Design an organizational health review cadence for [CHRO / business leadership] that surfaces emerging issues early."
2. "What governance forum should own organizational effectiveness across [business units / functions]?"
3. "How do we ensure org effectiveness insights translate into action plans with clear ownership and timelines?"
4. "Build an org effectiveness reporting dashboard for [board / executive team] covering [key health indicators]."

## Tips

- Organizational effectiveness problems are almost always systemic — resist the temptation to diagnose team performance issues as people problems before ruling out structural, process, and leadership root causes.
- The best org effectiveness diagnostics combine quantitative data (engagement, attrition, performance) with qualitative insight (interviews, focus groups) — neither alone tells the full story.
- Speed and quality of decision-making are often the most impactful and least measured dimensions of organizational effectiveness — include them in every assessment.
- Org effectiveness insights are only valuable if they lead to decisions — design your diagnostic and reporting process with action and accountability built in from the start.
- Sustained org effectiveness requires regular measurement cadences, not one-off diagnostics — build annual or bi-annual OE reviews into the governance calendar.
