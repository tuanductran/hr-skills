# HR Coordination and Operations

## Overview

HR coordination is the execution layer of the HR function — scheduling interviews, managing onboarding logistics, coordinating documentation, tracking compliance, and supporting HR programs and processes. It is often undervalued because it is less visible than strategic HR work, and over-relied upon because it is the function that ensures HR commitments are actually delivered.

Strong HR coordination is the difference between an HR team that designs good programs and one that also delivers them reliably.

## Interview and Hiring Coordination

Interview coordination is the most time-visible HR coordination activity — delays here directly affect time-to-hire and candidate experience.

### Scheduling best practices

**Centralize interview scheduling:** Whether handled by a dedicated coordinator or a recruiter, scheduling should be owned by one person per requisition, not distributed across interviewers and candidates. Distributed scheduling creates coordination failures.

**Use scheduling tools:** Calendly, GoodTime, Greenhouse Scheduling, and similar tools reduce back-and-forth for straightforward scheduling. Reserve manual coordination for complex multi-interviewer panels or senior candidate schedules.

**Confirm 24-48 hours in advance:** Send a confirmation with the full interview plan (who, format, duration, location or video link) 24-48 hours before the interview. This reduces no-shows and gives candidates time to prepare.

**Briefing packet for interviewers:** Each interviewer should receive the candidate's CV, their assigned scorecard, the role context, and logistics (call-in information or room booking) at least 24 hours before the interview. Interviewers who show up unprepared damage the candidate experience and the assessment quality.

**Buffer interviewer calendars:** Back-to-back interviews without buffer time produce late starts that cascade through the day. Block 15 minutes of buffer before the candidate's arrival time for the first interviewer, and between sequential interviewers.

### Tracking and documentation

Every active requisition should have a clear tracking view: candidates in each stage, last activity date, next scheduled action, and any blocking issues. Whether this lives in the ATS or a supplementary tracker, the recruiter and coordinator should have the same view.

## Onboarding Coordination

Onboarding logistics are the first operational experience a new hire has of the organization. Failures here — missing equipment, incorrect access, forgotten introductory meetings — create a first impression that is difficult to recover from.

### Pre-boarding checklist (before Day 1)

- IT equipment ordered and configured
- System access provisioned (email, HRIS, ATS if relevant, communication platforms, core tools)
- Building access or remote access credentials arranged
- Desk or workspace assigned (for in-office employees)
- Manager notified of new hire start logistics
- Day 1 schedule created and communicated to new hire
- Buddy or onboarding partner identified and briefed
- Benefits enrollment deadline and instructions communicated

**Owner:** HR coordinator or HR operations, confirmed at least 5 business days before start date.

### Day 1 coordination

- Welcome from direct manager (not just HR) as first experience
- Orientation to essential logistics (where things are, how systems work, who to contact for what)
- Introduction to the team (not just the direct team — relevant cross-functional contacts)
- First-week schedule communicated

### 30/60/90 day check-ins

Scheduled check-ins between the new hire and HR (not only the manager) at 30, 60, and 90 days surface onboarding issues that the new hire may not raise with their manager. These should be structured conversations, not informal coffee chats. Questions should cover: clarity of role, team integration, satisfaction with support received, and any unresolved concerns.

## HR Documentation and Records Management

### Documentation standards

HR documentation should be accurate, complete, and stored in the right system. Common documentation that requires active management:

- Offer letters and employment contracts (signed copies stored in HRIS)
- I-9 and work authorization documentation (Vietnam: work permits for foreign workers)
- Performance review records
- Disciplinary and employee relations documentation
- Benefits enrollment confirmation
- Training and certification records

Documentation stored in email folders, local drives, or personal files rather than the HRIS creates risk when employees leave, when audits occur, or when records need to be retrieved.

### Retention schedules

HR records must be retained for defined periods under employment law. In Vietnam, employee records must generally be retained for the duration of employment plus a defined period after departure. Employment contracts, social insurance records, and labor dispute documentation have specific retention requirements.

Work with legal counsel to define and implement a records retention schedule. Do not delete records informally without confirming the legal retention period has been met.

## Cross-Functional Coordination

HR coordination frequently involves coordinating with functions outside HR: IT for system access, Finance for payroll and budget approvals, Legal for employment agreements and disputes, Facilities for workspace, and line managers for scheduling and approvals.

### Managing cross-functional dependencies

- Identify the dependencies for each HR process that requires cross-functional input
- Establish lead times: how far in advance does IT need to know about a new hire to provision access on Day 1?
- Create shared SLAs: what response time does HR need from each function to meet its own delivery commitments?
- Escalation paths: who do you contact when a cross-functional dependency is blocked?

Cross-functional coordination failures in HR (IT not ready for Day 1, Finance approval delayed for offer) are often blamed on HR even when the root cause is outside HR's control. Managing dependencies proactively and tracking blockers reduces the frequency of these failures and creates clear accountability when they occur.
