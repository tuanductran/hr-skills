# HR Vietnam context

A supplementary skill covering Vietnam-specific employment law, compliance obligations, and cultural practices that apply when operating in or hiring for Vietnam. Use alongside other HR skills to adapt global frameworks to the Vietnam market.

## Supported tasks

- Interpreting and applying the Vietnam Labor Code
- Social Insurance, Health Insurance, and Unemployment Insurance enrollment and reporting
- Personal income tax (PIT) registration and payroll tax compliance
- Work permit and business visa management for foreign workers
- Drafting legally compliant employment contracts under Vietnamese law
- Disciplinary procedures and termination under Vietnam Labor Code
- Trade union obligations and worker representative engagement
- Managing Vietnam-specific leave entitlements (annual, maternity, sick, Tet)
- Adapting global HR policies for Vietnam cultural norms
- Navigating Vietnam's data localization and cybersecurity law

## Tips

- **Always use written contracts.** Verbal agreements have limited enforceability in Vietnam. Every employment relationship must be documented in a signed labor contract before or on the first day of work.
- **The 30-day enrollment rule is strict.** Social Insurance, Health Insurance, Unemployment Insurance, and PIT registration must all be completed within 30 days of the hire date. Late enrollment exposes the company to back contributions plus penalties.
- **Disciplinary procedures are procedurally rigid.** Vietnam's Labor Code requires specific steps (written notice, disciplinary meeting with witness, written decision) even for minor infractions. Skipping steps can void the disciplinary action and expose the company to reinstatement claims.
- **Tet bonus sets cultural expectations.** While not legally mandated, a 13th-month or Tet bonus is a strong cultural norm. Failing to pay it — or paying it late — significantly damages employee trust and retention. Define the policy clearly in contracts or company policy.
- **Work permit timelines are unpredictable.** Begin the work permit application at least 60 days before a foreign employee's start date. Factor in the labor need approval from the Department of Labour — a separate step that adds 10–15 business days before the permit application itself.
- **"Saving face" affects feedback quality.** Vietnamese employees may say "understood" or "yes" to avoid conflict, not because they agree. Build psychological safety through 1-on-1 sessions and anonymous surveys rather than relying on open group feedback.
