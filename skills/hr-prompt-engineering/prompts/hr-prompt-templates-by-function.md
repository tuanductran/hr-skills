# HR prompt templates by function

Ready-to-use prompt templates organized by HR function. Replace bracketed placeholders with your specific values before using.

## Talent acquisition

- "Write a job description for a [role] at [level] reporting to [manager title] in a [company type]. Include a two-sentence company overview, key responsibilities, required qualifications, preferred qualifications, and a note on [remote / hybrid / on-site] work. Use inclusive language and keep the total under 500 words."
- "Generate [number] behavioral interview questions assessing [competency] for a [level] [role]. For each question, include the intended signal and one follow-up probe."
- "Write a structured scorecard for evaluating candidates for [role] against these competencies: [list]. For each competency, include a 1-to-5 rating scale with observable behavioral anchors."
- "Draft a candidate rejection email for a [role] where the candidate reached [stage]. The tone should be respectful and encourage the candidate to apply for future roles."
- "Write an offer letter email for a [role] candidate at [compensation level]. Include: start date, role title, reporting manager, salary, and next steps. Keep the tone warm and professional."
- "Create a sourcing message for reaching out to a passive [role] candidate on [LinkedIn / email]. Keep it under 100 words and focus on the opportunity, not the company pitch."

## Performance management

- "Write a performance review narrative for an employee rated [rating level]. Key achievements this year: [list]. Development areas: [list]. Use a balanced, forward-looking tone. Maximum 300 words."
- "Generate [number] SMART goals for a [role] in [department] focused on [objective]. For each goal, include the metric, timeline, and how success will be measured."
- "Write a performance improvement plan for a [role] with the following performance gap: [describe gap]. Include: expectations, specific improvement actions, milestones at 30 and 60 days, and consequences if performance does not improve."
- "Summarize the following 360-degree feedback comments into key themes for a [role]. Group into: strengths, development areas, and patterns to watch. Comments: [paste comments]."

## Employee communications

- "Write a company-wide communication announcing [change]. Audience: all employees. Tone: [transparent / reassuring / direct]. Include: what is changing, why it is changing, when it takes effect, and what employees should do next."
- "Draft a team communication from [manager title] to [team name] explaining [change or announcement]. Keep it under 200 words. Tone: direct but supportive."
- "Write an FAQ document answering the top [number] questions employees are likely to ask about [policy / change / initiative]. Use plain language appropriate for a non-HR audience."
- "Create a talking-points document for managers to use when discussing [topic] with their teams. Include: key message, how to handle common questions, and what to escalate to HR."

## Learning and development

- "Design a 90-day onboarding plan for a [role] joining [department or team]. Include: Week 1 orientation activities, Month 1 role-specific learning, Month 2 independent contribution, Month 3 full role ownership. Format as a structured table."
- "Write a learning objective for a [topic] training module targeted at [audience]. Use an action verb and a measurable outcome."
- "Create a pre-work prompt for a [workshop / training session] on [topic] for [audience]. Include: a context-setting paragraph, two to three reflection questions, and a short reading or activity to complete in advance."
- "Write a manager development resource on [skill] with: a one-paragraph overview, three practical actions managers can take this week, and one scenario-based practice exercise."

## HR operations and compliance

- "Summarize the following HR policy in plain language for a new employee audience. Highlight the three most important things employees need to know. Keep it under 200 words: [paste policy text]."
- "Write a process guide for [HR process] targeting [audience — e.g., new manager / new employee / HR coordinator]. Include: purpose, step-by-step instructions, common questions, and who to contact for support."
- "Create a checklist for [offboarding / onboarding / leave request / performance review cycle] covering all required steps, owners, and due dates."
- "Draft an HR audit finding summary for [audit area]. Include: finding, risk level, root cause, and recommended corrective action."

## HR business partnering

- "Prepare a talking-points brief for an HRBP meeting with [leader / department head] on [topic]. Include: current state, key risks, recommended actions, and questions to explore in the meeting."
- "Write an analysis memo summarizing [workforce data or people issue] for senior leadership. Format: Executive summary (3 sentences), key findings (bullet list), risks (bullet list), recommended next steps."
- "Generate three strategic options for addressing [people challenge] in [department]. For each option, include: description, pros, cons, and implementation timeline."
