# Building an internal mobility program from scratch

## Context

A 600-person technology company has no formal internal mobility program. Attrition data shows 22% of voluntary departures cite lack of growth opportunities, and exit interviews confirm employees didn't know internal moves were possible. HR is tasked with designing and launching a program within one quarter.

## Step 1: Define the framework

Sample prompt: "Design an internal mobility framework for a 600-person technology company, including eligibility criteria, application process, and manager approval workflow."

Expected response: A framework covering:

- Eligibility: minimum 12 months in current role; no active performance improvement plan; manager informed before applying (not as a condition of approval)
- Process: employee applies through internal job posting; hiring manager interviews; offer contingent on backfill plan agreed within 30 days
- Approval: current manager cannot block an application but must agree on a transition timeline of no more than 8 weeks
- Exceptions: business-critical roles may have a 90-day release window; exceptions require HRBP approval

## Step 2: Create the internal job posting policy

Sample prompt: "Write an internal job posting policy covering minimum tenure, posting timelines, and the internal vs. external sequence."

Expected response: Internal roles are posted for a minimum of 5 business days before external posting. All open roles except confidential or replacement searches must go through the internal posting process first. Internal candidates receive a decision within 2 weeks of application.

## Step 3: Enable managers

Sample prompt: "Draft conversation guides for managers responding to an employee who expresses interest in moving to a different team."

Expected response:

- Acknowledge and welcome the conversation — don't signal that the request is a problem.
- Ask about the employee's career goals, not just the specific role they mentioned.
- Be honest about timing and backfill constraints without making them sound like permanent blocks.
- Commit to supporting the move within a reasonable timeline even if the immediate timing is difficult.
- Log the interest and surface it to the HRBP for pipeline visibility.

## Step 4: Launch and measure

Sample prompt: "Create an internal mobility metrics dashboard covering internal fill rate, time-to-fill for internal moves, and retention uplift."

Expected response: A dashboard with:

- Internal fill rate: % of open roles filled by internal candidates (target: 20-30% at program maturity)
- Internal time-to-fill: average days from internal posting to accepted offer
- Internal vs. external retention: 12-month retention comparison for internally moved vs. externally hired employees
- Mobility rate by function: identifies departments where the program is underutilized

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-internal-mobility` to design a clear, fair framework; equip managers to support rather than block moves; and track the program's impact on retention and employee growth before and after launch.
