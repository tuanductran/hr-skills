# HR internal mobility

Design and operate internal mobility programs that move talent across roles, teams, and projects — reducing regrettable attrition, developing employee careers, and creating a culture where growth happens within the organization.

## Supported tasks

- Designing internal mobility frameworks and policies
- Creating internal job posting processes and communication templates
- Building talent marketplace models for project-based mobility
- Developing manager guides for supporting internal moves
- Writing internal transfer request and approval workflows
- Creating career lattice maps for non-linear growth paths
- Designing rotation programs for high-potential employees
- Measuring internal mobility rates and benchmarking against industry
- Training HR business partners to facilitate internal placement conversations
- Aligning internal mobility with succession planning and retention strategy

## Key prompts

### Program design

1. "Design an internal mobility framework for a [company size] organization."
2. "Create an internal job posting policy with eligibility criteria and timelines."
3. "Build a talent marketplace model for project-based internal mobility."
4. "Develop a rotation program structure for [high-potential/early career] employees."
5. "Create a career lattice framework for [function] showing lateral and vertical paths."

### Process and workflow

1. "Write an internal transfer request process from employee application to start date."
2. "Create an internal interview process guide that differs from external hiring."
3. "Develop a manager approval workflow for internal mobility requests."
4. "Design an internal mobility communication plan for open roles."
5. "Write a manager guide for releasing employees to internal opportunities."

### Employee and manager enablement

1. "Create an employee self-assessment tool for identifying internal mobility readiness."
2. "Write conversation guides for managers discussing internal move aspirations."
3. "Develop a career conversation framework focused on internal growth options."
4. "Design an internal mobility FAQ for employees and managers."
5. "Create onboarding materials specifically for internally transferred employees."

### Measurement and analytics

1. "Build an internal mobility metrics dashboard with fill rate, time-to-fill, and retention impact."
2. "Design a cohort analysis comparing retention of internally mobile vs. non-mobile employees."
3. "Create a benchmark report template for internal vs. external hire performance."
4. "Develop a mobility health index for tracking program effectiveness."

## Tips

- Establish a minimum tenure policy (typically 12-18 months) before employees can apply internally, but make exceptions for critical business needs.
- Train managers to be talent multipliers, not talent hoarders—their willingness to release people drives program success.
- Make internal roles visible before external posting to signal commitment to internal growth.
- Track internal mobility rates by function and level to identify where the program is underutilized.
- Connect internal mobility directly to the employee value proposition and retention messaging.
- Distinguish between mobility types: lateral, upward, cross-functional, and project-based each require different support.
