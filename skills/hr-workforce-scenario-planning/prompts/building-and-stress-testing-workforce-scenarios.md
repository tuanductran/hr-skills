# Building and stress-testing workforce scenarios

Prompts for constructing multi-scenario workforce models, identifying trigger points, pre-planning workforce actions, and facilitating scenario planning sessions with leadership.

## Building the scenario set

- "Build three workforce scenarios (base, upside, downside) for [function or company] over [timeframe]. For each scenario, show headcount by function, key assumptions, and estimated workforce cost."
- "Design a disruption scenario for [company or function] where [AI adoption / regulatory change / competitor disruption] fundamentally changes workforce demand. Show the workforce implications over [timeframe]."
- "What are the most important assumptions to vary across our workforce scenarios given our [industry / business model / current market conditions]?"
- "We only want to build two scenarios for this planning cycle. Help us choose the two that will be most decision-useful for leadership: [describe business context]."
- "Identify the key workforce uncertainties in [industry] that should drive our scenario assumptions for the next [timeframe]."
- "Compare the headcount and cost implications of these three scenarios in a format leadership can use to make investment decisions: [paste scenario summaries]."

## Stress-testing existing plans

- "Stress-test our current workforce plan against a scenario where revenue declines [20%] due to [market contraction / major customer loss]. Show which hiring plans become unaffordable and what actions we should take."
- "Stress-test our hiring plan for [function] against a scenario where the external talent market tightens significantly for [role type]. What are the workforce implications and contingencies?"
- "We plan to hire [number] people in [function] over the next [timeframe]. Stress-test this plan against a hiring freeze scenario and a scenario where we need to accelerate by 30%."
- "Identify the workforce decisions in our current plan that are irreversible or hardest to unwind if conditions shift. Flag these as high-stakes decision points."
- "Which workforce actions in the downside scenario should we pre-authorize now so we can act immediately if the trigger is reached, rather than deliberating under pressure?"

## Trigger points and monitoring

- "Define specific, observable trigger points for each of our [number] workforce scenarios so we know when we are shifting from the base case toward the upside or downside."
- "Design a monthly workforce scenario monitoring dashboard that tracks the indicators most likely to signal a scenario transition: [paste current scenarios]."
- "Write a trigger point definition for the transition from base scenario to downside scenario for [company or function], using [revenue / pipeline / market share / headcount attrition] as leading indicators."
- "How far in advance of a trigger do we need to start pre-planned workforce actions to avoid being caught behind the curve in the [downside / upside] scenario?"

## Pre-planned workforce actions

- "For each of our [number] scenarios, design a set of pre-planned workforce actions so we are not making these decisions from scratch when a trigger fires: [paste scenarios]."
- "What workforce actions should we take in the first 30 days of a hiring freeze that preserve optionality and minimize long-term capability damage?"
- "Design a rapid-scale hiring plan we would activate in the upside scenario, covering: sourcing activation, interviewing acceleration, onboarding at volume, and contingent talent deployment."
- "Which workforce actions in the restructuring scenario should require a final leadership decision when triggered, versus which can be pre-authorized now?"

## Facilitation and leadership alignment

- "Design a 90-minute scenario planning workshop agenda for business and finance leaders covering: scenario review, assumption alignment, trigger point discussion, and pre-planned action sign-off."
- "Write the pre-work brief for a scenario planning session that explains what scenarios are, why we are building them, and what decisions we will make in the session."
- "How do we keep a scenario planning workshop productive when leaders anchor on the base scenario and resist engaging seriously with the downside?"
- "Write a one-page decision brief for the executive team showing which scenario we are currently tracking toward based on the latest [revenue / pipeline / market] data, and what that implies for workforce decisions this quarter."
- "Present these three workforce scenarios to the board in a format that informs capital allocation without implying false precision about which future will occur: [paste scenarios]."

## Scenario updates and cadence

- "Our business conditions have shifted significantly since we built our workforce scenarios [timeframe] ago. Run a lightweight assumption check: which scenario variables need to be updated, and does this change our view of which scenario we are tracking toward?"
- "Design a quarterly workforce scenario review cadence covering: what we monitor monthly, what we review quarterly, and when we trigger a full scenario refresh."
- "Our base scenario is no longer plausible. Help us quickly rebuild the base case using these updated business assumptions, and adjust the upside and downside bounds accordingly: [paste new assumptions]."
