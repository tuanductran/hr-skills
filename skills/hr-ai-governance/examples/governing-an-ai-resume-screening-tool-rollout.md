# Governing an AI Resume-Screening Tool Rollout

## Context

A mid-size company wants to adopt a third-party AI resume-screening tool to reduce recruiter workload, but legal and HR want proper governance in place before launch.

## Step 1: Vendor due diligence

Sample prompt: "Create a checklist to evaluate an AI vendor's fairness and transparency practices."

Expected response: A checklist covering training data sources, independent bias audit results, explainability of scoring, and data privacy/retention practices, to be completed before signing a contract.

## Step 2: Design the review and disclosure process

Sample prompt: "Design a governance review process required before adopting a new HR AI tool" and "Write an employee-facing disclosure explaining how AI is used in the hiring process."

Expected response: A governance gate requiring legal, HR, and IT security sign-off, plus a candidate-facing notice explaining that an AI tool assists initial screening and that a recruiter reviews all shortlisting decisions.

## Step 3: Build human-in-the-loop and monitoring

Sample prompt: "Write a human-in-the-loop requirement for AI-assisted hiring decisions" and "Design a quarterly bias re-testing process for a deployed hiring algorithm."

Expected response: A requirement that no candidate is rejected based solely on the tool's score without recruiter review, plus a quarterly audit comparing pass-through rates across demographic groups.

## Workflow summary

The company vets the vendor thoroughly, builds a governance gate with clear disclosure, and requires ongoing human review and bias monitoring rather than treating the tool as a fully automated black box.
