# HR AI Governance

## Overview

HR AI governance covers the policies, review processes, and safeguards organizations put in place to use AI responsibly across the employee lifecycle — hiring, performance management, monitoring, and more — while managing legal and ethical risk.

## Main topics

### Regulatory landscape

A growing number of jurisdictions regulate "automated employment decision tools," typically requiring bias audits, candidate notice, and options to request an alternative process. Requirements vary significantly by jurisdiction and evolve quickly.

### Bias and disparate impact

AI hiring and screening tools can encode bias present in historical training data. Bias audits test whether a tool produces disparate outcomes across protected groups, ideally before deployment and periodically afterward.

### Human-in-the-loop review

Best practice keeps a human reviewer responsible for any AI-assisted decision with material consequences, using AI output as one input rather than the final determination.

### Vendor due diligence

Before adopting a third-party HR AI tool, organizations should request documentation on training data sources, validation/bias testing methodology, and how the vendor handles employee/candidate data privacy.

### Disclosure and employee rights

Transparent, plain-language disclosure to candidates and employees about where and how AI is used builds trust and is increasingly a legal requirement, often paired with a right to request human review or an alternative process.

## Practical guidance

- Build an internal governance review gate before any new HR AI tool goes live.
- Require documented human review for consequential decisions, not just a rubber-stamp step.
- Re-test deployed tools for bias periodically, not only at initial rollout.
- Keep disclosure language plain and specific about what the AI tool does and doesn't decide.

## Conclusion

Responsible HR AI governance balances the efficiency AI tools offer with the legal, ethical, and reputational risks of automated decisions about people's employment — requiring ongoing vigilance rather than a one-time policy.
