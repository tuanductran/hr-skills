# Talent CRM

Design and operate a talent relationship management system that segments candidate pools, automates nurture campaigns, and keeps warm talent engaged ahead of actual hiring need.

## Supported tasks

- Designing talent CRM segmentation by skill, function, seniority, and interest
- Building automated nurture campaign sequences within a talent CRM
- Re-engaging silver medalists and past applicants systematically
- Structuring talent communities and talent network sign-up experiences
- Setting data governance and consent rules for talent CRM records
- Integrating talent CRM data with the ATS and sourcing tools
- Designing event-based triggers for CRM outreach (new posting, milestone, content)
- Measuring talent CRM engagement and conversion into applicants
- Cleaning and deduplicating talent CRM records over time
- Building talent community content (newsletters, events, resources)
- Training recruiters to use talent CRM effectively alongside active sourcing
- Auditing talent CRM ROI against pipeline generated

## Key prompts

### Segmentation and structure

1. "Design talent CRM segments for [function/company] based on skill, seniority, and engagement level."
2. "How should we structure a talent community sign-up flow to capture useful segmentation data without discouraging sign-ups?"
3. "Build a data governance and consent framework for storing and re-engaging candidates in our talent CRM."

### Nurture campaigns

1. "Design a 5-touch nurture campaign for candidates in our [persona] talent pool who aren't ready for an active role yet."
2. "Write a re-engagement campaign for silver medalist candidates from the past 12 months who weren't hired."
3. "Design event-based triggers that should prompt automated CRM outreach (new relevant posting, company milestone, content publish)."

### Measurement and hygiene

1. "Design a measurement framework for talent CRM engagement and conversion into applicants and hires."
2. "How should we approach deduplicating and cleaning talent CRM records that have accumulated inconsistent data over time?"
3. "Audit our talent CRM ROI — is nurture campaign investment translating into faster fills for the roles we prioritized?"

### Content and community

1. "Plan a quarterly newsletter for our talent community covering company news, open roles, and industry content."
2. "Design a virtual talent community event to engage passive candidates in [function] without a hard sell."

## Tips

- Segment for relevance, not just volume — a large, poorly segmented database is harder to use than a smaller, well-tagged one.
- Get explicit consent and honor opt-outs; a talent CRM built on stale or unconsented data creates compliance and trust risk.
- Automate the routine touches but keep high-value candidates on a human-reviewed track — full automation can feel impersonal for senior talent.
- Review CRM data quality regularly; contact info and role interest go stale faster than most teams expect.
- Measure CRM success by pipeline generated and time-to-fill improvement, not just database size or open rates.
