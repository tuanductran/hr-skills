# Building a talent pipeline in CRM

## Context

A talent acquisition team wants to build a pre-qualified pipeline of Senior Frontend Engineers in their CRM to reduce future time-to-fill for upcoming quarterly expansion.

## Step 1: Define talent pool segments and tagging taxonomy

Sample prompt: "Draft a CRM talent pool segmentation guide for Senior Frontend Engineers based on framework expertise and career interest."

Expected response: A structured segmentation layout defining primary tags (React, Vue, Architecture), engagement status levels, and candidate origin source tracking.

## Step 2: Build multi-touch nurture campaign sequence

Sample prompt: "Create a 3-part email nurture sequence for passive candidates in a Talent CRM pipeline sharing tech blogs and engineering culture."

Expected response: A 3-touch email workflow (Touch 1: Technical blog highlight, Touch 2: Open-source project showcase, Touch 3: Informal invitation to tech webinar) designed to maintain brand awareness.

## Step 3: Monitor pipeline engagement and conversion

Sample prompt: "Draft a CRM engagement tracking dashboard layout monitoring email open rates, click-throughs, and candidate conversion to active requisitions."

Expected response: A metrics dashboard specification tracking active pipeline size, email engagement rates, and conversion velocity when new requisitions open.

## Workflow summary

The recruiter establishes segmented CRM talent pools, deploys automated content nurture cadences, and converts engaged passive candidates into active applicants when requisitions open.
