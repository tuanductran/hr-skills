# Talent Acquisition Strategy and Operations — Examples

## Example 1: TA strategy for scaling engineering hiring

**Scenario:** Series B startup growing from 50 to 150 engineers in 18 months. Currently 1 recruiter, time-to-fill is 90 days (too slow). Need to scale recruiting without losing quality.

**Sample prompt:**

> "We're scaling engineering from 50 to 150 in 18 months. Currently 1 recruiter, TTF is 90 days. We can't scale at this pace. Design a TA strategy to hit our hiring targets and reduce TTF to 50 days."

**Expected response:**

1. **TA operating model:** Need 3-4 engineering recruiters (1 per specialization: backend, frontend, infrastructure, senior levels). Hire in waves: month 1 (1 new recruiter to support, ramp existing), month 4 (2nd recruiter), month 8 (3rd recruiter).

2. **Sourcing strategy:** Current likely 80% recruiter outreach. Need to diversify: 40% direct search, 30% referrals (build referral program), 20% passive inbound, 10% agencies. Referrals will accelerate hiring and improve quality.

3. **ATS and process:** Implement Greenhouse or Lever. 4-stage interview (screen → technical → competency → offer). Run interviews in parallel to compress timeline.

4. **SLAs:** Screen-to-interview: 3 days. Interview-to-decision: 5 days. Offer-to-start: 2 weeks. Total: 50 days.

5. **Investment:** 3 recruiters × $100K/year salary = $300K. Referral bonus budget: $50K. Agencies: $100K. ATS + tools: $40K. Total annual: $490K. Expected hires: 100. Cost-per-hire: $4.9K (reasonable for engineering).

6. **Timeline:** Month 1-3: hire first recruiter, launch referral program. Month 4-6: second recruiter ramps, direct sourcing accelerates. Month 7-9: third recruiter hired, agencies engaged. Month 10+: hiring at target pace (100 engineering hires over 18 months = ~5-6/month).

## Example 2: Recruiting SLA framework for enterprise

**Scenario:** 10,000-person organization, 2,000 hires annually. Currently no SLAs. Hiring managers expect 2-week hiring; recruiting takes 8 weeks. Misalignment and frustration.

**Sample prompt:**

> "Set SLAs for recruiting to manage expectations. We hire 2,000/year, but hiring timeline is poorly understood. Hiring managers want 2 weeks; we need 60+ days. Design SLAs that are realistic and transparent."

**Expected response:**

1. **SLA framework by role category:**
   - Entry-level, abundant supply (2,000 applicants): 30-day target
   - Mid-level, moderate supply (200 applicants): 45-day target
   - Senior/specialized, tight supply (30 applicants): 70-day target

2. **Stage SLAs:**
   - Requisition opens → sourcing starts: 1 day
   - Candidate sourced → screened: 5 days
   - Screen pass → interview scheduled: 5 days
   - Interview complete → offer: 7 days
   - Offer extended → accepted: 10 days
   - Accepted → start date: 14-21 days
   - Total for standard role: ~45 days

3. **Communication:** Publish SLA document to all hiring managers. Send monthly recruiting dashboard showing: requisitions open/filled, time-to-fill by stage, offer acceptance rate. Hold monthly recruiting forum with hiring managers.

4. **Accountability:** TA accountable for hitting screen/interview/offer SLAs. Hiring manager accountable for interview availability and quick decisions. If hiring manager delays interview by 10 days, that's added to their timeline, not TA's fault.

## Example 3: Sourcing channel mix for competitive market

**Scenario:** Tech company needs to hire 30 software engineers in 6 months in a competitive Bay Area market. Similar companies are also hiring heavily.

**Sample prompt:**

> "Build a sourcing strategy for 30 engineers in 6 months in Bay Area. Market is competitive. Mix of channels? Budget? Expected results?"

**Expected response:**

1. **Sourcing mix:**
   - Inbound/LinkedIn passive: 8-10 hires (limited supply in tight market)
   - Referral program (aggressive): 8-10 hires (best channel)
   - Direct search (LinkedIn Recruiter, Boolean): 8-10 hires (time-intensive but high-quality)
   - Agencies: 3-5 hires (fill gaps, high cost)
   - Total: 30 hires

2. **Channel details:**
   - **Referral program:** $5K bonus per engineer hired. Target: 10 referrals (assuming 20% convert). Requires 50 referrals = ~1 referral per employee. Cost: $50K.
   - **Direct search:** 2 recruiters × 20 hours/week sourcing = 40 hours/week. Expected: 2 sourced candidates/week → 1 hire every 2-3 weeks = 10 hires in 6 months. Cost: $200K salary + $20K tools.
   - **Inbound:** 1 recruiter managing inbound, assume 30-40% of hires. Cost: $100K salary.
   - **Agencies:** 15-20% placement fee ($20-30K per engineer). Get 3-5 hires. Cost: $75K.

3. **Total investment:** $425K (salaries + bonuses + agencies).

4. **Timeline:** Months 1-3 referral program ramp-up, direct search starts slow/accelerates. Months 4-6 hiring accelerates as pipeline builds.

5. **Risk:** If market conditions tighten further, adjust: increase agency spend, go remote (expand geography), or slow hiring target.
