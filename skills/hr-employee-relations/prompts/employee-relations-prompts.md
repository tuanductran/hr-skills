# Employee relations prompts

Use these prompts when managing employee relations cases, advising managers on workplace conflicts, or designing HR investigation and disciplinary processes.

- "Triage this employee relations situation and tell me: what information do I need to gather before taking any action, who needs to be involved, and what are the biggest legal or reputational risks I need to manage from the start?"
- "Design a workplace investigation process for [company size] that covers investigator assignment, initial complaint assessment, interview protocol, documentation standards, and how findings are communicated to the complainant and respondent."
- "An employee has alleged harassment by their direct manager. What are the immediate HR obligations, what actions must happen within the first 24 hours, and who needs to be notified?"
- "Write a manager guide for handling employee relations concerns raised informally — covering what managers should and should not do in the first conversation, when to escalate to HR, and how to document the interaction."
- "Build a progressive discipline framework for [company] that is legally defensible, applied consistently across managers, and includes a documentation standard that would hold up in an employment tribunal."
- "Design a performance improvement plan for [employee role] based on [specific performance concerns] — including measurable expectations, support commitments, check-in cadence, and what happens at the end of the PIP period."
- "How do we conduct a workplace conflict investigation where both parties are senior leaders and the business is reluctant to involve an external investigator?"
- "Write a script for an HR investigative interview with a witness in an employee relations case — covering how to open the conversation, the questions to ask, how to handle reluctant or evasive witnesses, and how to close the interview."
- "Design an involuntary separation process for [company] that covers the decision documentation, manager preparation, the separation meeting script, final pay and benefits logistics, and the post-separation communication plan."
- "How do we identify when an employee relations pattern in a team or department reflects a manager conduct issue rather than multiple independent individual issues — and what do we do differently when it does?"
