# Using Network Analysis to Identify Collaboration Bottlenecks

## Context

A global financial services firm undergoing digital transformation is experiencing delays in product launches despite having formal cross-functional project teams. HR suspects that formal org charts do not reflect actual decision-making and collaboration flows. The HR Analytics Director conducts an Organizational Network Analysis (ONA) to map informal networks and resolve hidden bottlenecks.

## Step 1: Design ONA data collection

Sample prompt: "Design a passive and active ONA data collection survey measuring information flow, central connectors, and peripheral isolated teams."

Expected response: A combined active survey and passive metadata analysis methodology mapping peer-nominated advice networks, communication density, and information brokers across business units.

## Step 2: Analyze centrality and bottleneck risks

Sample prompt: "Build a risk mitigation framework for key-person dependency based on organizational network analysis centrality metrics."

Expected response: An analysis identifying three senior architects operating as extreme "information bottlenecks" (high betweenness centrality), creating burnout risks and slowing down approval cycles for ten dependent project pods.

## Step 3: Implement network interventions

Sample prompt: "Create an intervention plan to bridge structural holes between R&D and Commercial operations identified through ONA network mapping."

Expected response: A targeted intervention plan distributing decision authority, establishing peer-to-peer knowledge sharing forums, and assigning boundary-spanning liaisons to connect isolated commercial teams directly with R&D.

## Workflow summary

The HR Analytics Director maps informal communication networks via ONA, pinpoints critical information bottlenecks, and restructures communication pathways to accelerate project delivery across functional silos.
