# HR management prompts

Use these prompts when designing, leading, or improving the HR function — covering team structure, strategy, budget, governance, and positioning HR as a credible business partner.

- "Define the strategic priorities for the HR function at [company] for the next 12 months, aligned to [business strategy or growth stage], and identify which HR activities should be stopped, started, or invested in."
- "Design an HR team structure for a [company size]-person company with [number] HR professionals — including role definitions, reporting lines, and how HRBP, specialist, and coordinator work is divided."
- "Write OKRs for the HR team for [quarter / year] that connect HR deliverables to measurable business outcomes rather than HR activity metrics."
- "Build an HR department budget framework for [company size] covering people costs, program spend, technology, and vendor relationships — and identify where the highest-value investments are."
- "How do we build the business case to add [HRBP headcount / L&D investment / HRIS upgrade] when the CFO is asking for HR to operate with flat headcount?"
- "Design an HR function scorecard that measures effectiveness across talent acquisition, retention, manager effectiveness, compliance, and HR operations — and identify the three most important metrics for the executive team."
- "What are the warning signs that the HR function has lost credibility with business leaders, and what actions rebuild trust most effectively?"
- "Design a quarterly HR business review for [CHRO / VP HR] to present to the executive team — covering what HR delivered, what the people data says about the business, and what HR recommends for next quarter."
- "How do we shift the HR team's time allocation from predominantly administrative and reactive work toward strategic HR business partnership without adding headcount?"
- "Write a professional development plan for an [HR generalist / HRBP / HR coordinator] on the HR team that builds toward [next career level] over the next 12 months."
