# Piloting a Four-Day Workweek

## Context

A mid-size marketing agency wants to test a four-day, 32-hour workweek with one department before considering a company-wide rollout.

## Step 1: Design the pilot

Sample prompt: "Draft a compressed workweek pilot plan including success metrics."

Expected response: A pilot plan defining scope (one department, 3-month duration), success metrics (client satisfaction, project delivery timelines, employee engagement scores, overtime hours), and a clear rollback plan if metrics decline.

## Step 2: Assess readiness

Sample prompt: "Assess our organization's readiness for a fully flexible work model."

Expected response: A readiness assessment covering client service expectations, workload distribution, and manager capability to manage output rather than hours.

## Step 3: Prepare manager guidance

Sample prompt: "Create a guide for managers leading hybrid or fully distributed teams" adapted to "Create a guide for managers running a four-day workweek pilot."

Expected response: Guidance on setting clear weekly priorities, protecting focus time, and communicating client expectations during the pilot period.

## Workflow summary

The agency designs a bounded pilot with clear metrics, assesses organizational readiness honestly, and equips managers to run the pilot successfully before considering a broader rollout.
