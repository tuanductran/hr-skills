# HR Job Description

Comprehensive guidance for writing modern, inclusive, and candidate-focused job descriptions that clearly communicate the opportunity, set realistic expectations, and attract qualified talent.

## Supported tasks

- Writing complete job descriptions
- Rewriting existing job descriptions
- Creating inclusive job postings
- Defining role responsibilities
- Writing opportunity summaries
- Defining 30/60/90-day success expectations
- Separating must-have and nice-to-have qualifications
- Writing team and culture descriptions
- Writing compensation and benefits sections
- Creating employer branding content for job postings
- Improving job description readability
- Identifying and removing biased or unnecessary requirements

## What a modern job description means

A modern job description is more than a list of responsibilities.

It serves as both:

- a recruitment marketing document
- a realistic preview of the role

An effective job description helps qualified candidates understand:

- why the role exists
- how they will create value
- what success looks like
- who they will work with
- what qualifications are truly required
- why they should join the organization

Well-written job descriptions improve candidate quality, encourage self-selection, and create alignment between hiring managers, recruiters, and applicants.

## Anatomy of an effective job description

### Opportunity Summary

Begin by describing the opportunity rather than immediately listing requirements.

A strong introduction should explain:

- why the role exists
- the business impact
- growth opportunities
- why the position is exciting

Candidates are more likely to continue reading when they understand the purpose and impact of the role.

### Role Overview

Describe what the employee will accomplish rather than simply listing duties.

Where appropriate, define success during:

- First 30 days
- First 60 days
- First 90 days

This helps candidates understand expectations and provides a stronger foundation for onboarding.

### Responsibilities

Describe the core responsibilities of the role.

Responsibilities should be:

- clear
- outcome-focused
- prioritized
- realistic

Avoid creating long lists that mix essential responsibilities with occasional tasks.

### Qualifications

Separate qualifications into two categories.

#### Must-have

Include only the qualifications that are genuinely essential for success.

Keep this section concise whenever possible.

#### Nice-to-have

List skills, experiences, or knowledge that would be beneficial but are not required.

Separating essential and preferred qualifications helps reduce unnecessary barriers and encourages a broader range of qualified candidates to apply.

### Team and Working Environment

Help candidates understand:

- who they will work with
- reporting relationships
- cross-functional collaboration
- team culture
- working style

Candidates increasingly evaluate organizations based on team dynamics and culture, not only responsibilities.

### Compensation and Benefits

Whenever possible, include:

- salary range
- bonus eligibility
- benefits
- flexibility
- remote or hybrid arrangements
- professional development opportunities

Compensation transparency improves candidate trust and application quality while helping candidates self-select appropriately.

### Inclusive Closing

End with an inclusive statement that encourages applications from qualified candidates with diverse backgrounds.

The closing should emphasize:

- equal opportunity
- inclusion
- accessibility
- growth potential

Inclusive language helps expand the potential candidate pool.

## Characteristics of effective job descriptions

Strong job descriptions are:

- candidate-focused
- inclusive
- concise
- transparent
- realistic
- outcome-oriented

Rather than describing an ideal person, describe the work that needs to be accomplished.

## Common mistakes

### Starting with requirements

Opening with a long list of qualifications often discourages qualified candidates before they understand the opportunity.

### Credential inflation

Avoid requiring degrees, certifications, or years of experience unless they are genuinely necessary for successful job performance.

### Unrealistic requirement lists

Long lists of mandatory skills reduce application rates and may discourage otherwise qualified candidates.

### Generic responsibilities

Responsibilities should describe meaningful outcomes instead of vague statements such as:

- "Handle assigned tasks"
- "Support business operations"
- "Other duties as assigned"

### Missing compensation information

When organizational policy allows, include salary ranges and benefits to improve transparency and candidate experience.

### Biased language

Avoid:

- gender-coded language
- age-related language
- unnecessary educational requirements
- exclusionary terminology
- internal jargon

Use simple, inclusive language that is understandable to a broad audience.

## Writing principles

Every job description should:

- begin with the opportunity
- describe measurable success
- separate required and preferred qualifications
- explain team context
- communicate compensation and benefits when available
- use inclusive language throughout
- remain easy to read

Focus on attracting qualified candidates rather than filtering out as many applicants as possible.

## Key prompts

### Creating job descriptions

1. "Write a complete job description for a [Senior Product Manager]."
2. "Create an inclusive job description for a [Backend Engineer]."
3. "Write a modern job posting for a [HR Business Partner]."
4. "Generate a job description for a [Customer Success Manager] with 30/60/90-day success expectations."
5. "Write a compelling job description for a startup hiring its first [Marketing Manager]."

### Improving existing JDs

1. "Rewrite this job description using more inclusive language."
2. "Separate the must-have and nice-to-have qualifications in this JD."
3. "Improve this job description to attract more qualified candidates."
4. "Remove unnecessary requirements from this job description."
5. "Rewrite this job posting to focus on business impact instead of responsibilities."

### Hiring requirements

1. "Define realistic hiring requirements for a [Senior Data Engineer]."
2. "Which qualifications should be must-have versus nice-to-have?"
3. "Create measurable success expectations for the first 90 days."
4. "Write a team description that reflects our collaborative culture."
5. "Recommend a compensation and benefits section for this position."

## Tips

- Lead with the opportunity and business impact before listing qualifications.
- Describe what success looks like during the first 30, 60, and 90 days whenever practical.
- Separate essential qualifications from preferred qualifications to improve inclusivity.
- Use clear, inclusive language and avoid unnecessary credential or experience requirements.
- Include compensation, benefits, and working arrangements whenever organizational policy allows.
