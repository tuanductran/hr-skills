# Candidate assessment

Design and run structured assessments — skills tests, work samples, simulations, and assessment centers — that predict on-the-job performance more reliably than unstructured interviews alone.

## Supported tasks

- Selecting the right assessment method for a given role and level
- Designing skills tests and work-sample exercises tied to real job tasks
- Building assessment centers combining multiple exercises for senior roles
- Creating scoring rubrics that reduce rater subjectivity
- Calibrating assessors before a hiring round to improve consistency
- Balancing assessment rigor against candidate time and drop-off risk
- Evaluating third-party assessment tools and vendors for fit and validity
- Designing assessments that reduce adverse impact and bias
- Combining assessment scores with interview data into a hiring decision
- Communicating assessment results and feedback to candidates
- Piloting a new assessment before rolling it out broadly
- Auditing existing assessments for predictive validity over time

## Key prompts

### Designing assessments

1. "Design a work-sample exercise for [role] that reflects a real task they'd do in the first 90 days."
2. "What assessment method — skills test, case study, simulation, or work sample — best fits [role] at [level]?"
3. "Build an assessment center agenda for a senior [role] combining a presentation, case exercise, and structured interview."
4. "Design a scoring rubric for [exercise] with clear behavioral anchors at each rating level."

### Calibration and fairness

1. "Write an assessor calibration guide to reduce scoring inconsistency across interviewers for [assessment]."
2. "Review this assessment exercise for potential adverse impact or unnecessary barriers unrelated to job performance."
3. "How do we balance assessment thoroughness against candidate drop-off from a lengthy process?"

### Evaluating tools and results

1. "What criteria should we use to evaluate a third-party assessment vendor for [role type]?"
2. "How do we combine assessment scores with interview feedback into a single hiring recommendation?"
3. "Design a process for auditing whether our [role] assessment actually predicts on-the-job performance."

### Candidate-facing communication

1. "Draft candidate-facing instructions for [assessment exercise] that set clear expectations without giving away the evaluation criteria."
2. "Write feedback for a candidate who didn't pass the assessment stage, focused on constructive, specific points."

## Tips

- Anchor every assessment exercise to a real job task — assessments disconnected from actual work predict poorly and frustrate candidates.
- Use structured rubrics with behavioral anchors, not just numeric scales, to materially reduce rater bias and inconsistency.
- Calibrate assessors together before scoring live candidates, especially for subjective exercises like presentations or case studies.
- Keep total assessment time proportional to role seniority and candidate market competitiveness — over-testing loses strong candidates in competitive markets.
- Periodically check whether assessment scores actually correlate with post-hire performance; an assessment that isn't predictive is just candidate friction.
