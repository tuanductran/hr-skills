# Conducting HR Due Diligence and Talent Retention Risk Assessment

## Context

A software enterprise is acquiring a 150-person specialized artificial intelligence startup. The deal team needs HR to evaluate target talent, uncover hidden HR liabilities (unvested equity, non-competes, benefit gaps), and structure a retention strategy for 25 key engineers critical to the acquisition thesis.

## Step 1: Execute HR due diligence

Sample prompt: "Build an HR M&A due diligence checklist covering talent evaluation, compensation parity, benefit liabilities, and legal compliance risks."

Expected response: A detailed due diligence audit covering target company employment agreements, compensation benchmark gaps, unfunded pension or PTO liabilities, pending labor disputes, and IP assignment clauses.

## Step 2: Assess key talent retention risk

Sample prompt: "Draft a key talent retention risk assessment and retention bonus structure for a target acquisition company."

Expected response: A talent flight-risk model assessing 25 key engineers on flight risk versus strategic criticality, recommending a 2-year retention bonus pool tied to key product integration milestones.

## Step 3: Present deal risk briefing to steering committee

Sample prompt: "Draft an executive summary of HR deal risks and financial synergy implications for the M&A steering committee."

Expected response: A concise executive presentation detailing talent retention costs, benefits harmonization liabilities, and integration risks with clear mitigation strategies for deal sign-off.

## Workflow summary

The M&A HR Lead executes thorough due diligence, structures a targeted retention strategy for critical talent, and delivers actionable deal-risk guidance to the executive steering committee.
