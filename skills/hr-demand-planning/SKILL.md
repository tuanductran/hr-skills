# HR demand planning

Translate business strategy and operational plans into accurate headcount demand forecasts — from gathering demand signals and modeling capacity requirements to prioritizing critical roles and aligning recruiting resources.

## Supported tasks

- Translating business plans into headcount demand models
- Gathering and validating demand signals from business leaders
- Modeling headcount requirements by role, level, and location
- Prioritizing critical hires against available recruiting capacity
- Designing demand intake and approval processes
- Building rolling demand forecasts with scenario modeling
- Aligning demand plans with budget and span-of-control targets
- Identifying demand volatility patterns and mitigation strategies
- Reporting demand vs. supply gaps to TA and HRBP partners
- Designing workforce demand review cadences with business stakeholders
- Connecting demand plans to recruiting pipeline and sourcing strategy

## Key prompts

### Demand signal gathering

1. "Design a headcount demand intake process for [company] that gathers accurate signals from business leaders."
2. "What questions should we ask business leaders to translate product or revenue growth into role-level headcount demand?"
3. "How do we validate demand signals to avoid inflated headcount requests that drain recruiting capacity?"
4. "Build a demand intake template for [department or function] that captures role, level, location, start date, and business justification."
5. "How should HR business partners run quarterly demand planning conversations with their business unit leaders?"

### Demand modeling

1. "Model headcount demand for [department] growing from [current headcount] to [target] over [timeframe]."
2. "What factors should drive a productivity-based headcount model for [function, for example, sales, engineering, support]?"
3. "Design a scenario model that shows headcount demand under [conservative / base / aggressive] growth assumptions."
4. "How do we account for attrition, internal transfers, and backfills when building a net new headcount demand model?"
5. "What span-of-control ratios should we apply when modeling management headcount demand for [organization size]?"
6. "How do we model headcount demand for a product launch with uncertain ramp timing?"

### Demand prioritization

1. "How do we prioritize [number] open reqs when TA capacity can only support [number] per quarter?"
2. "Build a role criticality scoring framework to prioritize demand when supply is constrained."
3. "What criteria should a hiring committee use to decide which roles to defer versus accelerate in a slow-hiring quarter?"
4. "How do we communicate demand prioritization decisions back to business leaders in a way they find credible?"

### Demand and supply alignment

1. "Compare our current hiring demand plan against TA capacity and identify where gaps will occur in [quarter]."
2. "How do we translate a demand plan into sourcing channel requirements and recruiter workload projections?"
3. "Design a weekly demand vs. supply review process for [TA team / HRBP team]."
4. "What early warning metrics tell us demand is outpacing our TA supply chain before the pipeline dries up?"
5. "How should we adjust our demand plan when business strategy changes mid-year?"

### Reporting and governance

1. "Build a monthly headcount demand report for [CHRO / CFO / business leadership] showing demand, approved positions, and pipeline coverage."
2. "What governance process ensures demand changes go through proper review before recruiting resources are committed?"
3. "How do we track demand forecast accuracy and use it to improve planning in future quarters?"

## Tips

- Demand quality matters more than demand quantity — one accurate, well-scoped req is worth more to TA than five vague or rapidly changing ones.
- Business leaders often conflate "wish list" with "demand" — use business justification gates and budget confirmation steps to surface real versus aspirational need.
- Build rolling 90-day and 6-month demand horizons; annual plans become stale quickly in high-growth or volatile environments.
- Segment demand by criticality (revenue-generating, compliance-critical, backfill, growth) to make prioritization discussions with leadership easier and more defensible.
- Demand planning accuracy improves significantly when HR partners attend business planning meetings rather than receiving the output after decisions are made.
