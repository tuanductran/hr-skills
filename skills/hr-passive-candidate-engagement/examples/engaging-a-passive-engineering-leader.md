# Engaging a passive engineering leader

## Context

A recruiter is sourcing for a Director of Platform Engineering role at a Series B company. The talent market is competitive, and most qualified candidates are employed and not actively looking. The recruiter identifies a strong target who recently gave a conference talk on distributed systems and has been at their current company for four years — a tenure that often signals openness to the right conversation.

## Step 1: Write the first-touch message

Sample prompt: "Write a first-touch outreach message to a passive Director of Platform Engineering candidate who gave a talk on distributed systems at a recent conference — don't mention the role in the first two sentences."

Expected response:

```text
Subject: Your talk on distributed consensus at [Conference]

Hi [Name],

Your session on [specific topic from talk] was one of the more grounded takes I've heard on [technical challenge] — the tradeoff analysis in particular was useful.

I'm building out an engineering leadership search for a company working on [problem space] at meaningful scale. The problems they're solving are right in the territory you described. Would you be open to a short call to see if there's any relevance for you?

[Name]
```

## Step 2: Handle no response — touch 2

Sample prompt: "Write a follow-up message for a candidate who opened the email but didn't reply — different angle from touch 1."

Expected response: Shift from the conference reference to a specific product or company milestone — "Thought of you when I saw [company]'s recent [product announcement] — the engineering challenge behind it is similar to what [our client] is tackling." End with a low-friction ask: "Even if timing isn't right, I'd value a perspective from someone building at this scale."

## Step 3: Handle the "happy where I am" response

Sample prompt: "Write a reply to a passive candidate who said 'I'm happy where I am right now.'"

Expected response:

```text
That's exactly where most good candidates are — thank you for being direct. I'll make a note not to follow up on this specific role.

If you'd ever be open to a broader conversation about what the next chapter might look like for you — whether that's in 3 months or 18 — I'd welcome it. No agenda.

Either way, I'll leave it here for now.
```

## Step 4: Re-engage after 9 months

Sample prompt: "Draft a re-engagement message for a candidate we contacted 9 months ago who wasn't ready then."

Expected response: Reference the prior conversation briefly and specifically ("We spoke last [month] about [topic]"), acknowledge that things may have shifted since then, and offer a new angle or updated reason the timing might be better now (company growth, new product direction, team expansion). Keep it to three sentences and don't re-pitch the full role.

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-passive-candidate-engagement` to open conversations that feel relevant and personal, sustain them through multi-touch sequences with a different angle each time, and handle responses — including objections — in ways that build trust and keep doors open for future roles even when current timing is wrong.
