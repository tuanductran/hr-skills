# Passive candidate engagement prompts

Use these prompts when reaching out to candidates who aren't actively job-searching or building nurture relationships.

- "Write a first-touch outreach message to a passive [role] candidate who recently [shipped a major product / published a case study / spoke at a conference] — don't mention the role in the first two sentences."
- "Draft three variations of an outreach opening line for a [role] candidate, ranging from relationship-first to more direct value proposition."
- "Design a 4-touch nurture sequence for a passive [role] candidate who hasn't responded, spaced over 6 weeks — with a different angle in each touch."
- "Write a follow-up message for a candidate who opened the email but didn't reply — acknowledge the silence without being passive-aggressive."
- "Draft a re-engagement message for a passive candidate we contacted 9 months ago who wasn't ready then but may be more open now."
- "Write a reply to a [role] candidate who responded with 'I'm happy where I am right now' — keep the door open without being pushy."
- "Draft a response to a passive candidate asking for more detail about the role before they'll agree to a conversation."
- "Create outreach messaging for a [senior / executive] level candidate that reflects the gravitas and seniority of the conversation without sounding transactional."
- "Write a message that maintains a relationship with a candidate who was strong but not the right fit for the current role — sets up future engagement."
- "Analyze this outreach message and identify what's likely to get it ignored — then rewrite it with a stronger hook and a clearer reason to respond."
