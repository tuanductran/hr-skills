# HR function management

Practical guidance for leading and managing the HR function — covering HR team structure, goal setting, budget management, capability development, governance, and building a high-performing HR organization that earns business trust.

## Supported tasks

- Setting HR function strategy, goals, and OKRs
- Managing HR department budgets and resource allocation
- Designing HR team structures and role definitions
- Building HR team capability and professional development plans
- Establishing HR governance and decision-making frameworks
- Managing HR vendor and partner relationships
- Measuring HR function effectiveness and value delivery
- Managing HR team performance, feedback, and career development
- Building HR function credibility and business partnership
- Designing HR communication and reporting to leadership
- Running HR team planning and retrospectives
- Building HR function resilience and succession

## Key prompts

### HR function strategy and goals

1. "Define strategic priorities for the HR function at [company] aligned to [business strategy]."
2. "Write OKRs for the HR team for [quarter / year] that connect to [company's] growth goals."
3. "How should the HR function evolve as [company] scales from [current stage] to [next stage]?"
4. "What are the highest-usee activities the HR function should focus on to create business value?"
5. "Design an HR function scorecard that measures effectiveness across all HR domains."

### HR team management

1. "Design an HR team structure for [company size] with [number] HR professionals."
2. "How should we allocate HR team capacity across strategic, operational, and administrative work?"
3. "Write performance expectations for an [HR Business Partner / HR generalist / HR specialist] role."
4. "How do we build a culture of continuous learning and professional growth within the HR team?"
5. "What career paths exist within HR and how do we support HR professionals who want to advance?"
6. "How do we conduct meaningful performance reviews for HR team members whose impact is indirect?"

### HR budgeting and resource allocation

1. "Build an HR department budget framework for [company size] covering headcount, programs, technology, and vendor costs."
2. "How do we prioritize HR budget allocation when resources are constrained?"
3. "What benchmarks exist for HR cost-per-employee ratios by [industry / company size]?"
4. "How do we build the business case to increase [HR headcount / L&D budget / technology investment]?"
5. "Design a cost-benefit analysis for a proposed HR program costing [budget amount]."

### HR governance and compliance

1. "Design an HR governance framework for [company] covering policy authority, approval processes, and compliance oversight."
2. "What HR policies require board or executive approval versus HR director authority?"
3. "How do we ensure consistent HR policy application across multiple locations or business units?"
4. "Build an HR audit calendar covering compliance checks, policy reviews, and process audits for [year]."

### HR function effectiveness

1. "How do we measure the business value delivered by the HR function, not just HR activity metrics?"
2. "What are the warning signs that the HR function has lost credibility with business leaders?"
3. "How do we position HR as a strategic partner rather than a compliance and admin function?"
4. "Design a quarterly HR business review agenda for [CHRO / VP HR] to present to leadership."
5. "What capabilities does the HR function need to build to support [AI adoption / global expansion / rapid growth]?"

## Tips

- HR credibility is built through consistent delivery, business knowledge, and proactive insight — not through HR programs alone.
- Set clear expectations about what HR owns versus what managers own; blurred accountability leads to HR doing work that belongs to the business.
- HR team members often lack structured professional development plans — model what you preach by investing in your own team's growth.
- Budget conversations are easier when you connect HR investments to business outcomes (revenue impact, attrition cost, compliance risk) rather than defending HR programs on their own merits.
- Regular HR team retrospectives surface process inefficiencies and morale issues before they become retention problems within the HR function itself.
