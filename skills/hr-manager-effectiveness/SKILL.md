# Manager effectiveness

Assess, measure, and improve people manager effectiveness at scale — from designing manager capability frameworks and 360 feedback tools to targeted development programs, accountability systems, and organizational health diagnostics that surface the true quality of management across the business.

## Supported tasks

- Defining manager effectiveness standards and competencies
- Designing manager capability assessments and 360 feedback
- Measuring manager impact on team engagement and performance
- Identifying underperforming managers and designing intervention plans
- Building manager effectiveness development programs
- Designing manager onboarding and early transition programs
- Building management accountability frameworks and consequences
- Running manager effectiveness diagnostics for specific teams or units
- Designing upward feedback processes that surface management issues
- Connecting manager effectiveness to team health and attrition data
- Building manager cohort programs and peer learning communities
- Reporting manager effectiveness trends to HR and business leadership

## Key prompts

### Manager effectiveness standards

1. "Define what great people management looks like at [company] across [team lead / manager / director] levels."
2. "Design a manager effectiveness competency framework for [company] that we can use for assessment and development."
3. "What behaviors most strongly predict manager effectiveness in [high-growth / technical / distributed] teams?"
4. "Write a Manager Expectations Framework for [company] that covers team performance, team health, and individual development."
5. "How do we differentiate manager performance standards for [individual contributor managers] versus [managers of managers]?"

### Assessment and measurement

1. "Design a manager 360-degree feedback process for [company] that generates actionable data, not just scores."
2. "What quantitative metrics can we use to measure manager effectiveness from existing HR data?"
3. "How do we identify managers who may be effective on business metrics but are damaging team health?"
4. "Design a manager effectiveness dashboard for HRBPs that flags early warning signals for at-risk teams."
5. "Write an upward feedback survey that employees can complete honestly in a psychologically safe way."
6. "How do we link engagement survey data, attrition data, and performance data to assess manager effectiveness at scale?"

### Development and improvement

1. "Design a manager effectiveness improvement program for [company] targeting [identified capability gaps]."
2. "What development approaches work best for improving manager effectiveness in [technical / sales / operations] functions?"
3. "Design a 90-day new manager program that reduces common first-time manager mistakes."
4. "How do we build a peer learning community for managers that produces real behavioral change?"
5. "Write a development plan for a manager who is strong on task delivery but weak on team development."
6. "What coaching interventions are most effective for managers who receive consistently low upward feedback scores?"

### Accountability and performance management

1. "Design a management accountability framework that makes manager people leadership part of performance reviews."
2. "How do we handle a manager who consistently delivers results but causes high team attrition?"
3. "What is the right process for addressing a manager who receives persistently low upward feedback from their team?"
4. "Write talking points for an HRBP conversation with a business leader about a manager who is a performance risk."
5. "How do we create meaningful consequences for poor people management without undermining business continuity?"

### Organizational health and diagnostics

1. "Design a manager effectiveness diagnostic for [business unit] that identifies management quality variation."
2. "How do we identify which managers are most at risk of losing key team members in the next six months?"
3. "What organizational patterns indicate systemic management quality problems rather than individual manager issues?"
4. "Design a regular manager health check process that HRBPs can run quarterly across their business unit."

## Tips

- Manager effectiveness is the biggest lever in employee experience and retention — invest here before investing in programs that treat symptoms like low engagement.
- Most manager problems are predictable: the biggest risks are newly promoted managers, managers in hypergrowth environments, and managers who were promoted for technical excellence without people skills.
- Upward feedback is powerful but fragile — it requires psychological safety design, anonymous aggregation, and clear follow-through commitments from HR to be trusted.
- Accountability for manager effectiveness must exist at the business leader level, not just the HR level; if business leaders protect strong-results-but-bad-people managers, no HR program will change behavior.
- Distinguish between manager capability gaps (training can help) and manager motivation gaps (coaching or consequences may be needed) before designing interventions.
