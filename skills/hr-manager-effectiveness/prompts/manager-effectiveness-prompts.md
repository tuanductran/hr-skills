# Manager effectiveness prompts

Use these prompts when assessing, measuring, or improving people manager effectiveness across a team, business unit, or organization.

- "Design a manager 360-degree feedback process for [company] that generates actionable development data — including who provides input, how results are aggregated, and how managers receive and act on the feedback."
- "Build a manager effectiveness dashboard for HRBPs supporting [business unit] that flags early warning signals — combining engagement scores, attrition data, performance review outcomes, and upward feedback in a single view."
- "Identify the top three manager effectiveness gaps in [business unit] based on [engagement survey results / attrition data / upward feedback scores] and recommend targeted interventions for each."
- "Design a 90-day new manager program for first-time people managers at [company] — covering the most common early mistakes, the skills to prioritize, and the support cadence from HR."
- "Write a development plan for a manager who consistently delivers strong business results but receives low upward feedback scores on [team development / psychological safety / communication]."
- "How do we handle a manager who is producing strong results but causing sustained team attrition — and what is the right sequence of HR, business leader, and legal involvement?"
- "Design a manager effectiveness improvement plan for [manager name / role] based on [specific feedback themes] — including coaching commitments, behavioral milestones, and a 60-day check-in structure."
- "Write talking points for an HRBP conversation with a business leader about a manager who is a people risk — where the business leader is protective of the manager because of their results."
- "Design an upward feedback survey for [company] that employees can complete honestly — including the question design, anonymity approach, and minimum threshold for reporting results."
- "What organizational patterns in [business unit / function] indicate a systemic management quality problem rather than individual manager issues — and how do we distinguish between the two?"
