# Coaching a Manager Through a Performance Issue and Designing a Workforce Plan

## Context

You are the HR Business Partner supporting a 60-person product engineering unit. One of the senior managers has an underperforming individual contributor who has missed three consecutive quarterly targets. At the same time, the business is launching a new product line that requires a workforce plan for the next two quarters. The manager is asking for guidance on both the performance issue and the headcount plan. Leadership expects a credible HRBP strategy, not just policy enforcement.

## Step 1 — Choosing Where to Start

**Sample prompt:**

> "I have a team member missing targets and a new product launch requiring more headcount. Where should I start?"

**Expected skill response:**

Given your situation — a clear performance issue and a workforce planning need — start with two priority areas before drafting any formal documentation:

**Performance diagnosis (the underperforming employee):**

- Clarify whether the issue is a skill gap, will gap, or structural workload issue  
- Review documentation of missed targets and manager feedback consistency  
- Identify whether coaching or a performance improvement plan (PIP) is appropriate  

**Workforce planning (the product launch):**

- Map current headcount against product priorities  
- Identify critical roles needed in the next two quarters  
- Model scenarios for aggressive hiring versus flat budget constraints  

> **Avoid:** Do not let the workforce plan overshadow the immediate performance issue. A manager struggling with underperformance will not be able to lead new hires effectively without first addressing the existing gap.

## Step 2 — Modeling Strategic Options

**Sample prompt:**

> "Help me model a few options for handling the performance issue and workforce plan so I can compare trade-offs before advising the manager."

**Skill walkthrough:**

**Option A — Immediate PIP and defer workforce plan:**

```text
Approach: Launch a formal PIP for the underperformer now,
          defer workforce plan until performance issue stabilizes
Timeline: 6-8 weeks for PIP cycle
Trade-off: Clear accountability, but delays workforce plan
           and risks under-resourcing product launch
```

**Option B — Parallel approach (PIP + workforce plan simultaneously):**

```text
Approach: Launch PIP while modeling workforce plan in parallel,
          ensuring manager has coaching support
Timeline: 6-8 weeks for PIP, workforce plan ready in 2-3 weeks
Trade-off: Addresses both issues, but requires strong HRBP
           support to prevent manager overload
```

**Option C — Coaching-first, defer formal PIP:**

```text
Approach: Provide coaching framework for manager to deliver
          direct feedback, monitor improvement before formal PIP,
          prioritize workforce plan immediately
Timeline: 2-3 weeks coaching, workforce plan ready in parallel
Trade-off: Faster workforce plan delivery, but risks credibility
           if performance issue persists without formal structure
```

> **Recommendation framing:** Given leadership urgency on product launch, Option B — parallel approach — balances credibility and speed. It ensures the performance issue is addressed while the workforce plan is delivered on time.

## Step 3 — Diagnosing the Underlying Performance Gap

**Sample prompt:**

> "The employee has missed three targets. How do I know if this is a skill gap or a motivation issue?"

**Skill response — diagnostic framework:**

Gather signals across three dimensions:

**1. Skill gap concerns:**
- Has the employee received training or support?  
- Are missed targets due to lack of capability or knowledge?  

**2. Will gap concerns:**
- Does feedback show reluctance or disengagement?  
- Are there signs of motivation decline or resistance to accountability?  

**3. Structural/workload concerns:**
- Is workload realistic given role design?  
- Are targets aligned with available resources?  

> **What to bring to leadership:** Present whether the issue is skill, will, or structural. Recommend coaching or PIP accordingly, not a generic “performance problem.”

## Step 4 — Building the Performance and Workforce Plan

**Sample prompt:**

> "Help me build a plan that addresses the performance issue and delivers a workforce plan for the product launch."

**Skill response — phased plan:**

| Phase | Current Status | Action Needed |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Performance diagnosis | Employee missed 3 targets | Clarify skill vs will vs structural gap |
| Coaching/PIP | Not yet started | Launch coaching framework or PIP depending on diagnosis |
| Workforce plan | Product launch requires 10 new roles | Model scenarios aligned to budget |
| Evidence | Not yet built | Track improvement signals and workforce plan readiness |
| Stabilization | Premature | Sequence follow-up coaching and workforce plan execution |

**Stakeholder-specific communication sequence:**

1. Manager — coaching support and PIP guidance  
2. Leadership — workforce plan scenarios with trade-offs  
3. Employee — clear expectations and documented improvement plan  

## Step 5 — Presenting the Plan to Leadership

**Sample prompt:**

> "I need to bring this plan to leadership. How do I frame it?"

**Skill response — leadership communication framework:**

**Part 1 — Affirm the goal with a concrete plan:**

"I want us to deliver the workforce plan on time while addressing the performance issue credibly. I recommend launching a PIP in parallel with workforce plan modeling."

**Part 2 — Name the current risk directly:**

"We risk under-resourcing the product launch if we delay workforce planning, and we risk credibility if we ignore the performance issue. Addressing both in parallel is the fastest credible path."

**Part 3 — Present the plan and ask for what you need:**

"I need leadership support for budget flexibility in workforce planning and manager time for coaching and PIP documentation."

## Step 6 — Using AI to Draft HRBP Content

**Sample prompt:**

> "I need to draft the PIP and workforce plan scenarios. How do I use AI tools effectively?"

**Skill response — AI-assisted drafting workflow:**

**Step 1 — Prepare context:** Summarize performance data, workforce priorities, and budget constraints.  
**Step 2 — Use structured prompts:** Ask AI to draft PIP templates, coaching talking points, and workforce plan scenarios.  
**Step 3 — Follow up with stabilization planning:** Draft a 90-day monitoring framework for both performance and workforce plan execution.

## Step 7 — Designing the 90-Day Stabilization Plan

**Sample prompt:**

> "How do I ensure this doesn’t fade after initial fixes?"

**Skill response — stabilization design:**

**30-day checkpoint:** Is the employee showing improvement? Is workforce plan draft complete?  
**60-day checkpoint:** Has PIP produced measurable progress? Has workforce plan been approved?  
**90-day checkpoint:** Is the employee stabilized or exiting? Is workforce plan execution underway?

## Full HRBP Workflow Summary

```text
Identify trigger (performance issue, workforce need)
                    ↓
Diagnose skill vs will vs structural gap
                    ↓
Model multiple advisory options and compare trade-offs
                    ↓
Build a parallel plan for performance and workforce
                    ↓
Present recommendation to leadership with risk framing
                    ↓
Use AI to draft PIP and workforce scenarios
                    ↓
Design a 90-day stabilization plan
                    ↓
Report progress and readiness at checkpoints
```

### Common HRBP Mistakes

| Mistake | How to avoid it |
|---------|----------------|
| Treating performance issue as isolated | Diagnose skill, will, structural causes |
| Delaying workforce plan until performance issue resolves | Model parallel approach |
| Finalizing one option without trade-off analysis | Compare 2-3 options before recommending |
| Assuming coaching alone will resolve underperformance | Document with PIP if needed |
| Treating workforce planning as annual exercise | Align continuously with product priorities |
| Skipping stabilization plan | Build 90-day cadence with named owners |
