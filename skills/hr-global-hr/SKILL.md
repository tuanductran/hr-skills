# HR Global HR

## Supported tasks

- Designing global HR operating models and governance frameworks
- Managing multi-country employment compliance and labor law requirements
- Developing global mobility and expatriate management programs
- Creating global compensation and benefits frameworks with local flexibility
- Building cross-cultural competency programs for global teams
- Designing global HR policies with local adaptation guidelines
- Managing international payroll strategy and vendor coordination
- Developing global talent acquisition strategies across geographies
- Creating global employee experience standards with local nuance
- Advising on employer of record (EOR) and entity setup decisions for new markets

## Key prompts

### Global HR operations

1. "Design a global HR operating model for a company with entities in [countries]."
2. "Create a country HR compliance checklist for expanding to [country]."
3. "Develop a global HR policy framework distinguishing global standards from local requirements."
4. "Build a global HR governance model with center of excellence and local HRBP roles."
5. "Design a global HR technology strategy to support multi-country operations."

### Global mobility

1. "Create an expatriate policy covering relocation, compensation, benefits, and repatriation."
2. "Develop a short-term assignment framework for [duration] international deployments."
3. "Write a global mobility decision guide for choosing between assignment types."
4. "Design a pre-departure preparation program for international assignees."
5. "Create a repatriation and knowledge transfer plan for returning expatriates."

### Multi-country compliance

1. "Identify the key employment law requirements for hiring in [country]."
2. "Create a compliance risk assessment for HR operations in [region]."
3. "Develop a global termination process guide accounting for local notice and severance requirements."
4. "Write a cross-border hiring decision framework: entity, EOR, or contractor."
5. "Design an annual HR compliance audit for multi-country operations."

### Cross-cultural practices

1. "Develop a cross-cultural competency framework for [globally distributed teams]."
2. "Create a cultural onboarding guide for employees joining a [country] team."
3. "Write manager guidance for leading across [specific cultural dimensions]."
4. "Design a global team effectiveness program addressing communication and collaboration differences."
5. "Develop a global inclusion framework that respects local cultural norms."

## Tips

- Never assume global policies can be applied uniformly—employment law, cultural norms, and market conditions vary significantly by country.
- Engage local legal counsel before making employment decisions in new markets, particularly for terminations and benefits.
- Balance global consistency with local relevance—over-centralizing HR creates compliance risk and employee experience gaps.
- Track the total cost of international assignments carefully; mobility programs frequently exceed budget when indirect costs are not accounted for.
- Build cultural intelligence into leadership development, especially for managers leading cross-border teams.
- Review the EOR versus entity decision annually as headcount grows—EOR is flexible but expensive at scale.
