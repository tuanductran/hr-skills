# Managing a global mobility program

## Context

A technology company with entities in the US, UK, Singapore, and Australia wants to formalize its global mobility program. Previously mobility was handled ad hoc. HR needs to create a policy framework, assignment types, and tax and compliance guardrails before the next cohort of international assignments starts.

## Step 1: Define assignment types

Sample prompt: "Write a global mobility decision matrix for choosing between permanent transfer, long-term assignment, short-term assignment, and commuter arrangement."

Expected response:

| Assignment type | Duration | Tax treatment | Cost level | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Permanent transfer | Indefinite | Host country | Low | Career moves, restructures |
| Long-term assignment | 1-5 years | Split or host | High | Leadership development, market building |
| Short-term assignment | Up to 12 months | Home country | Medium | Project delivery, skills transfer |
| Commuter | Weekly cross-border | Complex | High | Proximity markets only |

## Step 2: Draft the assignment policy

Sample prompt: "Draft an expatriate policy covering pre-departure support, cost-of-living adjustments, housing allowance, home leave, and repatriation."

Expected response: A policy outline covering:

- Eligibility criteria (permanent employees, minimum tenure)
- Pre-departure: visa support, cultural training, school search assistance
- On-assignment benefits: housing allowance, COLA, hardship premium where applicable
- Home leave: frequency and class of travel
- Repatriation: role guarantee terms, knowledge transfer plan, reverse culture shock support

## Step 3: Build compliance guardrails

Sample prompt: "Build a cross-border hiring decision guide covering when to use a local entity, EOR, or independent contractor."

Expected response: A decision framework with three clear paths:

- Local entity: use when the company already has a registered entity and the role is permanent and ongoing
- EOR: use for new markets below 10-15 headcount or when entity setup timeline doesn't match the hiring deadline
- Contractor: only where there is genuine project-based, non-substitutable work and the jurisdiction's contractor rules are met — flag misclassification risk for any ongoing, directed-work arrangement

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-global-hr` to design the policy framework before assignments start, create clear decision paths for assignment type and hiring structure, and build compliance guardrails that prevent costly corrections after the assignment begins.
