# Scenario: Hiring a Spatial UX Designer for a VR Training Product

## Context

An enterprise training company building VR-based safety simulations needs a spatial UX designer with strong comfort-design experience, since early user testing has shown motion-sickness complaints.

## Step 1: Write the job description

Sample prompt: "Draft a job description for a spatial UX designer role focused on comfort and accessibility."

Expected response: A job description explicitly calling out experience designing for motion-sickness reduction and accessibility in VR training or simulation contexts.

## Step 2: Review portfolio and interview

Sample prompt: "Create a portfolio review guide for evaluating a spatial UX designer's past shipped experiences" and "Design interview questions probing a candidate's approach to reducing motion sickness in VR experiences."

Expected response: A portfolio review guide prioritizing recorded demos of shipped VR experiences, plus interview questions asking the candidate to walk through specific comfort-design decisions (locomotion method, field-of-view adjustments) they made on past projects.

## Step 3: Verify with references

Sample prompt: "Draft reference-check questions focused on a candidate's role in a shipped AR/VR application."

Expected response: Reference questions confirming the candidate's specific ownership of comfort-related design decisions and how those decisions were validated with real users.

## Workflow summary

The company writes a job description targeting the specific comfort-design gap it needs filled, reviews genuine shipped VR work, and verifies real ownership of comfort-related decisions through targeted references.
