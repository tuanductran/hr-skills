# Scenario: Building an Attrition Risk Early-Warning Model

## Context

A tech company with high turnover in its engineering org wants to identify at-risk employees before they resign, to enable proactive retention conversations.

## Step 1: Identify indicators

Sample prompt: "Identify indicators most correlated with voluntary attrition in engineering teams."

Expected response: A list of candidate indicators to test — tenure since last promotion, manager tenure/changes, engagement survey scores, compensation percentile versus market, and recent performance rating trend.

## Step 2: Build the scoring model

Sample prompt: "Build a flight-risk scoring model based on tenure, performance, and engagement data."

Expected response: A weighted scoring approach combining the validated indicators into a risk tier (low/medium/high), with guidance on how to validate weights against historical attrition data before deployment.

## Step 3: Analyze exit interview themes

Sample prompt: "Analyze exit interview themes and summarize the top three attrition drivers."

Expected response: A qualitative summary identifying themes such as limited growth opportunities, compensation concerns, and manager relationship issues, cross-referenced against the quantitative model.

## Workflow summary

The team combines quantitative risk scoring with qualitative exit interview themes to build a validated early-warning model, enabling HR business partners to have proactive retention conversations with high-risk employees.
