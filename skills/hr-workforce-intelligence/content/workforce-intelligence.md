# Workforce Intelligence

## Overview

Workforce intelligence (often called people analytics) applies data analysis to workforce questions — who is at risk of leaving, where skills gaps exist, how the organization is structured — to support better people decisions.

## Main topics

### Headcount and composition analysis

Headcount analysis tracks growth, tenure distribution, span of control, and organizational layers over time, often segmented by function, level, and location to spot imbalances.

### Attrition analysis

Attrition models identify patterns associated with voluntary turnover (tenure milestones, manager changes, compensation compression, engagement scores) to flag risk before it becomes a resignation, while exit interview analysis surfaces qualitative themes.

### Skills inventories

A skills inventory catalogs the skills present in the current workforce, compared against skills required for future business needs, revealing capability gaps that inform hiring, reskilling, or redeployment decisions.

### Pay equity analysis

Compensation distribution analysis checks for unexplained pay gaps across gender, ethnicity, or other protected characteristics after controlling for legitimate factors like role, level, and tenure.

### Data governance

Workforce intelligence requires consistent data definitions (what counts as headcount, active employee, attrition event) and strict privacy safeguards, especially for small-cohort analysis where individuals could be identified.

## Practical guidance

- Start from a business question, not from "what data do we have."
- Validate and reconcile source system data (HRIS, payroll, ATS) before combining datasets.
- Suppress or aggregate results for small cohorts to protect privacy.
- Pair quantitative dashboards with qualitative context (exit interviews, engagement comments) for a fuller picture.

## Conclusion

Workforce intelligence turns scattered HR data into decision-ready insight, but its value depends on data quality, clear governance, and framing analysis around real business questions rather than data availability.
