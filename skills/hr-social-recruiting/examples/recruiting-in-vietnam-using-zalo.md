# Recruiting in Vietnam Using Zalo

## Context

You are an HR Generalist at a manufacturing company expanding operations in Vietnam. You need to hire **five Production Supervisors** and **one HR Business Partner** across two provinces.

Your company has never used Zalo for recruiting. Until now, you've relied primarily on job boards and employee referrals. However, local HR professionals have told you that Zalo is where a significant portion of the candidate market is active, particularly for manufacturing and operational roles outside major cities.

You need a practical, culturally appropriate recruiting strategy that leverages Zalo effectively while respecting both local expectations and platform norms.

---

## Step 1 — Understanding Why Zalo Matters for Recruiting in Vietnam

### Sample prompt

> "Why should we use Zalo instead of relying only on job boards for hiring in Vietnam?"

### Expected skill response

- Zalo is Vietnam's dominant messaging platform and is deeply integrated into everyday communication across the country. For many workers—especially those outside Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi—it is used more frequently than LinkedIn and often more consistently than Facebook.
- Zalo Groups and Official Accounts (OAs) serve as community hubs and direct communication channels, allowing employers to engage candidates in a more personal and responsive way than traditional job boards.
- Candidates for production, manufacturing, technical, logistics, and operations roles are often much more active in local Zalo communities than on professional networking platforms.
- Because Zalo is messaging-first, candidates commonly expect to ask questions before submitting an application. Your hiring process should support conversational engagement rather than requiring lengthy application forms as the first step.

---

## Step 2 — Setting Up a Zalo Official Account (OA)

### Sample prompt

> "What is a Zalo Official Account, and should we create one before recruiting?"

### Expected skill response

- A Zalo Official Account (OA) is a verified business account, similar to a Facebook Page. It enables organizations to publish updates, respond to inquiries, and gradually build an audience of followers.
- A verified OA increases credibility. Given the prevalence of recruiting scams on messaging platforms, candidates are understandably cautious when approached by unfamiliar personal accounts.

### Setup checklist

- [ ] Register the OA using the company's legal or publicly recognized brand name.
- [ ] Upload a professional profile image (typically the company logo) and an appropriate cover image.
- [ ] Write a company description that clearly explains your business, locations, and the types of positions you typically hire.
- [ ] Publish several posts—such as company culture, workplace photos, or current vacancies—before beginning candidate outreach.
- [ ] Assign one or more HR team members to monitor and respond to messages promptly.

> **HR note:** An empty, newly created OA that immediately begins contacting candidates may appear suspicious. Build a minimal but credible presence before initiating outreach.

## Step 3 — Finding and Joining Relevant Zalo Groups

### Sample prompt

> "How can I find relevant Zalo groups for production and manufacturing jobs in a specific province?"

### Expected skill response

- Search directly within Zalo using province- or district-specific keywords such as "Việc làm [Province]" or "Tuyển dụng KCN [Industrial Park Name]."
- Ask local HR professionals, current employees, staffing agencies, or industrial park management offices for recommendations. Many of the most active recruiting groups are discovered through referrals rather than search.
- Prioritize groups focused on the industrial park or district where your facility is located. For production roles, commuting distance often has a major impact on candidate interest.
- Before participating, review each group's activity level, engagement, and posting rules.

### Checklist

- [ ] Identify three to five groups focused on the target industrial park or district.
- [ ] Identify one or two broader provincial job groups as secondary channels.
- [ ] Read the group's pinned rules, including posting format and frequency restrictions.
- [ ] Confirm whether administrator approval is required before posts become visible.

## Step 4 — Following Zalo Group Etiquette

### Sample prompt

> "What's the best way to post recruitment content in Zalo groups without appearing spammy?"

### Expected skill response

- Most groups expect job posts to follow a familiar structure that includes the position, location, salary range, and application method. Following local conventions helps establish credibility.
- Providing a genuine salary range is considered standard practice in many Vietnamese recruiting communities. Vague terms such as "competitive salary" often reduce trust rather than increase it.
- Avoid posting identical messages across multiple groups within a short period. Space posts throughout the day and tailor them slightly for each group.
- Respond promptly to comments and direct messages. Fast, transparent communication is a key trust signal for candidates.

### Sample post

```text
[HIRING] Production Supervisor

- Location: [Industrial Park], [Province]
- Salary: VND 12–15 million/month + allowances
- Requirements: Experience supervising a production team or manufacturing line; experience in [industry] preferred
- Schedule: Full-time, with company shuttle service available from [area]

Interested candidates can message our Zalo Official Account ([Company Name]) directly or leave a phone number below. Our HR team will respond within the same day.
```

## Step 5 — Conducting Direct Candidate Outreach

### Sample prompt

> "Write a direct outreach message for someone on Zalo whose profile suggests production supervisor experience."

### Expected skill response

> "Hello [Name], my name is [Recruiter's Name], and I work in the HR team at [Company Name] in [Industrial Park]. I noticed your experience as a Production Supervisor and wanted to reach out. We're currently hiring for a similar role with a salary range of VND 12–15 million plus benefits. If you're open to exploring new opportunities, I'd be happy to share more details."

### Why this approach works

- Opens with a polite introduction and clearly identifies both the recruiter and the company.
- Explains why the candidate is being contacted.
- Includes salary information upfront instead of making candidates ask.
- Ends with a low-pressure question that allows candidates to respond comfortably.

> ⚠️ **Warning:** Never begin the conversation by sending external application links or requesting identification documents. This closely resembles common recruiting scams and is likely to reduce candidate trust or result in reports.

## Step 6 — Avoiding Account Restrictions

### Sample prompt

> "How many people can we message per day on Zalo before the account gets restricted?"

### Expected skill response

- Zalo uses behavioral signals—including messaging patterns, account age, report rates, response rates, and other factors—to detect spam-like activity. The platform does not publish fixed messaging limits, and any thresholds may change over time.
- Rather than focusing on a specific daily quota, reduce risk by following good outreach practices:
  - Personalize each message instead of sending identical copy-and-paste messages.
  - Spread outreach naturally throughout the day or week rather than sending messages in large bursts.
  - Use an established Official Account or well-maintained recruiter account whenever possible.
  - Monitor response quality and report/block rates. If engagement drops significantly or negative feedback increases, reduce outreach volume and review your messaging approach.
- For a company that is new to Zalo and hiring only a handful of positions, prioritize group participation and gradual, personalized outreach instead of high-volume messaging.

---

## Step 7 — Respecting Candidate Privacy

### Sample prompt

> "What privacy considerations should we keep in mind when recruiting on Zalo?"

### Expected skill response

- Do not share candidate phone numbers, profiles, or screenshots outside the recruiting team without permission. Zalo profiles often contain more personal information than professional networking profiles.
- Be transparent about how candidate information will be used. If you intend to retain their information for future opportunities, communicate this clearly.
- Do not request sensitive personal information—such as national ID numbers or banking details—through chat. Collect sensitive information only through secure, official recruitment processes.
- Respect a lack of response as an indication that the candidate is not interested. Avoid repeated follow-up messages or unsolicited phone calls after silence.

---

## Step 8 — Managing Ongoing Candidate Communication

### Sample prompt

> "A candidate has responded positively on Zalo. How should we communicate the rest of the hiring process?"

### Expected skill response

- [ ] Clearly explain the next steps in writing within the Zalo conversation.
- [ ] Set realistic expectations for response times and interview scheduling.
- [ ] Use Zalo for quick communication and scheduling, but move formal documentation—such as offer letters and employment contracts—to official channels like email or in-person meetings, as appropriate.
- [ ] If a candidate is no longer being considered, send a polite closing message rather than ending communication without explanation. Professional follow-up contributes significantly to employer reputation.

## Common Mistakes When Recruiting on Zalo

| Mistake | Best Practice |
| --- | --- |
| Using a brand-new, empty Official Account for outreach | Build credibility with company information and content before contacting candidates |
| Omitting salary information | Include a realistic salary range whenever possible |
| Sending identical messages to many candidates in a short period | Personalize messages and spread outreach over time |
| Requesting sensitive personal information through chat | Collect sensitive information only through secure, official processes |
| Disappearing after initial conversations | Always follow up, even when declining a candidate |
| Treating Zalo groups like LinkedIn or Facebook without understanding local expectations | Follow each group's preferred posting format and community norms |
