# Building a Complete Social Recruiting Strategy for a Startup

## Context

You are the first dedicated People/Talent hire at a startup that just closed a Series B and needs to grow from **20 employees to 100 employees** over the next 12 months, primarily in engineering, sales, and customer success. There is no existing recruiting infrastructure — no employer brand presence, no sourcing process, no candidate pipeline. The founders have asked you to build a complete social recruiting strategy from the ground up, one that can scale as hiring volume increases each quarter.

## Step 1 — Assessing the Starting Point

**Sample prompt:**

> "We're going from 20 to 100 people in a year with no existing recruiting function. Help me figure out where to even start."

**Expected skill response — situational assessment:**

| Factor | Current state | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Employer brand | Nonexistent — no LinkedIn presence, no content history | Inbound will be near-zero initially; active sourcing must carry early hiring |
| Hiring volume | Rapid, multi-function (eng, sales, CS) | Need a repeatable, scalable process, not one-off tactics per role |
| Team bandwidth | Solo Talent hire initially | Prioritize channels and processes that compound, avoid spreading thin across every possible platform |
| Timeline pressure | 5x headcount in 12 months | Front-load brand-building now — it takes months to pay off, so delaying it costs hiring velocity later in the year |

**HR note:** With this scale of hiring ahead, the strategy has to be built as an engine, not a series of individual sourcing sprints. Every choice below should be evaluated on whether it scales as volume increases each quarter.

## Step 2 — Platform Selection by Function

**Sample prompt:**

> "Which platforms should we actually prioritize for engineering, sales, and customer success hiring, given we can't do everything well at once?"

**Expected skill response:**

| Function | Primary platform(s) | Secondary | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Engineering | LinkedIn, GitHub | Discord/Slack communities (as volume grows) | LinkedIn for broad reach and outreach infrastructure; GitHub for validating and sourcing technical depth |
| Sales | LinkedIn, X | — | Sales talent is highly LinkedIn-native; X useful for reaching sales leaders who engage in public commentary |
| Customer Success | LinkedIn | Niche CS community Slack/Discord groups | CS hiring benefits from LinkedIn's broad reach; niche communities add depth for senior/specialized CS hires |

**Recommendation for a solo/small team:** concentrate initial infrastructure-building (recruiter profiles, employer brand foundations, outreach process) on LinkedIn across all functions, since it's shared infrastructure, then layer in GitHub and community-specific channels for engineering as bandwidth allows.

## Step 3 — Employer Branding Foundation

**Sample prompt:**

> "Before we do heavy sourcing, help me build the employer branding foundation so cold outreach doesn't fall flat."

**Expected skill response — foundational checklist:**

- [ ] Company LinkedIn page: complete About section, current logo/cover image, careers link visible
- [ ] Founders' and hiring managers' personal LinkedIn profiles: updated headlines reflecting current company/hiring focus
- [ ] A small initial content backlog (4–6 posts) ready before active sourcing begins, so candidates who check the page see genuine activity, not emptiness
- [ ] A simple, honest careers page or job board presence linked consistently everywhere

**HR note:** This foundation doesn't need to be extensive on day one — it needs to be credible. A thin but authentic presence beats a rushed, generic one.

## Step 4 — Recruiter and Founder Personal Branding

**Sample prompt:**

> "Should I be building my own personal brand as the recruiter, or should this focus on the founders?"

**Expected skill response:**

- Both, but weighted differently by function: engineering candidates often respond more to engineering leaders' authentic voice; sales/CS candidates often respond well to a strong, consistent recruiter presence
- As the sole Talent hire, your profile becomes core sourcing infrastructure — invest early in headline, About section, and consistent activity, since you'll be the face of most outbound outreach across all functions
- Encourage (don't force) 1–2 founders or senior engineering/sales leaders to post occasionally — even light, infrequent founder content tends to perform well early in a company's public presence, since it's inherently more novel and credible than recruiter-only content

## Step 5 — Building the Recruiting Funnel

**Sample prompt:**

> "Design a repeatable recruiting funnel that can handle multiple simultaneous roles across functions."

**Expected skill response:**

```text
Role opens
     ↓
Sourcing brief defined (must-haves, nice-to-haves, disqualifiers)
     ↓
Channel selection based on function (per Step 2 table)
     ↓
Active sourcing (Boolean search + personalized outreach)
     +
Passive pipeline engagement (warm relationships already being built)
     +
Inbound (from employer brand content, once compounding)
     ↓
Standardized first-touch message + 3-touch follow-up sequence
     ↓
Screening call
     ↓
Structured interview loop (role-specific)
     ↓
Debrief + scorecard
     ↓
Offer
```

**Checklist for making this repeatable across a solo/small team:**

- [ ] Save reusable message templates per function, with clear personalization slots
- [ ] Maintain a running sourcing brief template so each new role doesn't start from zero
- [ ] Track every candidate's channel source in the ATS from day one — critical for later ROI analysis
- [ ] Standardize the follow-up cadence (per earlier examples: initial, +6–7 days, +13–14 days, then stop) across all roles

## Step 6 — Content Calendar Across Functions

**Sample prompt:**

> "Build a content calendar that supports hiring across engineering, sales, and CS simultaneously without overwhelming a one-person team."

**Expected skill response:**

| Week | Theme | Rotates across functions |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Company story/why now | General — applies to all functions |
| 2 | Engineering culture/technical post | Engineering |
| 3 | Open role spotlight | Rotate: eng → sales → CS across cycles |
| 4 | Employee spotlight | Rotate across functions |
| 5 | Process transparency (what interviewing here looks like) | General |
| 6 | Sales/CS-specific culture or customer-win story | Sales/CS |

**HR note:** With limited bandwidth, aim for 1–2 posts per week consistently rather than a heavier but unsustainable cadence — consistency compounds more than volume in the early months.

## Step 7 — Passive and Active Sourcing Balance

**Sample prompt:**

> "How much of our effort should go to active outreach versus building passive/inbound pipelines, given the aggressive timeline?"

**Expected skill response:**

| Approach | Time horizon to results | Role fit at this stage |
|---|---|---|
| Active sourcing | Fast (days–weeks) | Carries most of the first 2 quarters of hiring, since brand/inbound hasn't compounded yet |
| Employer brand/inbound | Slow (months), then compounding | Should be started immediately despite the delay, since 12 months of hiring means later quarters benefit significantly if started now |
| Passive relationship pipelines | Medium (weeks–months) | Worth investing in for senior/hard-to-fill roles expected later in the year, started now so they're warm by the time roles open |

> **HR note:** A common mistake at this growth stage is under-investing in employer brand because active sourcing produces faster short-term results. Given a full year of aggressive hiring ahead, delaying brand-building simply pushes the payoff further out — start it in parallel with active sourcing from week one.

## Step 8 — Community Engagement Strategy

**Sample prompt:**

> "As engineering hiring ramps up, when should we start engaging in GitHub/Discord/Slack communities, and how much effort should that take?"

**Expected skill response:**

- Start light community presence (following, genuine engagement) as soon as bandwidth allows — even before active hiring need, since community trust takes time to build
- Prioritize 2–3 well-chosen communities directly relevant to your specific tech stack over broad participation in many loosely-relevant ones
- As engineering hiring volume increases (later quarters), formalize this into a defined cadence — e.g., a specific team member spending a set amount of time weekly on community participation, rather than ad hoc effort
- Apply the same "participate before recruiting" principle throughout — this doesn't scale down as urgency increases; skipping it produces the same credibility problems at any hiring volume

## Step 9 — Recruiting KPIs and Scorecard

**Sample prompt:**

> "What KPIs should I report to the founders monthly to show this strategy is working?"

**Expected skill response — KPI framework:**

| KPI | What it measures | Reporting cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Source-of-hire breakdown | Which channels are actually producing hires, not just activity | Monthly |
| Time-to-fill by function | Whether the funnel is fast enough to hit growth targets | Monthly |
| Outreach response rate (by channel) | Message/targeting/profile quality | Monthly |
| Employer brand engagement trend | Whether content investment is compounding | Quarterly |
| Cost-per-hire by channel (rough estimate: recruiter time + any tool costs) | Channel efficiency, especially vs. agency/paid alternatives | Quarterly |
| Pipeline health (passive relationships warmed, by function) | Whether future quarters are being set up for success | Quarterly |

## Step 10 — ROI Measurement Over the Full Year

**Sample prompt:**

> "How do we prove this whole strategy was worth the investment by the time we hit 100 employees?"

**Expected skill response:**

- Compare cost-per-hire and time-to-fill across channels (social/organic sourcing vs. any paid job boards or agencies used) — the case for continued investment is strongest when social channels show comparable or better time-to-fill at meaningfully lower cost
- Track the shift in source-of-hire mix over the year — a healthy trajectory shows active sourcing's share gradually decreasing as inbound/brand-driven and referral hiring increases, reflecting brand compounding as intended
- Track quality-of-hire signals where available (manager satisfaction at 90 days, retention) segmented by source, not just volume — a channel that produces fast, cheap, but poor-fit hires isn't actually a win
- Present the full-year narrative to founders as a maturity curve: early months carried by active sourcing investment, later months increasingly supported by the brand and community infrastructure built from month one

## Full Recruiting Workflow (End-to-End)

```text
Assess starting state and function-specific hiring needs
                    ↓
Select primary/secondary platforms per function
                    ↓
Build employer brand foundation (page, profiles, initial content)
                    ↓
Launch repeatable recruiting funnel with reusable templates
                    ↓
Balance active sourcing (immediate) with brand/passive pipeline (compounding)
                    ↓
Run consistent, sustainable content calendar
                    ↓
Begin community engagement early, formalize as volume grows
                    ↓
Track monthly KPIs via scorecard; review brand/pipeline health quarterly
                    ↓
Measure full-year ROI: cost-per-hire, time-to-fill, source mix shift, quality of hire
```

### Common Mistakes When Scaling Social Recruiting at a Startup

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Delaying employer brand investment because active sourcing works faster short-term | Start both in parallel from week one — brand payoff takes months regardless of when you start |
| Trying to be active on every platform at once with limited bandwidth | Prioritize 1–2 primary platforms per function; expand only as bandwidth allows |
| Treating each new role as a one-off sourcing project | Build reusable templates and a repeatable funnel from the start |
| Only tracking hiring volume, not source-of-hire or quality-of-hire | Track source and quality metrics from day one, not retroactively |
| Skipping community "participate before recruiting" norms under time pressure | Maintain the principle regardless of urgency — shortcuts here damage long-term community access |
| Reporting only activity metrics to leadership (messages sent, posts published) | Report outcome metrics: response rates, time-to-fill, source-of-hire trends |

### Best Practices Recap

- Build brand and active sourcing in parallel from day one, not sequentially
- Standardize templates and processes early so the system scales with volume, not just headcount added to recruiting
- Track source-of-hire and channel-level KPIs monthly, and review brand/community health quarterly
- Treat community and passive-pipeline investment as compounding infrastructure, not urgent-req tactics
- Reassess platform priorities each quarter as headcount, function mix, and hiring volume shift
