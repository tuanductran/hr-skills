# HR Performance Review

Comprehensive guidance for writing constructive, evidence-based performance reviews that recognize achievements, identify development opportunities, and support employee growth through actionable feedback.

## Supported tasks

- Writing balanced performance reviews
- Drafting manager performance feedback
- Writing employee self-assessments
- Reviewing goal achievement
- Identifying employee strengths
- Writing constructive development feedback
- Creating development plans
- Setting SMART goals for future performance
- Writing promotion and readiness assessments
- Writing probation and annual review summaries
- Avoiding common performance review biases
- Improving the quality and consistency of written feedback

## What performance reviews mean

Performance reviews are structured evaluations that summarize an employee's contributions during a defined review period while providing clear guidance for future development.

Effective performance reviews should:

- recognize measurable achievements
- provide balanced feedback
- identify development opportunities
- align individual performance with business goals
- support future career growth

Well-written reviews focus on observable behaviors, business outcomes, and evidence rather than personal opinions or personality traits.

## Performance review structure

A comprehensive performance review typically includes:

### Overall Performance Summary

Provide a concise summary of overall performance.

A strong summary should:

- remain balanced
- recognize achievements
- acknowledge development opportunities
- reflect performance across the entire review period

Avoid exaggeration or overly generic praise.

### Strengths

Highlight two or three major strengths supported by specific examples.

Strong strengths usually demonstrate:

- measurable business impact
- consistent performance
- collaboration
- ownership
- problem solving
- leadership when applicable

Every strength should include evidence rather than general statements.

### Areas for Development

Development areas should focus on future growth rather than personal shortcomings.

Effective development feedback should:

- describe observable behaviors
- explain business impact
- suggest practical improvements
- maintain a supportive tone

Separate performance from personality whenever possible.

### Goals Review

Review each goal established at the beginning of the review period.

For every goal, summarize:

- original objective
- current status
- measurable outcomes
- supporting evidence
- lessons learned when appropriate

Goals should be evaluated against documented expectations rather than recent performance alone.

### Development Plan

Create a practical development plan that includes:

- specific improvement actions
- ownership
- expected outcomes
- realistic timelines
- manager support where appropriate

Development plans should be actionable rather than aspirational.

### Next Period Goals

Define three to five SMART goals that are:

- Specific
- Measurable
- Achievable
- Relevant
- Time-bound

Goals should support both business priorities and employee development.

## Characteristics of effective feedback

Strong performance reviews are:

- evidence-based
- balanced
- specific
- objective
- future-focused
- actionable

Good feedback explains both **what happened** and **why it matters** while helping employees understand how to improve.

## Common performance review biases

### Recency Bias

Avoid placing excessive emphasis on recent events.

Evaluate performance across the entire review period.

### Halo Effect

Do not allow one outstanding strength to influence every performance dimension.

### Horn Effect

Do not let one weakness overshadow overall performance.

### Similarity Bias

Evaluate employees against role expectations rather than personal preferences.

### Leniency and Severity Bias

Apply consistent standards across all employees.

## Writing principles

Performance reviews should:

- include concrete examples
- describe observable behaviors
- explain business impact
- separate facts from opinions
- recognize achievements
- identify realistic development opportunities

Avoid:

- vague praise
- unsupported criticism
- emotional language
- assumptions about motivation
- personality judgments

## Key prompts

### Manager reviews

1. "Write a balanced annual performance review for a [Senior Software Engineer]."
2. "Draft constructive manager feedback for an employee who exceeded expectations."
3. "Write a performance review for an employee who partially met their goals."
4. "Create a performance review that recognizes strong collaboration and leadership."
5. "Write a probation review with actionable development recommendations."

### Self-assessments

1. "Help me write a professional self-performance review."
2. "Rewrite my self-review to be more evidence-based."
3. "Summarize my accomplishments into a concise performance review."
4. "Write a self-assessment based on these achievements."
5. "Improve my self-review without exaggerating accomplishments."

### Development planning

1. "Create a development plan based on this performance review."
2. "Recommend SMART goals for the next review period."
3. "Turn this feedback into actionable improvement steps."
4. "Identify development opportunities from these review notes."
5. "Create manager coaching recommendations."

## Tips

- Support every major point with a specific example or measurable outcome.
- Evaluate performance across the full review period to reduce recency bias.
- Frame development areas as opportunities for growth with actionable next steps.
- Separate performance, behaviors, and business impact from personality.
- Align future goals with both organizational priorities and employee development.
