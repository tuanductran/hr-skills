# Scenario: Hiring a Firmware Engineer for a Medical Device Product

## Context

A medical device startup needs a firmware engineer experienced with safety-critical, regulated hardware development, and the HR team has limited embedded hiring experience.

## Step 1: Write the job description

Sample prompt: "Draft a job description for a hardware-software integration engineer role in medical devices."

Expected response: A job description specifying the target microcontroller platform, RTOS, and explicitly calling out experience with safety-critical development practices and regulatory documentation requirements.

## Step 2: Screen and interview

Sample prompt: "Create screening questions distinguishing real low-level embedded experience from general software experience" and "Draft interview questions covering memory management and real-time constraints in embedded systems."

Expected response: Screening questions probing specific real-time scheduling and memory-constraint scenarios, plus interview questions asking the candidate to reason through a priority-inversion scenario relevant to safety-critical systems.

## Step 3: Verify regulated-industry experience

Sample prompt: "Draft reference-check questions asking specifically about a candidate's role in a certified/regulated hardware product."

Expected response: Reference questions confirming the candidate's actual involvement in regulatory documentation and safety-certification processes on a past shipped product, not just general team membership.

## Workflow summary

The team writes a platform-specific, regulation-aware job description, screens for genuine low-level and safety-critical experience, and verifies regulated-industry involvement through targeted reference checks.
