# Running a retained VP of Sales search

## Context

An executive search consultant has been engaged by a B2B SaaS company to find a VP of Sales. The engagement is retained with milestone-based billing. The search is now in the candidate presentation phase after 4 weeks of research. One finalist has a competing offer with a 5-day deadline, and the client is undecided.

## Step 1: Structure the engagement

Sample prompt: "Draft a retained search engagement outline with milestone-based fee structure for a VP of Sales search."

Expected response:

| Milestone | Deliverable | % of total fee |
|---|---|---|
| Engagement start | Signed agreement, kickoff meeting, position specification | 33% |
| Candidate presentation | Slate of 3-5 shortlisted candidates with comparative assessment | 33% |
| Placement | Accepted offer from placed candidate | 34% |

Scope: exclusive engagement, 90-day replacement guarantee, 6-month search timeline, weekly status updates.

## Step 2: Run the kickoff meeting

Sample prompt: "What should be covered in a retained search kickoff meeting with the client for a VP of Sales search?"

Expected response:

- Position specification: success criteria for the role in the first 12 months; team structure and growth plan; key stakeholders the VP will work with
- Cultural context: leadership style the team responds to; relationship between Sales and Product/Marketing; decision-making pace
- Candidate profile: what "great" looks like vs. the minimum bar; deal-breakers; internal candidates to be aware of
- Process: interview stages, decision-makers, timeline; how the client will give feedback after each candidate presentation
- Communication: how and when the consultant will update the client; how to handle candidate confidentiality

## Step 3: Present the candidate slate

Sample prompt: "Draft a candidate slate presentation summarizing 4 shortlisted VP of Sales candidates with comparative strengths and risks."

Expected response: A structured slate with each candidate covering:

- Background: current role, company stage and size, revenue ownership
- Strengths: the 2-3 strongest signals for this specific role and client
- Risks: the 1-2 areas requiring validation or careful onboarding
- Cultural fit assessment: based on kickoff brief and interview observations
- Recommended ranking: with clear rationale for ordering

## Step 4: Close the competing-offer situation

Sample prompt: "Draft a negotiation approach for closing a competing-offer situation with a VP of Sales finalist who has a 5-day deadline."

Expected response:

- Clarify whether the competing offer is genuinely superior or being used as a closing mechanism — ask the candidate directly and listen carefully.
- Help the client accelerate their decision timeline: call a decision meeting within 24 hours of the competing offer being disclosed.
- Coach the client to make the offer compelling beyond base: equity, role scope, growth opportunity, and the quality of the leadership team the candidate will work with.
- If the client cannot move, be honest with the candidate about timeline rather than stringing them along — protecting the relationship for future roles matters.

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-retained-search` to structure the engagement clearly from the start, run a disciplined search process with consistent client communication, present candidates in a way that helps the client decide quickly, and handle closing situations — including competing offers — in a way that protects both the placement and the long-term client relationship.
