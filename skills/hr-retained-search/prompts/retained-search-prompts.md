# Retained search prompts

Use these prompts when structuring, running, or closing retained search engagements.

- "Draft a retained search engagement outline with milestone-based fee structure for a [role] search, including deliverables at each milestone."
- "Write client-facing language explaining what differentiates retained search from contingent recruiting — when to use each, and why retained makes sense for this engagement."
- "Draft a scope-of-work document for a retained search engagement covering deliverables, exclusivity terms, timeline, and replacement guarantee."
- "What should be covered in a retained search kickoff meeting with the client — including position specification, cultural context, process, and communication cadence?"
- "Build a research and longlist methodology for a retained search on [role], including target company list, title variants, and screening criteria."
- "Draft a candidate slate presentation summarizing 4 shortlisted candidates for [role] with comparative strengths and identified risks."
- "Design a weekly search status update template that keeps the client informed without overwhelming them with operational detail."
- "How should I communicate a search setback — thin talent market, candidate withdrawal, or scope shift — to the client without losing confidence in the engagement?"
- "Draft a negotiation approach for closing a competing-offer situation with a finalist candidate on behalf of a retained search client."
- "Write a post-placement check-in cadence for the guarantee period of a retained search — covering timing, format, and what to address at each touchpoint."
