# HR Service Delivery

## Overview

HR service delivery is the operating model through which HR provides services to employees and managers — spanning self-service tools, contact centers, and specialist teams — designed for consistency, speed, and scale.

## Main topics

### Tiered support model

- **Tier 0**: self-service (knowledge base, chatbots, portals) for simple, high-volume questions.
- **Tier 1**: generalist HR service center agents handling common transactional requests.
- **Tier 2**: specialists (benefits, leave, payroll) handling more complex cases.
- **Tier 3**: HR business partners or legal/compliance teams handling sensitive or strategic issues.

### Service catalog

A service catalog lists every HR request type an employee or manager can submit, along with expected turnaround time and required information, giving both employees and agents a shared reference.

### Case management

Case management systems log every inquiry, route it to the right tier/team, track SLA compliance, and retain history for audits and trend analysis.

### SLAs

SLAs define expected response and resolution times per case type. Sensitive case types (harassment, medical leave, terminations) warrant faster and more carefully governed SLAs than routine requests (address changes, pay stub requests).

## Practical guidance

- Design the service catalog around actual employee request volume, not assumptions.
- Build clear routing rules so cases reach the right team on the first attempt.
- Regularly mine case data for trends that point to root-cause policy or system issues.
- Keep specialist escalation paths short and clearly signposted for sensitive topics.

## Conclusion

A well-designed HR service delivery model reduces resolution time, improves employee experience, and frees HR business partners to focus on strategic work rather than transactional requests.
