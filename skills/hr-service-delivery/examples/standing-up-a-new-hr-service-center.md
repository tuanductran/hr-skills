# Scenario: Standing Up a New HR Service Center

## Context

A 3,000-employee company currently routes all HR questions directly to HR business partners, causing delays and inconsistent answers. Leadership wants to stand up a centralized HR service center.

## Step 1: Design the tiered model

Sample prompt: "Design a 3-tier HR service delivery model for a company of 3,000 employees."

Expected response: A model with Tier 0 self-service portal for FAQs, Tier 1 generalist agents for transactional requests (address changes, verification letters), Tier 2 specialists for benefits/leave, and Tier 3 HRBPs for sensitive or strategic issues.

## Step 2: Build the service catalog and SLAs

Sample prompt: "Build an HR service catalog covering the most common employee requests" followed by "Write an SLA for response and resolution time on each case type."

Expected response: A catalog of 15-20 request types with defined SLAs, e.g., employment verification letters resolved within 1 business day, leave-of-absence cases acknowledged within 4 hours.

## Step 3: Set up case routing and knowledge base

Sample prompt: "Design a case routing workflow for leave-of-absence requests" and "Write a knowledge-base article explaining how to request a leave of absence."

Expected response: A routing workflow directing leave cases straight to Tier 2 leave specialists, plus a self-service article reducing the volume of basic "how do I request leave" tickets.

## Workflow summary

HR designs a tiered model matched to case complexity, defines a service catalog with realistic SLAs, and builds self-service content to deflect simple questions — freeing HRBPs to focus on complex, high-value work.
