# Strategic workforce planning

Prompts for translating business strategy into workforce implications, building phased workforce roadmaps, and aligning workforce planning with executive and financial planning cycles.

## Translating strategy to workforce demand

- "Read this [3-year / 5-year] business strategy and identify the workforce demand implications — which roles and capabilities need to grow, which will shrink, and what new role types does the strategy create: [paste strategy]."
- "We are entering [new market / new product line / new geography]. Model the workforce demand implications over the next [timeframe], including roles needed, volume by year, and lead-time risks."
- "Identify the future critical roles [company or function] will need to execute [strategic priority] that do not exist or are not scaled in our current workforce structure."
- "What workforce risks are embedded in our current [3-year] business strategy that leadership has not explicitly surfaced?"
- "Interview guide: write the questions I should ask each functional leader to understand the workforce implications of their part of the business strategy."
- "Translate this business goal into workforce demand by function, level, and year: [paste goal and current headcount data]."

## Supply analysis and gap identification

- "Project our current workforce supply over [3 years] using these attrition rates by function: [paste rates]. Show what the workforce looks like in years 1, 2, and 3 without any hiring or development action."
- "Compare this workforce demand model to our projected supply and identify the top [number] gaps by volume, capability, and timing: [paste demand model and supply projection]."
- "Identify key person risk in our [function or organization] — critical capabilities held by a small number of individuals with no internal backup."
- "Build a gap prioritization matrix for [function] using two axes: strategic criticality and gap severity. Rank the top [number] gaps."
- "Which of our current workforce gaps will still be open in [year] if we take no action beyond our existing hiring and L&D plans?"

## Build/buy/borrow/automate decisions

- "For each of these [number] workforce gaps, recommend whether to build, buy, borrow, or automate — and explain the key factors that drive the recommendation: [paste gap list]."
- "Assess the feasibility of building [capability] internally over [timeframe] versus hiring externally given current talent market conditions."
- "Which roles in our [function] are most susceptible to automation over the next [timeframe], and how should we reflect that in workforce demand modeling?"
- "Design a workforce sourcing strategy for [critical role] that combines external hiring, internal development, and contingent talent to close the gap within [timeframe]."

## Building the workforce roadmap

- "Build a phased strategic workforce roadmap for [function or company] over [3 years] aligned to these business strategy milestones: [paste milestones]. Include hiring targets, L&D priorities, and internal mobility goals by year."
- "Summarize this workforce gap analysis into a one-page workforce roadmap that a business leader can read and act on: [paste gap analysis]."
- "Identify the top five workforce investments that will deliver the highest strategic return given our gap priorities and available budget."
- "Sequence these competing workforce investments when the budget only supports funding [number] of the [total] identified priorities: [paste investment list]."

## Facilitation and alignment with leadership

- "Design a strategic workforce planning session agenda for a two-hour executive workshop tied to the annual business planning cycle. Include: pre-work, discussion structure, decisions to reach, and outputs."
- "How do we get functional leaders to treat strategic workforce planning as a business exercise, not an HR reporting exercise?"
- "Write a pre-read briefing document for business leaders before the strategic workforce planning session, covering: why it matters, what we will decide, and what data they should bring."
- "Design a governance model for strategic workforce planning that clarifies HR's role versus the business's role in making workforce decisions."

## Communication and reporting

- "Summarize this strategic workforce plan into a [board presentation / executive committee briefing / one-page summary] that connects workforce investment to business outcomes: [paste plan]."
- "Write a quarterly workforce plan update for the executive team covering: where we are tracking against plan, what has changed in assumptions, and what decisions are needed."
- "What leading indicators should tell us our strategic workforce plan needs revisiting before the next annual planning cycle?"
- "Draft a one-page summary translating the strategic workforce plan into the specific actions each functional leader needs to take this quarter."
