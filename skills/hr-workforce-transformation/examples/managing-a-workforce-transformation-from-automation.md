# Scenario: Managing a Workforce Transformation from Automation

## Context

A logistics company is automating its warehouse inventory reconciliation process, affecting 150 employees across 6 warehouses.

## Step 1: Assess impact

Sample prompt: "Assess the workforce impact of automating warehouse inventory reconciliation."

Expected response: A task-level breakdown showing which parts of the role are automatable (manual counting, data entry) versus which remain human-dependent (exception handling, vendor coordination), estimating that roughly 60% of affected employees can shift into adjacent roles.

## Step 2: Design reskilling and redeployment

Sample prompt: "Design a reskilling program for employees in roles being automated" and "Create a redeployment process for employees whose roles are being eliminated."

Expected response: A reskilling curriculum for warehouse exception-handling and light technical roles, plus a redeployment process matching employees to open roles in quality control and logistics coordination before considering layoffs.

## Step 3: Plan communication

Sample prompt: "Draft a leadership communication announcing the workforce transformation plan" and "Create a manager talking points guide for discussing role changes with their teams."

Expected response: A sequenced communication plan — leadership briefed first, then warehouse managers trained on talking points, then a company-wide announcement pairing the automation news with the reskilling and redeployment plan.

## Workflow summary

The company assesses true task-level impact, builds concrete reskilling and redeployment pathways, and sequences communication so managers can support their teams before broad announcements land.
