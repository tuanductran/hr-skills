# Hiring an IoT Engineer for an Industrial Monitoring Product

## Context

An industrial equipment company is building a sensor-based predictive maintenance product and needs an IoT engineer who understands both harsh-environment device deployment and cloud data pipelines.

## Step 1: Clarify scope and write the job description

Sample prompt: "Draft a job description for an industrial IoT engineer role, distinct from consumer smart-home IoT."

Expected response: A job description emphasizing ruggedized device deployment, long device lifecycle expectations, and integration with a cloud-based predictive maintenance data pipeline.

## Step 2: Screen and interview

Sample prompt: "Create screening questions distinguishing full-stack IoT experience from single-layer specialization" and "Design an interview exercise covering device provisioning and fleet management at scale."

Expected response: Screening questions mapping the candidate's actual experience across device, connectivity, and cloud layers, plus an exercise walking through how they'd provision and monitor 10,000 deployed sensors.

## Step 3: Verify with references

Sample prompt: "Draft reference-check questions focused on a candidate's role in a shipped connected-device product."

Expected response: Reference questions confirming the candidate's specific ownership of fleet provisioning, OTA update safety, or cloud integration on a real shipped industrial IoT product.

## Workflow summary

The company clarifies exactly which stack layers the role requires, screens specifically for that combination of skills, and verifies genuine fleet-scale operational experience through targeted references.
