# IoT Engineering Hiring

## Overview

IoT (Internet of Things) engineering roles span multiple technical layers — device firmware, connectivity protocols, and cloud/edge integration — making hiring more multidimensional than most single-discipline engineering roles.

## Main topics

### The IoT stack

Device layer: firmware running on sensors/microcontrollers. Connectivity layer: protocols like MQTT, BLE, LoRaWAN, Zigbee, or cellular (NB-IoT) connecting devices to networks. Cloud/edge layer: ingestion, processing, and management of device data and fleets.

### Layer specialization vs. full-stack IoT roles

Some IoT roles specialize in one layer (e.g., a connectivity protocol specialist), while others require working across layers. Job descriptions should be explicit about which layers the role actually spans.

### Fleet-scale operational concerns

Production IoT systems require robust device provisioning, over-the-air (OTA) firmware update mechanisms with safe rollback, and fleet monitoring at scale — operational concerns distinct from single-device prototyping skills.

### Domain differences

Industrial IoT (harsh environments, long device lifecycles, safety/compliance requirements), consumer smart-home IoT (cost sensitivity, user experience focus), and wearables (power/size constraints) each demand different engineering trade-offs.

## Practical guidance

- Clarify which stack layers a given role actually requires before writing the job description.
- Weight fleet-scale operational experience heavily for production-facing roles.
- Be explicit about the target connectivity protocols and industry domain in postings.
- Verify claimed full-stack experience carefully; genuine expertise across all layers is uncommon.

## Conclusion

Effective IoT hiring starts by clarifying which layers of the stack a role truly spans, then screens specifically for genuine experience at those layers rather than assuming broad IoT experience is interchangeable.
