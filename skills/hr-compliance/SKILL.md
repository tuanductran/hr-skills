# HR compliance and workplace policies

Comprehensive HR compliance support — from OSHA, EEO, FMLA, and HIPAA compliance to drafting workplace policies, employee handbooks, harassment prevention programs, and audit preparations.

## Supported tasks

- Anti-discrimination and equal opportunity compliance
- Workplace health and safety regulations
- Statutory leave and absence management
- Conducting global HR compliance audits and OFCCP audit preparations
- Creating harassment prevention and whistleblower protection policies
- Developing employment, drug testing, and background check policies
- Writing employee handbooks and privacy policies
- Managing work permits, visas, and immigration compliance
- Disability inclusion and reasonable accommodations
- Writing workplace accommodation and anti-bullying policies
- Developing ethical business conduct policies
- Vietnam Labor Code and Social Insurance compliance

## Key prompts

### Equal opportunity and anti-discrimination

1. "How should I manage equal opportunity compliance to ensure our hiring, promotion, and compensation practices are non-discriminatory?"
2. "What are the key requirements for equal opportunity compliance in [country/jurisdiction] and how should I prepare for a regulatory audit?"
3. "How can I develop an affirmative action or positive discrimination strategy that meets local legal requirements?"
4. "What data should I collect and analyze to identify potential equal opportunity compliance risks in our hiring process?"
5. "How should I respond to a discrimination complaint or equal opportunity charge filed with a regulatory authority?"

### Workplace health and safety

1. "What are our key obligations under workplace health and safety regulations for [industry/workplace type]?"
2. "How can I develop a workplace health and safety compliance program that meets local regulatory requirements?"
3. "What should a workplace safety recordkeeping and reporting process include for our organization?"
4. "How should I conduct an internal workplace safety audit to identify and remediate compliance gaps?"

## Tips

- Consult employment counsel when drafting or updating major policies — legal requirements vary by state and country.
- Train managers annually on compliance topics — most violations arise from managerial actions, not written policies.
- Maintain thorough documentation for all compliance-sensitive decisions (accommodation, termination, discipline).
- Conduct periodic self-audits of I-9s, pay practices, and accommodation files before regulatory audits.
- Keep current on regulatory changes — employment law evolves rapidly and non-compliance can be costly.
- In Vietnam, the Labor Code and Social Insurance Law mandate employer contributions to social insurance, health insurance, and unemployment insurance — ensure these obligations are met accurately and on time.
