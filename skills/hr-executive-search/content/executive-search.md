# Executive Search

## Overview

Executive search is the specialized process of identifying, assessing, and recruiting senior leadership and C-suite candidates, typically involving confidential outreach, board-level evaluation, and careful compensation negotiation.

## Main topics

### Position specification

A position specification aligns the board or hiring committee on the role's mandate, required experience, leadership competencies, and success criteria before search work begins — the single most important step for avoiding a stalled or misaligned search.

### Search strategy

Executive search typically combines direct, confidential outreach to passive candidates identified through target company mapping with input from search firms who bring networks and market intelligence.

### Candidate assessment

Assessment frameworks for executive roles blend competency-based interviews, case-based strategic thinking exercises, and reference checks that go beyond standard reference calls to probe leadership track record in depth.

### Confidentiality

Because executive candidates are often currently employed in visible roles, searches require careful confidentiality protocols throughout outreach, interviews, and reference checking to protect both the candidate and the search.

### Succession planning

CEO and executive succession planning assesses internal readiness against future leadership needs on an ongoing basis, so that an external search (if needed) starts from a position of strength rather than urgency.

## Practical guidance

- Get explicit board/committee sign-off on the position specification before sourcing.
- Use a consistent, structured evaluation process across all finalists for fair comparison.
- Build confidentiality into every step, not just initial outreach.
- Start executive onboarding planning before the candidate accepts, not after start date.

## Conclusion

Executive search combines rigorous alignment on requirements, disciplined and confidential candidate assessment, and thoughtful onboarding planning — reflecting the high stakes and visibility of senior leadership hires.
