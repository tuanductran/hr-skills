# Scenario: Running a Confidential CFO Search

## Context

A private company's board needs to replace its retiring CFO within 4 months, requiring a confidential search given the sensitivity of the transition and the CFO's public-facing investor relationships.

## Step 1: Align on the position specification

Sample prompt: "Draft a position specification document to align the board on the CFO requirements."

Expected response: A specification covering required experience (public company reporting, capital markets exposure), leadership competencies, and success criteria for the first 18 months, reviewed and approved by the board before sourcing starts.

## Step 2: Build search strategy and outreach

Sample prompt: "Build a target company list for sourcing CFO candidates" and "Draft a confidential outreach message for a passive executive candidate."

Expected response: A target list of comparable-stage companies with strong finance leadership benches, plus discreet outreach messaging that doesn't reveal the search publicly.

## Step 3: Assess finalists

Sample prompt: "Build a candidate assessment framework for a CFO role" and "Create a candidate comparison scorecard for the board to review finalists."

Expected response: A structured assessment covering financial leadership track record, capital markets experience, and cultural fit, with a scorecard enabling apples-to-apples board comparison of finalists.

## Workflow summary

The board aligns on requirements first, sources confidentially against a targeted list, and evaluates finalists with a consistent, structured framework — protecting confidentiality throughout a sensitive leadership transition.
