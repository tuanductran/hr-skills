# Conducting a job analysis workshop

## Context

A mid-size financial services company is redesigning its Compliance Analyst role family after two years of significant regulatory changes. The existing job descriptions are out of date, the classification is disputed by managers, and compensation is misaligned with the market. HR is running a structured job analysis before any reclassification decisions are made.

## Step 1: Design the data-collection approach

Sample prompt: "Design a job analysis plan for the Compliance Analyst role family, including methods, data sources, and stakeholders."

Expected response:

- Methods: structured incumbent interviews (4-6 analysts at each level), supervisor input sessions, and a written task inventory questionnaire distributed to all role holders
- Data sources: incumbents, supervisors, job descriptions, regulatory requirements documentation
- Stakeholders: HR compensation team, Compliance function head, selected incumbents at each level, legal counsel for classification guidance
- Timeline: 3 weeks for data collection, 1 week for validation, 1 week for documentation

## Step 2: Run the job analysis interviews

Sample prompt: "Write a job analysis interview guide for Compliance Analysts covering essential tasks, decision-making authority, and required knowledge."

Expected response: A structured guide with sections for:

- Role overview: what does this role exist to do?
- Essential tasks: what do you do regularly? How often? What % of time?
- Decision-making: what decisions do you make independently vs. escalate?
- Knowledge and skills: what must you know to perform this role at a fully effective level?
- Interactions: who do you work with, and in what capacity?
- Challenges: what is hardest about this role that someone new wouldn't anticipate?

## Step 3: Document job content

Sample prompt: "Write the job content for a mid-level Compliance Analyst including essential duties, KSAOs, and a distinction between essential and marginal functions."

Expected response:

Essential duties:

- Monitor regulatory developments and assess impact on business operations
- Review and analyze transactions for policy adherence
- Draft and maintain compliance policies and procedures
- Advise business units on regulatory requirements and interpretation

KSAOs:

- Knowledge: regulatory frameworks relevant to [industry], risk assessment methodologies
- Skills: policy writing, data analysis, stakeholder communication
- Abilities: analytical reasoning, attention to detail, working under regulatory pressure
- Other: relevant certification (where required by jurisdiction)

## Step 4: Support the classification decision

Sample prompt: "Evaluate the complexity of the mid-level Compliance Analyst role using point-factor methodology."

Expected response: Scores across knowledge, problem-solving, accountability, and working conditions dimensions with a recommended grade level and a written rationale that can be shared with the business in a classification discussion.

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-job-analysis` to ground reclassification decisions in structured data collected from incumbents and supervisors — producing defensible documentation for compensation, legal, and manager conversations before any classification outcome is communicated.
