# Building HR business cases

Use these prompts when preparing to advise senior leaders, build investment cases for people programs, or position HR recommendations in business terms.

- "Build a business case for investing in [manager effectiveness training/onboarding redesign/compensation benchmarking] including the problem statement, cost of inaction, proposed investment, and expected ROI."
- "Help me translate our HR engagement survey findings into a business risk briefing that a CFO and COO would respond to, not dismiss as soft data."
- "Structure a recommendation for [a new HR technology/a headcount request/an L&D program] using a consulting-style problem-solution-benefit framework."
- "What quantitative and qualitative data should I gather before advising the CEO that [engineering attrition/manager effectiveness/DEI outcomes] is a top business risk?"
- "Draft a one-page executive brief summarizing our people strategy for [Q4 board presentation/leadership offsite/business planning cycle]."
- "How should I prepare for a 30-minute advisory conversation with a business unit leader who thinks HR can't help with their specific [operating model/team performance/talent pipeline] problem?"
- "Write the talking points I need to challenge a leader's assumption that the solution to low team performance is to 'fire and replace' the bottom performers, using data-driven framing."
- "Create a decision framework for recommending whether to [build internally/buy externally/partner with a vendor] for a new HR capability the business is requesting."
