# Diagnosing people problems

Use these prompts when conducting an HR diagnostic, advising a business leader on a people issue, or scoping an HR consulting engagement.

- "A business leader tells me their team's performance is declining. Walk me through a diagnostic framework to identify whether this is a capability problem, motivation problem, structure problem, or leadership problem."
- "Design a structured HR diagnostic for a [sales team/engineering department/customer support function] that is missing targets — covering data collection, stakeholder interviews, and hypothesis testing."
- "A leader says 'my people just aren't motivated.' What questions should I ask to distinguish between engagement issues, compensation misalignment, unclear direction, and burnout?"
- "What are the five most common root causes HR business partners misdiagnose as 'talent problems' when they are actually [management/process/strategy] problems?"
- "Create an HR issue intake template I can use when a business leader walks in with a vague people problem, so I can structure the problem before proposing solutions."
- "How do I test the hypothesis that high turnover in [team/function] is caused by [manager quality/compensation/lack of career paths] before recommending a fix?"
- "Design a 4-week HR diagnostic sprint for a team with elevated attrition, disengagement survey scores in the bottom quartile, and two recent leadership exits."
