# Rolling Out an Attendance Point System

## Context

A retail chain with 40 hourly stores wants to replace an inconsistent, manager-discretion-based attendance approach with a standardized points system.

## Step 1: Design the policy

Sample prompt: "Design a points-based attendance tracking system for hourly employees."

Expected response: A point scale (e.g., 1 point for lateness under 15 minutes, 2 points for absence with notice, 4 points for no-call/no-show), a rolling 12-month expiration window, and escalation tiers (verbal warning at 4 points, written warning at 6, termination review at 9).

## Step 2: Draft communication

Sample prompt: "Write an email announcing a change to the attendance policy."

Expected response: A clear announcement explaining the rationale, effective date, how existing occurrences carry over (or don't), and where employees can ask questions.

## Step 3: Build the manager guide

Sample prompt: "Create a manager's guide for handling attendance exceptions."

Expected response: A short guide covering how to log occurrences, when discretion is and isn't allowed, and how to document coaching conversations consistently across stores.

## Workflow summary

HR designs a standardized, legally reviewed points system, communicates it clearly with lead time, and equips managers with a consistent process so enforcement is fair across all locations.
