# Skills taxonomy and gap analysis

Prompts for building organizational skills taxonomies, proficiency scales, and conducting gap analyses at individual, team, and organizational levels.

## Building a skills taxonomy

- "Build a skills taxonomy for [function or industry] covering technical, behavioral, and leadership skills. Organize into categories, subcategories, and individual skills."
- "Create a skills classification framework for [role family] with three proficiency levels and observable behavioral anchors at each level."
- "Develop a future-critical skills list for [industry or function] over the next three to five years, based on current technology and market trends."
- "Design a skills ontology that maps related and adjacent skills for [domain], showing which skills enable faster reskilling into [target skill area]."
- "Review this draft skills taxonomy and identify: missing skills, overlapping categories, and skills that are too broad to be meaningfully assessed: [paste taxonomy]."
- "Create a skills glossary for [function] that defines each skill in plain language so employees can accurately self-assess their proficiency."

## Designing proficiency scales

- "Design a four-level proficiency scale for [skill] with observable behavioral descriptions at each level — Foundational, Developing, Proficient, and Expert."
- "Write proficiency anchors for [skill] that managers can use to validate employee self-assessments without requiring technical expertise."
- "Create a proficiency benchmark table for the top [number] skills in [role], showing the minimum proficiency level expected at each career level."
- "Review these proficiency definitions and make them more observable and less subjective: [paste definitions]."

## Conducting skills gap analyses

- "Conduct a skills gap analysis for [team or function] preparing for [business change or strategic initiative]. Identify the top five gaps by business criticality."
- "Design a skills self-assessment survey for employees in [role family]. Include instructions for calibrating ratings and a manager validation step."
- "Build a skills heat map structure showing current versus required proficiency levels across [organization unit or role group]."
- "Develop a skills demand forecast for [function] aligned to the [number]-year business strategy. What skills will we need more of, less of, and what is emerging?"
- "Create a skills gap priority matrix using two axes: business criticality (high to low) and gap severity (large to small). Plot our top [number] gaps."
- "Summarize the following skills assessment data into a gap analysis with key findings, priority gaps, and recommended next steps: [paste data]."

## Skills inventory and data collection

- "Design a skills inventory data collection process for [organization size] employees that balances data quality with employee time investment."
- "Write an employee communication explaining the purpose of the skills inventory, how data will be used, and what employees should expect: [company context]."
- "Create a manager guide for validating team skills self-assessments, including how to handle disagreements and calibrate ratings across the team."
- "Design a skills data quality framework that defines how often skills data should be refreshed, who owns validation, and how to handle missing data."
- "What data sources can we combine to build a skills inventory without requiring a separate self-assessment exercise? [describe available HR systems]."

## Skills benchmarking and market analysis

- "Benchmark the current skills profile of our [function] against market job postings for similar roles. Identify skills we are underweighting versus the market."
- "Identify the top emerging skills in [industry] based on current hiring trends and technology adoption patterns."
- "Compare our internal skills taxonomy for [role] against industry frameworks such as [SFIA / O*NET / LinkedIn Skills Graph] and identify gaps or misalignments."
- "Develop a skills scarcity analysis for [skill area] covering: current internal supply, external market availability, and build versus buy recommendation."
