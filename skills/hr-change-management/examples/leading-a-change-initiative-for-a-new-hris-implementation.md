# Leading a Change Initiative for a New HRIS Implementation

## Context

You are the HR Change Manager supporting a 500-person company that is rolling out a new HRIS (Human Resource Information System). Leadership has announced the system launch in three months, but there is no change readiness assessment, no stakeholder communication plan, and no training program in place. Employees are already expressing concern about workload and confusion. The CEO expects a credible change management strategy, not just a technical rollout.

## Step 1 — Choosing Where to Start

**Sample prompt:**

> "We’re launching a new HRIS in three months, but employees are worried. Where should I start?"

**Expected skill response:**

Given your situation — a major system change with visible employee concern — start with two priority areas before drafting any technical training:

**Change readiness assessment:**

- Run surveys and focus groups to measure employee confidence and concerns  
- Identify high-risk groups (payroll, managers, HR staff) most impacted by the change  
- Assess manager capability to support adoption  

**Stakeholder communication planning:**

- Map stakeholders (executives, managers, employees) and their information needs  
- Identify communication channels (town halls, email, manager briefings)  
- Sequence messaging to build trust before training begins  

> **Avoid:** Do not let IT drive communication alone. A technical rollout without change management will increase resistance and attrition risk.

## Step 2 — Modeling Strategic Options

**Sample prompt:**

> "Help me model a few options for handling this HRIS change so I can compare trade-offs before advising leadership."

**Skill walkthrough:**

**Option A — Training-first approach:**

```text
Approach: Launch training sessions immediately,
          defer readiness assessment and communication
Timeline: 4-6 weeks
Trade-off: Fast delivery, but risks low adoption
           if concerns are not addressed
```

**Option B — Readiness-first approach:**

```text
Approach: Run readiness assessment and communication plan first,
          launch training after concerns are addressed
Timeline: 6-8 weeks
Trade-off: Strong credibility, but risks delay
           in technical rollout timeline
```

**Option C — Parallel approach (readiness + communication + training):**

```text
Approach: Run readiness assessment and communication in parallel,
          design training modules simultaneously
Timeline: 8-10 weeks
Trade-off: Balances credibility and speed,
           but requires strong HRBP and manager support
```

> **Recommendation framing:** Given employee concern and leadership urgency, Option C — parallel approach — balances credibility and speed. It ensures readiness and communication are addressed while training is developed.

## Step 3 — Diagnosing the Underlying Resistance

**Sample prompt:**

> "Employees say they’re confused and worried. How do I know if this is resistance or workload concern?"

**Skill response — diagnostic framework:**

Gather signals across three dimensions:

**1. Attitudinal resistance:**
- Are employees expressing distrust in leadership or the system?  
- Do survey comments show skepticism about benefits?  

**2. Structural/workload concerns:**
- Are workloads already high, making adoption feel impossible?  
- Are managers worried about losing productivity during rollout?  

**3. Communication gaps:**
- Has leadership explained the “why” behind the change?  
- Are employees hearing consistent messages across channels?  

> **What to bring to leadership:** Present whether concerns are attitudinal, structural, or communication-based. Recommend targeted interventions accordingly.

## Step 4 — Building the Change Management Plan

**Sample prompt:**

> "Help me build a plan that addresses readiness, communication, and training."

**Skill response — phased plan:**

| Phase | Current Status | Action Needed |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Readiness | No assessment yet | Launch surveys and focus groups |
| Communication | No plan yet | Build stakeholder-specific communication sequence |
| Training | Not yet designed | Develop role-based training modules |
| Evidence | Not yet built | Track adoption signals and sentiment |
| Stabilization | Premature | Sequence reinforcement after launch |

**Stakeholder-specific communication sequence:**

1. Executives — align on consistent messaging and benefits  
2. Managers — coaching on how to support teams  
3. Employees — clear FAQs and role-specific training  

## Step 5 — Presenting the Plan to Leadership

**Sample prompt:**

> "I need to bring this plan to leadership. How do I frame it?"

**Skill response — leadership communication framework:**

**Part 1 — Affirm the goal with a concrete plan:**

"I want us to deliver the HRIS rollout credibly. I recommend readiness assessment, stakeholder communication, and training in parallel."

**Part 2 — Name the current risk directly:**

"We risk adoption failure if employees feel confused and unsupported. Addressing readiness and communication now is the fastest credible path."

**Part 3 — Present the plan and ask for what you need:**

"I need leadership support for survey participation, manager coaching time, and budget for training modules."

## Step 6 — Using AI to Draft Change Content

**Sample prompt:**

> "I need to draft the communication plan and FAQs. How do I use AI tools effectively?"

**Skill response — AI-assisted drafting workflow:**

**Step 1 — Prepare context:** Summarize readiness signals, stakeholder needs, and tone.  
**Step 2 — Use structured prompts:** Ask AI to draft leadership talking points, manager guides, and employee FAQs.  
**Step 3 — Follow up with stabilization planning:** Draft a 90-day monitoring framework for adoption and sentiment.

## Step 7 — Designing the 90-Day Stabilization Plan

**Sample prompt:**

> "How do I ensure adoption doesn’t fade after launch?"

**Skill response — stabilization design:**

**30-day checkpoint:** Are employees using the system daily? Are managers reinforcing adoption?  
**60-day checkpoint:** Has sentiment improved? Are resistance patterns declining?  
**90-day checkpoint:** Is adoption sustained? Are performance outcomes visible?

## Full Change Management Workflow Summary

```text
Identify trigger (system rollout, reorg, policy change)
                    ↓
Run readiness assessment and stakeholder mapping
                    ↓
Model multiple options and compare trade-offs
                    ↓
Build parallel plan for readiness, communication, training
                    ↓
Present recommendation to leadership with risk framing
                    ↓
Use AI to draft communication and FAQs
                    ↓
Design a 90-day stabilization plan
                    ↓
Report progress and adoption at checkpoints
```

### Common Change Management Mistakes

| Mistake | How to avoid it |
|---------|----------------|
| Treating change as technical rollout | Address readiness and communication |
| Delaying communication until launch | Build stakeholder plan early |
| Finalizing one option without trade-off analysis | Compare 2-3 approaches |
| Assuming training alone drives adoption | Pair with coaching and communication |
| Treating resistance as attitude only | Diagnose structural and workload concerns |
| Skipping stabilization plan | Build 90-day cadence with named owners |
