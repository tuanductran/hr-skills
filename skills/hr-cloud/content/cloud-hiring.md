# Cloud Hiring

## Overview

Hiring for cloud engineering, DevOps, and cloud architecture roles requires recruiters and hiring teams to translate fast-moving technical requirements into clear job descriptions, structured interviews, and consistent leveling — even when the recruiter isn't a cloud specialist.

## Main topics

### Role types

Common cloud-adjacent roles include Cloud Engineer (builds and maintains infrastructure), DevOps Engineer (bridges development and operations, focused on CI/CD and automation), Site Reliability Engineer (focused on reliability, incident response, and operational excellence), and Cloud Architect (designs overall cloud strategy and system architecture).

### Leveling

Seniority typically differentiates by scope (single system vs. multi-system/org-wide), ownership (executing designs vs. setting architecture direction), and incident response responsibility (participating in on-call vs. leading incident response).

### Certifications vs. experience

Cloud certifications (AWS/Azure/GCP) demonstrate baseline knowledge but don't substitute for hands-on production experience, especially incident response and cost/performance optimization under real constraints.

### Interview design

Effective cloud interviews combine a systems-design discussion (architecture trade-offs), a hands-on or take-home exercise (infrastructure-as-code, troubleshooting), and behavioral questions probing incident response and cross-team collaboration.

## Practical guidance

- Focus job descriptions on the 3-5 tools/skills that matter most for the actual role, not an exhaustive wishlist.
- Calibrate interviewers on leveling criteria before the loop starts to ensure consistent evaluation.
- Use production-incident reference questions to validate real operational experience beyond the interview.
- Keep take-home exercises time-boxed and clearly scoped.

## Conclusion

Strong cloud hiring processes translate deep technical requirements into structured, consistent evaluation criteria that non-specialist recruiters and technical panels can apply reliably across candidates.
