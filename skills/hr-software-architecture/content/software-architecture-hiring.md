# Software Architecture Hiring

## Overview

Hiring for software architect, staff, and principal engineer roles requires evaluating technical judgment and cross-team influence exercised without direct management authority — a different signal set than either junior engineering or people-management hiring.

## Main topics

### Leveling architecture-track roles

Staff engineers typically influence a team or small group of teams; principal engineers influence multiple teams or an entire technical domain; distinguished engineers often set technical direction organization-wide. Scope of influence, not years of experience alone, defines the level.

### Architectural judgment

Beyond technical depth, strong architecture candidates demonstrate the judgment to make appropriate trade-offs for a given context — knowing when a simple solution beats an elegant but overengineered one.

### Cross-team influence

Because architecture-track roles typically lack direct authority, evaluating how a candidate drives alignment, resolves technical disagreements, and influences without formal power is a critical, often underweighted signal.

### Portfolio and writing-sample review

Reviewing a candidate's past design documents, RFCs, or technical blog posts (when available) often provides higher-signal evidence of judgment and communication than a single interview conversation.

## Practical guidance

- Build interview loops around real, anonymized design scenarios rather than abstract trivia.
- Score technical depth and cross-team influence as separate rubric dimensions.
- Use portfolio/writing-sample review as a standard part of the process when material is available.
- Calibrate leveling definitions across teams to keep titles meaningful and equitable.

## Conclusion

Effective architecture-track hiring evaluates judgment and influence alongside technical depth, using real design artifacts and structured rubrics to produce fair, high-signal hiring decisions for senior individual-contributor roles.
