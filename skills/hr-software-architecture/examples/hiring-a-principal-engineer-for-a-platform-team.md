# Hiring a Principal Engineer for a Platform Team

## Context

A scale-up needs its first Principal Engineer to set technical direction across three platform teams, and the hiring manager wants to avoid over-indexing on pure coding ability.

## Step 1: Define scope and job description

Sample prompt: "Write a job description for a Principal Software Architect role" and "Build a leveling framework distinguishing staff, principal, and distinguished engineer scope."

Expected response: A job description emphasizing cross-team technical direction and influence over a domain, distinguished from a staff-level role scoped to a single team.

## Step 2: Design the interview loop

Sample prompt: "Design an interview loop assessing architectural judgment and cross-team influence" and "Draft an architecture-review case-study exercise based on a realistic scaling scenario."

Expected response: A loop including a realistic platform-scaling case study, a round specifically probing cross-team influence through past-behavior questions, and a portfolio review of the candidate's prior design documents.

## Step 3: Evaluate and reference-check

Sample prompt: "Create a rubric evaluating technical leadership impact without relying on people-management scope" and "Draft reference-check questions focused on a candidate's cross-team technical influence."

Expected response: A rubric separately scoring technical depth and cross-team influence, plus reference questions asking specifically how the candidate drove alignment on a contested technical decision.

## Workflow summary

The hiring team defines scope clearly, builds a loop centered on real architectural judgment and influence, and validates influence-related signal through structured reference checks rather than relying on coding ability alone.
