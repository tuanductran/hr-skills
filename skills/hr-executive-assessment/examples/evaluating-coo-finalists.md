# Evaluating executive leadership candidates

## Context

A board-backed private company is selecting a new Chief Operating Officer from two finalists. One is an internal candidate — the current VP of Operations — who is well-regarded internally but has never operated at this scope. The other is an external candidate with COO experience at a comparable company but is an unknown quantity culturally. The CHRO must structure a rigorous, consistent assessment and present a board-ready recommendation.

## Step 1: Design the assessment framework

Sample prompt: "Design an executive assessment framework for a COO hire covering strategic capability, operational leadership, stakeholder management, and derailment risk."

Expected response:

| Dimension | What to assess | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic capability | Can set direction, not just execute | Case scenario exercise |
| Operational track record | Evidence of at-scale operational leadership | Structured interview + references |
| Stakeholder management | Peer, board, and cross-functional influence | 360-style reference input |
| Cultural and organizational fit | Leadership style match with current team | Culture panel + CEO interview |
| Derailment risk | Rigidity, conflict avoidance, credit-taking | Behavioral interview probes |

## Step 2: Build the scenario exercise

Sample prompt: "Build a case-based scenario exercise for a COO finalist based on a real strategic challenge the business is facing."

Expected response: A scenario brief presenting the actual business challenge (for example, integrating a recently acquired division while maintaining operating performance), asking the candidate to walk through how they'd approach it in the first 90 days. The evaluation should note: Do they start with people and structure, or process and metrics? Do they show awareness of political complexity? Do they ask clarifying questions before jumping to solutions?

## Step 3: Structure the reference checks

Sample prompt: "Design a structured reference-check approach for a senior executive candidate — including what to ask and who to speak with."

Expected response:

- Speak with: direct reports (2), peers or cross-functional leaders (2), a prior manager if accessible
- For the external candidate, prioritize references from contexts similar to the current role scope
- Key questions: How did they operate when things went wrong? How did they handle disagreement with the CEO? What did their team say they needed to improve? What would they not do well in this role?
- For the internal candidate, use skip-level conversations with senior individual contributors as a proxy for 360 input

## Step 4: Compare finalists and present to the board

Sample prompt: "Compare the internal successor's readiness against the external candidate's profile for COO using consistent criteria."

Expected response: A structured comparison covering each assessment dimension with evidence, a summary of each candidate's primary strengths and risks, and a clear board recommendation — including the rationale for the recommendation and the mitigations proposed for the recommended candidate's gaps.

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-executive-assessment` to run a consistent, evidence-based evaluation process across finalists — using structured interviews, scenario exercises, and calibrated references — and synthesize findings into a board-ready recommendation that the committee can act on with confidence.
