# Executive assessment prompts

Use these prompts when designing, running, or synthesizing executive-level candidate assessments.

- "Design an executive assessment framework for a [C-suite role] hire covering strategic capability, leadership judgment, organizational fit, and derailment risk."
- "Build a case-based scenario exercise for a [executive role] finalist based on a real strategic challenge the business is facing — include the brief, what to look for, and how to score it."
- "What structured interview questions help surface derailment risks — including rigidity, poor stakeholder management, or conflict-avoidance — in a senior executive candidate?"
- "Design a structured reference-check approach for a senior executive whose current employer doesn't know they're interviewing — including what to ask and who to speak with."
- "Compare an internal successor's readiness against an external executive candidate's profile for [role] using consistent criteria across strategic thinking, leadership record, and cultural fit."
- "Summarize this executive assessment into a board-ready recommendation covering strengths, risks, and proposed onboarding mitigations."
- "How should we weigh a strong strategic thinker against a stronger operational executor when two finalists trade off on these dimensions for a [role]?"
- "Design a structured debrief process for the search committee to reach an executive hire decision without the most senior voice dominating the discussion."
- "Design a 100-day executive onboarding support plan based on the risks identified during assessment for a newly hired [C-suite role]."
- "Write a board-level briefing comparing two CEO finalists — their strategic approaches, leadership styles, stakeholder management track records, and the committee's key unresolved questions."
