# Executive assessment

Assess senior leadership and C-suite candidates for capability, strategic fit, and derailment risk — combining structured interviews, psychometric input, and stakeholder feedback into a defensible executive hiring decision.

## Supported tasks

- Designing executive assessment frameworks by role and organizational context
- Structuring board-level or committee-based candidate evaluation processes
- Assessing strategic thinking, judgment, and leadership capability at the executive level
- Identifying derailment risks (ego, rigidity, poor stakeholder management) in senior candidates
- Integrating psychometric and 360-style input into executive assessment
- Designing case-based or scenario exercises for executive finalists
- Assessing cultural and organizational fit at the leadership level
- Structuring reference checks appropriate for confidential executive searches
- Comparing internal successor readiness against external executive candidates
- Preparing assessment summaries for board or investor presentation
- Assessing executive onboarding risk and designing early support plans
- Calibrating multiple assessors' views into a single executive recommendation

## Key prompts

### Assessment design

1. "Design an executive assessment framework for a [C-suite role] hire, covering strategic capability, leadership style, and organizational fit."
2. "Build a case-based scenario exercise for a finalist [executive role] candidate reflecting a real strategic challenge the business faces."
3. "What should a board-level evaluation process look like for a [CEO/CFO] search, including who's involved at each stage?"

### Assessing risk and capability

1. "What questions or exercises help surface derailment risks like rigidity or poor stakeholder management in an executive candidate?"
2. "How do we assess strategic thinking in an interview rather than relying on the candidate's self-description of it?"
3. "Design an approach for integrating 360-style peer and direct-report input into an executive assessment process."

### Comparing candidates

1. "Compare an internal successor's readiness against an external executive candidate's profile for [role] using consistent criteria."
2. "Summarize this executive assessment into a board-ready recommendation with clear strengths, risks, and mitigations."

### Onboarding risk

1. "Design an early-tenure support plan for an incoming [executive role] hire based on the risks identified during assessment."
2. "What onboarding risks should we plan for given this candidate's assessed gaps in [area]?"

## Tips

- Weight judgment and self-awareness heavily — technical capability is usually well-documented by an executive's track record; judgment under ambiguity is harder to see and matters more.
- Use structured, comparable criteria across candidates even at the executive level; unstructured "gut feel" evaluation is where the biggest hiring mistakes happen.
- Treat derailment risk assessment as seriously as capability assessment — the reasons senior leaders fail are usually about behavior, not skill.
- Involve multiple assessors and reconcile differing views explicitly rather than letting the most senior voice in the room override quieter dissent.
- Plan the first 100 days before the hire starts; executive assessment findings should directly shape the onboarding and support plan.
