# Building a people leadership strategy after low values alignment scores

## Context

A CHRO at a 1,200-person professional services company discovers through the annual engagement survey that only 38% of employees agree their leaders act consistently with the company's stated values. The leadership team wants to close this gap but has defaulted to "more training." The CHRO needs a people leadership strategy that goes beyond a training event — covering leadership principles, development investments, accountability mechanisms, and how to measure progress over 18 months.

## Step 1: Define what people leadership looks like at this company

Sample prompt: "Design a people leadership competency framework for a 1,200-person professional services company covering team lead to VP levels — including observable behaviors for each competency at each level."

Expected response: A three-level framework across five competencies:

| Competency | Team Lead behaviors | Manager behaviors | Director/VP behaviors |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trust and transparency | Shares team context openly; doesn't filter information | Communicates business decisions with rationale, not just outcomes | Models vulnerability; shares own mistakes with their team publicly |
| Feedback and development | Gives informal weekly feedback; holds regular 1:1s | Conducts structured development conversations quarterly; advocates for direct reports | Builds a team culture where feedback flows in all directions |
| Psychological safety | Asks for input before deciding; doesn't penalize mistakes | Creates space for dissent in team meetings; follows up when concerns are raised | Publicly recognizes when they were wrong; removes people who suppress disagreement |
| Accountability | Sets clear expectations; follows up consistently | Addresses underperformance directly rather than working around it | Holds managers accountable for team health, not just delivery |
| Inclusive leadership | Actively includes quieter voices; distributes opportunities fairly | Audits their own patterns for bias in development, recognition, and stretch assignments | Champions equity in promotion and compensation decisions |

## Step 2: Design the accountability mechanism

Sample prompt: "How do we hold leaders accountable for creating inclusive and high-trust team environments in performance reviews — not just for business delivery results?"

Expected response:

Build a two-part leadership scorecard used in all manager performance reviews:

**Delivery scorecard** — existing business results metrics (revenue, project delivery, quality)

**People leadership scorecard** — weighted equally in the overall rating:

- Team engagement score trend (compared to prior year and company average)
- Voluntary attrition rate in their team
- Upward feedback score from direct reports (minimum threshold for aggregation)
- Development actions: did each direct report receive a career conversation and a development commitment in the last six months?

For senior leaders (director and above), add a 360 incorporating peer and cross-functional input. Make people leadership scores visible to the executive team in the annual talent review — not just HR.

## Step 3: Build the development investment

Sample prompt: "Design a peer learning program for managers to learn from each other's people leadership experiences — including session structure, facilitation approach, and how to make the learning transfer to day-to-day practice."

Expected response: A quarterly peer learning cohort format:

- **Group size**: 8–10 managers at a similar level
- **Session cadence**: 90-minute sessions every six weeks
- **Session structure**: (1) One manager presents a real leadership challenge they are facing — 15 minutes; (2) Peer coaching round — other managers ask questions and share what they would do — 30 minutes; (3) The presenting manager shares what they will try — 10 minutes; (4) Shared learning debrief — what principles emerged from this case — 20 minutes; (5) Commitment round — each participant names one leadership behavior they will practice before the next session — 15 minutes
- **Transfer mechanism**: Participants share a written "practice update" at the start of the next session — not a performance check-in, just a learning reflection

## Step 4: Measure progress at 12 and 18 months

Sample prompt: "What metrics and signals indicate a manager is not meeting people leadership expectations — and how do we distinguish a manager who is struggling from one who is the wrong fit for the role?"

Expected response:

Indicators a manager is struggling (and may respond to development):

- Engagement scores dropped in the last cycle but were previously average or above
- Upward feedback themes are consistent but the manager is not aware of the impact
- The manager is new to the role or recently took on a significantly larger team

Indicators a manager may not be the right fit for this role:

- Multiple cycles of low upward feedback with no improvement after coaching
- Attrition concentrated in high performers leaving the team specifically
- The manager has received direct feedback about specific behaviors and reverts within weeks
- Business leaders protect the manager despite consistent people data — investigate whether the accountability system itself is the problem

## Workflow summary

Use `hr-people-leadership` to build a leadership strategy that combines a competency framework, real accountability mechanisms, peer learning, and measurement — so that "act consistently with our values" moves from an aspiration to a verifiable, coached, and rewarded leadership behavior.
