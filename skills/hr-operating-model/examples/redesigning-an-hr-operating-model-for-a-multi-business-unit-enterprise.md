# Redesigning an HR Operating Model for a Multi-Business-Unit Enterprise

## Context

A mid-sized enterprise expanding from two to four business units is facing duplicated HR efforts, inconsistent employee experiences, and unclear boundaries between business-unit HR Business Partners and central HR specialists. The CHRO needs to redesign the HR operating model to balance business-unit responsiveness with enterprise efficiency.

## Step 1: Evaluate operating model archetypes

Sample prompt: "Draft an executive briefing for the Chief Human Resources Officer comparing centralized, decentralized, and federated HR operating models."

Expected response: A structured comparison evaluating governance, speed, cost efficiency, and employee experience across centralized, decentralized, and federated models, recommending a hybrid-federated model with shared operations and embedded business-unit HRBPs.

## Step 2: Define handoffs and role boundaries

Sample prompt: "Draft an operating model interface agreement defining clear handoffs between HR Business Partners, Centers of Excellence, and Shared Services."

Expected response: A RACI matrix and handoff protocol establishing HRBPs as strategic advisors to business units, Centers of Excellence (COEs) as owners of global policy and program design, and Shared Services as the single point of execution for transaction processing.

## Step 3: Establish intake and governance

Sample prompt: "Design an interaction model outlining SLA requirements and escalation paths for HR shared services intake."

Expected response: An intake workflow classifying requests by complexity, setting response SLAs for routine inquiries versus policy exceptions, and defining clear escalation paths to prevent ad-hoc requests from bypassing shared services.

## Workflow summary

The CHRO evaluates operating model archetypes against business growth goals, establishes clear handoff boundaries across HR functions, and institutes standardized intake governance to deliver consistent HR services across business units.
