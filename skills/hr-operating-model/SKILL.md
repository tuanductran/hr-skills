# HR operating model design

End-to-end support for designing and optimizing the HR operating model — from defining the three-pillar structure (HRBPs, Centers of Excellence, Shared Services) to governance, role clarity, service delivery design, and transformation roadmaps.

## Supported tasks

- Designing the three-pillar HR operating model
- Defining HRBP roles, accountabilities, and spans
- Building Centers of Excellence (CoE) charters and team structures
- Designing HR Shared Services scope, tiers, and SLAs
- Clarifying role boundaries between HR pillars
- Assessing current-state HR operating model effectiveness
- Designing HR governance and decision-making frameworks
- Planning HR operating model transformation roadmaps
- Rightsizing HR headcount and structure for business scale
- Designing hybrid HR models for matrix organizations
- Building the business case for HR restructuring

## Key prompts

### Operating model design

1. "Design a three-pillar HR operating model for [company size / industry] with clear role boundaries."
2. "What HR operating model structure works best for a [centralized / decentralized / matrix] organization?"
3. "How should we allocate HR headcount across HRBPs, CoEs, and Shared Services for [company size]?"
4. "Design an HR operating model for a company going through [merger / rapid growth / cost reduction]."
5. "What are the key design principles we should apply when restructuring our HR function?"
6. "Compare the Ulrich model, Shared Services only, and embedded HR models for [context]."

### HRBP role design

1. "Define the accountabilities and success metrics for an HR Business Partner supporting [business unit]."
2. "What competencies distinguish a strategic HRBP from a transactional HR generalist?"
3. "How do we transition HRBPs from operational firefighting to strategic advisory roles?"
4. "Write an HRBP role profile for [leadership level / business unit] including key deliverables."
5. "What is the right HRBP-to-employee ratio for [industry] and how does it vary by business complexity?"
6. "How should HRBPs manage their time across strategic, advisory, and operational activities?"

### Center of Excellence design

1. "Design a Talent Management CoE structure for [company] covering [scope areas]."
2. "What should a Compensation and Benefits CoE own versus delegate to HRBPs or Shared Services?"
3. "Write a CoE charter for the [L&D / Recruiting / Total Rewards] function."
4. "How do CoEs maintain enterprise consistency while allowing business unit flexibility?"
5. "What governance mechanisms keep CoE frameworks current and adopted across the business?"

### Shared Services design

1. "Design an HR Shared Services model covering Tier 0, Tier 1, and Tier 2 support for [organization]."
2. "What HR transactions are best suited for Shared Services versus HRBP handling?"
3. "Write SLA commitments for an HR Shared Services center serving [number] employees."
4. "What technology enablers are required to run effective HR Shared Services at [scale]?"
5. "How do we measure Shared Services quality beyond ticket volume and resolution time?"

### Model assessment and transformation

1. "Assess our current HR operating model against best-practice design principles."
2. "What are the warning signs that our HR operating model needs redesign?"
3. "Build a roadmap for transitioning from an embedded HR generalist model to a three-pillar model."
4. "How do we manage change resistance when restructuring the HR function itself?"
5. "What metrics indicate an HR operating model is working effectively?"

## Tips

- Role clarity between pillars is the most common failure point — invest in a RACI before restructuring headcount.
- HRBPs struggle to be strategic when they carry high administrative loads; use Shared Services adoption metrics to identify what's still landing with HRBPs incorrectly.
- CoEs often design excellent frameworks that line managers ignore — build manager capability and simplicity into every CoE output.
- Operating model design must follow business strategy, not HR preferences; start by understanding how the business creates value, then design HR to support it.
- Pilot structural changes in one business unit before full rollout to surface integration problems early.
