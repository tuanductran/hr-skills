# Launching a Regional HR Shared Services Hub

## Context

A multinational manufacturer with HR teams duplicated across 8 Southeast Asian countries wants to consolidate transactional HR work into a single regional hub based in Vietnam.

## Step 1: Define scope

Sample prompt: "Define the scope of services for an HR shared services center covering payroll support, benefits administration, and records management."

Expected response: A scope document listing in-scope processes (payroll data entry, benefits enrollment support, document management) and out-of-scope processes retained locally (employee relations, workforce planning).

## Step 2: Design governance and cost allocation

Sample prompt: "Design a governance model with a steering committee" and "Design a cost allocation model to charge back shared services costs to business units."

Expected response: A steering committee with country HR leads meeting quarterly, plus a chargeback model based on per-employee transaction volume with transparent quarterly cost reporting.

## Step 3: Build the transition and change management plan

Sample prompt: "Build a transition plan for migrating payroll support into a shared services center" and "Draft a change management communication plan for business units affected by centralization."

Expected response: A phased transition plan starting with the two largest countries, plus a communication plan addressing local HR teams' concerns about losing direct control.

## Workflow summary

The company defines a clear scope, builds transparent governance and cost allocation, and phases the rollout with strong change management to reduce resistance from local HR teams.
