# Auditing Contractor Classification Risk

## Context

A company discovers it has 40 "contractors" who have worked full-time hours on company-supervised projects for over two years, and legal wants to assess reclassification risk.

## Step 1: Assess classification risk

Sample prompt: "Assess the classification risk of engaging a worker as an independent contractor for full-time, company-supervised project work over multiple years."

Expected response: An analysis flagging high risk based on factors like direct supervision, integration into regular business operations, long tenure, and lack of independent business operation — all pointing toward employee status under most classification tests.

## Step 2: Build the audit process

Sample prompt: "Design an audit process to review contractor classifications annually."

Expected response: An annual review process checking tenure, degree of supervision, exclusivity, and integration for every active contractor engagement, flagging high-risk cases for legal review.

## Step 3: Plan remediation

Sample prompt: "Design a policy for converting a long-term contractor to a full-time employee."

Expected response: A conversion policy and process for transitioning high-risk long-term contractors to employee status, including timeline, compensation benchmarking, and communication guidance.

## Workflow summary

The company identifies high-risk contractor relationships through a structured audit, then builds a clear conversion pathway to remediate misclassification risk before it results in legal or financial exposure.
