# HR coordination and operations support

Practical support for HR coordinators and People Ops teams managing day-to-day HR operations — from interview scheduling and onboarding coordination to HR project support, cross-team communication, and process documentation.

## Supported tasks

- Coordinating interview scheduling and candidate logistics
- Managing onboarding and offboarding logistics and checklists
- Supporting HR projects and initiative coordination
- Tracking HR program calendars and event logistics
- Coordinating cross-functional HR activities across departments
- Maintaining HR documentation, templates, and process guides
- Managing HR data entry, reporting, and record-keeping
- Supporting benefits enrollment and HR inquiry management
- Coordinating performance review cycles and logistics
- Managing HR vendor and service provider coordination
- Drafting HR communications and announcements
- Coordinating HR compliance activities and training logistics

## Key prompts

### Interview and hiring coordination

1. "Build an interview coordination checklist for [role] covering scheduling, logistics, and candidate communications."
2. "Write a candidate-facing email confirming an interview schedule for [role] including all logistics details."
3. "Design a hiring coordinator workflow that manages [number] interviews per week with minimal errors."
4. "How do we streamline interview scheduling across multiple time zones for [location / remote roles]?"
5. "Write a hiring manager preparation guide that ensures panels are ready before each interview."

### Onboarding coordination

1. "Build a day-one onboarding logistics checklist for [role] at [company location]."
2. "Design a first-week onboarding schedule for [new hire name / role] including key meetings, introductions, and tasks."
3. "Write welcome communications for a new hire joining [department] on [start date]."
4. "How do we coordinate pre-boarding tasks across IT, Facilities, Payroll, and HR before a new hire starts?"
5. "Create an onboarding tracker that HR coordinators can use to monitor completion across all stakeholders."

### HR project and program support

1. "Build a project coordination plan for [HR initiative] including tasks, owners, deadlines, and dependencies."
2. "Design a tracking system for monitoring [performance review / engagement survey / benefits enrollment] completion rates."
3. "Write an HR all-hands agenda for [program or initiative] covering objectives, schedule, and follow-up actions."
4. "How do we coordinate a company-wide HR program rollout across [number] locations?"
5. "What tools help HR coordinators manage multiple concurrent projects without dropping tasks?"

### HR communications and documentation

1. "Write an HR announcement for [policy change / new program / company event] for [employee audience]."
2. "Design a template for documenting HR processes so coordinators can follow them without constant supervision."
3. "Write a FAQ document covering [top 10 employee HR questions] for [company]."
4. "How do we maintain a consistent tone and voice across all HR communications?"
5. "Create a monthly HR calendar template that captures all recurring HR activities and deadlines."

### Cross-functional coordination

1. "Design a coordination protocol between HR and [IT / Finance / Legal] for [onboarding / offboarding / compliance tasks]."
2. "How do we escalate HR coordination issues when other departments are unresponsive or slow?"
3. "Write a RACI for HR coordinator responsibilities across HR, IT, and the business during employee transitions."
4. "How do we track and follow up on open HR coordination tasks without micromanaging stakeholders?"

## Tips

- Build standardized checklists for every recurring coordination task — consistency is more valuable than improvisation in HR operations.
- Proactive communication is the most important HR coordination skill; don't wait to be asked for an update.
- Automate calendar management and reminder workflows where possible; manual scheduling at scale creates errors and rework.
- Create clear escalation paths for coordinators so they know when to handle independently versus when to involve an HRBP or manager.
- Document every process, even informal ones — HR coordinators often become single points of failure when institutional knowledge lives only in their heads.
