# Generative AI for HR professionals

Use Generative AI tools to improve HR productivity, content quality, and decision support across recruiting, performance, learning, communications, and people analytics — with practical guidance for effective and responsible use.

## Supported tasks

- Using GenAI to draft and improve HR documents and communications
- Generating and refining job descriptions, interview questions, and scorecards
- Using GenAI for performance review writing, development plans, and feedback
- Creating HR training content, learning materials, and onboarding documentation
- Using GenAI for HR data summarization and analysis support
- Generating policy drafts, employee communications, and change announcements
- Using GenAI for candidate screening and sourcing support
- Automating repetitive HR writing tasks with AI-assisted workflows
- Building GenAI use standards and guardrails for HR teams
- Evaluating GenAI output quality and editing for accuracy and tone
- Designing HR GenAI workflows that combine human judgment with AI productivity
- Training HR teams to use GenAI tools effectively and responsibly

## Key prompts

### Recruiting and hiring

1. "Use GenAI to write a job description for [role title] at [company type] that is clear, inclusive, and compelling."
2. "Generate a set of competency-based interview questions for assessing [competency] in [role type] candidates."
3. "Summarize this [candidate profile / resume] and identify alignment with our requirements for [role]."
4. "Draft an offer letter for [candidate name] for the [role] position with [compensation details]."
5. "Generate a candidate outreach message for [passive candidate type] on [LinkedIn / email] for [role]."
6. "Use GenAI to write rejection communications for candidates at [screening / interview / final] stage that are respectful and on-brand."

### Performance and feedback

1. "Draft a performance review for [employee name / role] based on the following accomplishments and behaviors: [input]."
2. "Rewrite this performance review draft to be more balanced, specific, and evidence-based."
3. "Generate development plan suggestions for an employee whose strengths are [X] and development areas are [Y]."
4. "Draft a difficult feedback message for a conversation about [performance issue] that is clear but empathetic."
5. "Summarize the key themes from [number] performance review inputs and identify patterns across the team."

### Learning and development

1. "Create a learning module outline for [topic] targeting [audience] with [duration] in mind."
2. "Write five quiz questions to assess understanding of [HR policy / process / concept]."
3. "Generate a manager onboarding guide for [company] covering the first 30 days."
4. "Summarize this [article / research report] and extract the three most actionable insights for HR."
5. "Create case study scenarios for an [unconscious bias / performance management / change leadership] training program."

### HR communications and documents

1. "Draft an all-company announcement for [change] that is clear, honest, and maintains trust."
2. "Rewrite this HR policy in plain language that employees can understand without HR interpretation."
3. "Generate an employee FAQ for [new program / policy / benefit change] covering the top 10 likely questions."
4. "Write a CHRO town hall script for [business update / culture initiative] with key messages and Q&A preparation."
5. "Draft an HR function update for the board covering [people priorities / workforce metrics / program results]."

### GenAI governance for HR

1. "Design a GenAI use policy for HR covering permitted uses, quality review requirements, and prohibited activities."
2. "What guardrails should HR teams apply when using GenAI to draft content that will go directly to employees?"
3. "How do we build a quality review checklist for GenAI-generated HR content before it is shared externally?"
4. "What HR tasks are inappropriate for GenAI use due to sensitivity, accuracy requirements, or legal risk?"
5. "Design GenAI training for HR teams covering effective prompting, output evaluation, and responsible use."

## Tips

- GenAI generates first drafts, not final outputs — always review AI-generated HR content for accuracy, tone, company context, and potential bias before using it.
- The quality of GenAI output is directly proportional to the quality of your prompt — invest time in writing specific, context-rich prompts rather than expecting good results from vague instructions.
- Never enter confidential employee data, compensation details, or personally identifiable information into public GenAI tools; use enterprise-grade tools with appropriate data handling agreements.
- GenAI is most valuable for time-consuming, repetitive writing tasks (first drafts, variations, summaries) — use human judgment for nuanced, high-stakes communications and decisions.
- Build team norms around GenAI use transparency — be clear with colleagues and stakeholders about when GenAI was used to create content and how it was reviewed.
