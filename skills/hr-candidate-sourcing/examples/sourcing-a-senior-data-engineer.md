# Sourcing a Senior Data Engineer

## Context

A recruiter needs to fill a Senior Data Engineer role at a Series C fintech startup within six weeks. Inbound applications have been low-quality, so the team decides to source proactively.

## Step 1: Define the persona

Sample prompt: "Build a candidate persona for a Senior Data Engineer at a Series C fintech company."

Expected response: A persona describing 5-8 years of experience, strong SQL/Python skills, experience with streaming pipelines (Kafka, Spark), prior fintech or high-compliance industry exposure, and likely current titles such as "Data Engineer II" or "Staff Data Engineer" at adjacent companies.

## Step 2: Build the search strategy

Sample prompt: "Write a Boolean search string for a Senior Data Engineer with fintech experience and Kafka/Spark skills."

Expected response: A Boolean string combining title variations, required tools, and industry keywords with AND/OR operators, plus suggested niche communities (data engineering Slack groups, dbt community) for additional sourcing.

## Step 3: Draft outreach

Sample prompt: "Draft a personalized LinkedIn InMail for a passive Senior Data Engineer candidate."

Expected response: A concise message referencing the candidate's likely current work, the company's mission, and a specific reason the role could be a step up, ending with a low-friction call to action (a short call, not a full application).

## Step 4: Build a cadence

Sample prompt: "Write a 3-touch outreach cadence for candidates who haven't responded."

Expected response: Day 0 InMail, Day 4 follow-up with new angle (e.g., a relevant blog post or team highlight), Day 10 final short check-in leaving the door open for future contact.

## Workflow summary

The recruiter defines the persona, builds a targeted search, drafts personalized multi-touch outreach, and logs unresponsive-but-qualified candidates into a pipeline for future roles.
