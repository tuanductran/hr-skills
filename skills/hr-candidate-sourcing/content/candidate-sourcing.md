# Candidate Sourcing

## Overview

Candidate sourcing is the proactive search for potential candidates, as opposed to reactive recruiting from inbound applications. It is the foundation of talent acquisition for hard-to-fill, senior, or niche roles.

## Main topics

### Sourcing channels

- **Professional networks**: LinkedIn, industry-specific platforms (e.g., GitHub for engineers, Behance for designers).
- **Job boards**: general (Indeed) and niche (industry- or skill-specific boards).
- **Communities**: Slack/Discord groups, forums, alumni networks, meetups.
- **Referrals**: employee referral programs, often the highest-quality and fastest channel.
- **Direct sourcing/X-ray search**: using search engines to find public profiles indexed outside a platform's native search.

### Boolean and X-ray search

Boolean search combines keywords with operators (AND, OR, NOT, quotation marks, parentheses) to narrow results on a platform. X-ray search uses a general search engine with a `site:` operator to search a specific platform's indexed pages, useful when native search access is limited.

### Candidate personas

A candidate persona describes the ideal candidate's skills, experience, motivations, and likely current job title, seniority, and company type. Personas keep sourcing focused and help tailor outreach messaging.

### Outreach cadences

A cadence is a planned sequence of touches (message, call, follow-up) spaced over time. Multi-touch cadences typically outperform single-message outreach because passive candidates often need more than one nudge.

### Talent pipelines

A pipeline is a maintained pool of pre-qualified candidates for roles that are recurring or anticipated. Pipelines reduce time-to-fill by giving recruiters a warm starting point instead of sourcing from scratch.

## Practical guidance

- Start with the job requirements, not the channel: define must-have vs. nice-to-have skills before searching.
- Layer channels: combine direct sourcing with referrals and community engagement rather than relying on one method.
- Track sourcing metrics (response rate, conversion to interview, source of hire) to refine strategy over time.
- Respect candidate preferences and opt-outs; unsolicited high-volume outreach damages employer brand.

## Conclusion

Effective sourcing blends structured search techniques, well-crafted personas, thoughtful outreach, and disciplined pipeline maintenance. Recruiters who source proactively and track what works consistently reduce time-to-fill and improve candidate quality.
