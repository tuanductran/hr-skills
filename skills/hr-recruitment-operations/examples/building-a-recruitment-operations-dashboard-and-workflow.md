# Building a recruitment operations dashboard and workflow

## Context

A fast-growing technology company experiences hiring bottlenecks, inconsistent interviewer feedback collection, and inaccurate time-to-fill tracking. The talent acquisition team needs a streamlined recruitment operations framework and reporting dashboard.

## Step 1: Define funnel stages and metrics

Sample prompt: "Write a recruiting funnel stage framework defining standard candidate stages, exit criteria, and key velocity metrics for an ATS implementation."

Expected response: A standardized 6-stage recruiting pipeline (Application Review, Recruiter Screen, Hiring Manager Interview, Technical Assessment, Panel Interview, Offer) with clear transition criteria and target SLAs for each stage.

## Step 2: Configure ATS stage triggers and automation

Sample prompt: "Draft automated email triggers and reminder cadences for candidates and interviewers at each recruiting funnel stage."

Expected response: An automated notification cadence including immediate candidate confirmation emails, 48-hour interviewer feedback scorecards reminders, and automated calendar invitations upon stage progression.

## Step 3: Build executive reporting and review cadence

Sample prompt: "Create a weekly recruiting operations dashboard layout tracking requisition aging, pipeline conversion rates, and interviewer SLA compliance."

Expected response: A dashboard template featuring funnel conversion visualization, requisition bottleneck flags, interviewer scorecard compliance tracking, and weekly talent acquisition review agenda templates.

## Workflow summary

The recruitment operations manager establishes standardized stage definitions, automates administrative reminders within the ATS, and delivers weekly executive dashboards to eliminate pipeline bottlenecks and maintain candidate momentum.
