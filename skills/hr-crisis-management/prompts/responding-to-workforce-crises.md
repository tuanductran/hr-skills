# Responding to workforce crises

Use these prompts when managing immediate HR responses to workforce crises including layoffs, safety incidents, leadership departures, and business disruption.

- "We need to conduct an unplanned layoff affecting 15% of our workforce in the next 72 hours. Walk me through the HR response plan covering legal, communication, logistics, and manager support."
- "A senior leader was terminated for cause this morning and employees are already asking questions. Draft an all-hands communication that is honest, doesn't create legal exposure, and doesn't increase anxiety."
- "A workplace safety incident occurred in our [warehouse/office/field operation] yesterday. What are HR's responsibilities in the first 24 hours?"
- "Our CEO resigned unexpectedly. What should HR do in the first 48 hours to stabilize the workforce, support the board, and prevent talent flight?"
- "A natural disaster has affected our [city/region] office and employees cannot get to work. Build the HR business continuity checklist for the first week."
- "We've received a credible workplace violence threat. What is the HR response protocol, and who are the immediate stakeholders HR must coordinate with?"
- "An employee posted confidential company information on social media. What is the HR response process, and how do we separate the employment action from the legal issue?"
- "Create a 30-day HR stabilization plan for the period immediately following a large reduction in force."
