# Planning multi-country M&A integration

Use these prompts when building integration plans, sequencing HR workstreams across countries, or preparing the deal team for country-specific people risks.

- "Build a 100-day HR integration plan for a cross-border acquisition where the target company has employees in [US, UK, Germany, and India], sequenced around the slowest consultation requirements."
- "Create a country-by-country integration complexity matrix comparing [US, France, Germany, Japan, Brazil] on: consultation requirements, restructuring flexibility, benefit harmonization complexity, and timeline risk."
- "Design the HR data room checklist for a deal that includes employees in [specific countries] — what employee data and contracts do we need from the target before signing?"
- "Write the employee FAQ for [Germany/France/UK] employees explaining the acquisition and what it means for their employment — adapted for local legal constraints on pre-close communications."
- "What cultural integration considerations should the HR team account for when integrating a [German/Indian/Japanese] workforce into a US-headquartered acquirer?"
- "Design a benefits harmonization strategy for a deal that creates employment in [US, UK, Germany, India] — what can be standardized globally and what must remain local?"
- "Create a risk register for the people workstream of a cross-border deal covering [specific country list], categorized by likelihood and impact on integration timeline."
- "How should the integration team sequence works council consultation in Germany alongside Day 1 actions in the US so we don't create legal exposure in either market?"
