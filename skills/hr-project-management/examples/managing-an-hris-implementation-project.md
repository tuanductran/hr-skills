# Scenario: Managing an HRIS Implementation Project

## Context

A company is replacing its legacy HR system with a new cloud HRIS platform, affecting payroll, benefits, and reporting across 2,000 employees.

## Step 1: Draft the charter and scope

Sample prompt: "Draft a project charter for an HRIS implementation" and "Draft a scope document defining what is and isn't included in the HRIS project."

Expected response: A charter naming the executive sponsor, objectives (data migration, process redesign, training), success criteria (go-live with zero payroll disruption), and a scope document explicitly excluding a planned future performance-module rollout from this phase.

## Step 2: Build governance and stakeholder plan

Sample prompt: "Create a governance structure with a steering committee" and "Create a stakeholder map identifying key groups affected by the project."

Expected response: A steering committee of HR, IT, and finance leaders meeting biweekly, and a stakeholder map identifying payroll, managers, and employees as groups needing tailored communication.

## Step 3: Track risk and prepare for go-live

Sample prompt: "Build a risk register identifying top risks and mitigation plans" and "Draft a go-live readiness checklist."

Expected response: A risk register flagging data migration accuracy and user adoption as top risks with named mitigation owners, plus a go-live checklist confirming data validation, training completion, and support coverage before cutover.

## Workflow summary

The project team establishes clear scope and governance up front, actively manages stakeholder communication and risk throughout, and confirms readiness with a structured checklist before going live.
