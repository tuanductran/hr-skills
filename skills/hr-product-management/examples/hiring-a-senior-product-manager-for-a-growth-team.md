# Scenario: Hiring a Senior Product Manager for a Growth Team

## Context

A subscription company wants to hire a Senior PM to own its growth and retention roadmap, and the hiring panel wants a rigorous, consistent evaluation process.

## Step 1: Define the role and case study

Sample prompt: "Write a job description for a Senior Product Manager role in [subscription/growth] focus" and "Design a product-sense case study prompt asking a candidate to improve retention for a subscription product."

Expected response: A job description emphasizing growth and retention ownership, paired with a case study asking the candidate to diagnose a hypothetical drop in renewal rate and propose a prioritized set of experiments.

## Step 2: Build the rubric

Sample prompt: "Create a rubric evaluating product sense, execution ability, and stakeholder leadership."

Expected response: A rubric scoring the candidate's problem diagnosis, experiment prioritization logic, and how clearly they defined success metrics, alongside a separate score for how they'd align engineering and design stakeholders.

## Step 3: Reference-check for outcomes

Sample prompt: "Draft reference-check questions focused on measurable product outcomes a candidate delivered."

Expected response: Reference questions asking the candidate's manager to describe a specific growth initiative the candidate led, the metric it moved, and how much of that outcome was attributable to the candidate's own leadership.

## Workflow summary

The panel builds a realistic, domain-relevant case study, scores candidates on separate product-sense and leadership dimensions, and validates claimed impact through targeted reference questions before making an offer decision.
