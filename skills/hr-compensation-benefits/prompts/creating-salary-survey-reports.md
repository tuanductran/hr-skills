# Creating salary survey reports

This AI assistant can be a valuable tool for creating salary survey reports. It has the ability to process vast amounts of data quickly and provide insights based on that data. By inputting specific information such as job title, geographic location, and experience level, it can generate customized salary reports for various industries and job types. This can save time and effort for those conducting salary surveys, and ensure that the resulting report is accurate and comprehensive.

- "Could you please provide me with a detailed salary report for [job title], [geographic location], and [industry] with [number of years of experience] years of experience, including the median, 25th and 75th percentile salaries, as well as any relevant bonuses, benefits, [variable pay structures], [remote work options], and [other specific criteria]?"
- "What is the average salary range for [industry] professionals in [geographic location], broken down by [job title], [education level], [years of experience], and [other relevant criteria], such as [gender], [ethnicity], [diversity and inclusion], [union membership], [certifications], or [specialized skills]?"
- "How does the average salary for [job title] in [geographic location] compare to the national average, and what are the [specific factors] that contribute to any differences, such as [the type of organization], [location-specific cost of living factors], [COVID-19 pandemic impacts], or [changing industry demands]?"
- "Can you generate a comprehensive salary report for [job title] at [company name] in [geographic location], including [historical salary trends] over the past few years, a comparison to the company's industry peers, [employee satisfaction metrics], and [other relevant metrics or insights], such as [employee retention rates], [work-life balance], or [employee turnover rates]?"
- "What is the median salary and range for [job title] in [geographic location] based on [type of industry], broken down by [company size], [geographic region], [educational level], [certifications], [union membership], and [other specific criteria], such as [gender], [ethnicity], [diversity and inclusion], or [specialized skills]?"
