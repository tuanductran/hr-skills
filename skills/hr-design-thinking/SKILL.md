# Design thinking for HR

Apply human-centered design principles to HR challenges — from empathy research and journey mapping to ideation, rapid prototyping, and testing HR solutions with real employees before full-scale rollout.

## Supported tasks

- Running HR design thinking sprints and workshops
- Conducting employee empathy research and interviews
- Mapping employee and candidate journey experiences
- Facilitating ideation sessions for HR challenges
- Prototyping and testing HR processes and solutions
- Designing HR services from the employee's perspective
- Applying design thinking to onboarding, offboarding, and performance processes
- Building HR team design thinking capability
- Facilitating co-design workshops with employees and managers
- Translating design research into actionable HR solutions
- Measuring prototype effectiveness before full rollout
- Connecting design thinking to HR agile delivery

## Key prompts

### Design thinking process and facilitation

1. "Facilitate a design thinking sprint for [HR challenge] at [company] following the five-stage process."
2. "Design a 2-day HR design sprint agenda for [challenge] involving [employee / manager / HR] participants."
3. "How do we apply design thinking to [onboarding / performance review / offboarding] without extensive time investment?"
4. "What design thinking facilitation techniques work best for HR teams unfamiliar with the methodology?"
5. "How do we build HR team capability in design thinking so we can run sprints independently?"

### Empathy and research

1. "Design an employee empathy interview guide to understand the lived experience of [HR process or moment, for example, performance review, new hire onboarding]."
2. "What research methods reveal the most useful insights about employee needs for [HR challenge]?"
3. "How do we synthesize interview and observation data into clear design insights for an HR team?"
4. "Write an empathy map for [employee persona, for example, new hire, first-time manager, remote employee] experiencing [HR process]."
5. "How do we recruit representative participants for HR design research across [diverse employee population]?"

### Journey mapping

1. "Map the employee journey for [HR process or life event] from the employee's perspective including touchpoints, emotions, and pain points."
2. "Design a candidate journey map for [hiring process] that surfaces the moments that most shape candidate perception."
3. "How do we use journey mapping to identify the highest-usee improvements to [employee experience]?"
4. "Build a manager journey map for [performance review cycle] showing where managers struggle most."
5. "How do we workshop a journey map with employees and managers rather than building it in isolation?"

### Ideation and prototyping

1. "Facilitate a brainstorming session to generate ideas for improving [HR process] using [HMW / SCAMPER / brainwriting] techniques."
2. "Design a rapid prototype for [new onboarding experience / revised performance process] that we can test within two weeks."
3. "How do we evaluate competing HR solution ideas against employee needs, feasibility, and business impact?"
4. "Write a test script for piloting a prototype [HR process change] with [number] employees."
5. "How do we run a low-cost experiment to test whether [proposed HR solution] addresses the employee need?"

### Measurement and iteration

1. "Design a measurement approach for evaluating whether our redesigned [HR process] is better from the employee perspective."
2. "How do we gather feedback from pilot participants in a way that generates actionable improvement insights?"
3. "What metrics should we track over time to know whether our design thinking intervention sustained its impact?"
4. "How do we decide when a piloted HR process change is ready to scale beyond the original pilot group?"

## Tips

- Design thinking works because it forces HR to start with employee needs rather than HR system constraints — protect the empathy research phase from being cut when time is short.
- Prototyping HR solutions before full rollout consistently reveals problems that look obvious in hindsight; invest a week in testing before investing months in implementation.
- Co-design with employees is more powerful than designing for employees — participants who shape a solution are more likely to adopt and champion it.
- Journey mapping is most valuable when it includes emotional states alongside steps — how employees feel at each touchpoint matters as much as what they do.
- Design thinking pairs well with agile HR delivery; use sprints to design, pilots to test, and iterative releases to scale — avoid designing the perfect solution in a vacuum.
