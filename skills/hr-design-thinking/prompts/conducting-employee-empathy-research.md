# Conducting employee empathy research

Use these prompts when planning or conducting empathy research, employee interviews, or journey mapping for HR design projects.

- "Write an empathy interview guide for understanding the lived experience of employees during their first 30 days, focusing on moments of confusion, delight, and unmet expectations."
- "Design a journey mapping session for the candidate experience from job ad to day one, identifying the emotional highs and lows at each stage."
- "Create a research synthesis framework that helps HR turn 15 employee interviews into patterns, insights, and design principles we can act on."
- "What are the most effective empathy research methods for understanding why employees are not using our self-service HR portal — and which method fits a 2-week discovery timeline?"
- "Write a set of observation questions HR can use during a shadowing session with a hiring manager to understand the real friction in our hiring process."
- "How should we structure a co-design session where we invite employees to redesign the elements of our performance process they find least useful?"
- "Create an affinity mapping exercise guide that helps an HR team of 6 synthesize employee research findings into themes and design opportunities."
- "Design a 5-person employee research panel that HR can consult on an ongoing basis as a lightweight alternative to running full discovery research for every HR initiative."
