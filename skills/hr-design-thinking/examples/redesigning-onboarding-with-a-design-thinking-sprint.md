# Redesigning onboarding with a design thinking sprint

## Context

You are an HR Experience Lead at a 500-person technology company. The company's 90-day new hire retention rate has declined for two consecutive quarters, and exit interview data shows "didn't feel connected" and "unclear what was expected" as recurring themes. The CHRO has asked you to redesign the onboarding experience. Rather than starting with a new checklist or a vendor demo, you decide to run a 3-day design thinking sprint to understand the problem before building a solution.

## Step 1 — Frame the problem

**Sample prompt:**

> "We have declining 90-day retention. People say they didn't feel connected or know what was expected. How do I frame this as a design thinking challenge?"

**Expected skill response:**

Translate the business problem into a design challenge before you run a single session:

**Point of view statement:**

> New employees at [Company] need to feel connected to their team and clear on what success looks like within the first 30 days, because without this foundation, they disengage before they have a chance to contribute — and they leave before the 90-day mark.

**How might we questions to anchor the sprint:**

- How might we help new hires build real relationships with their team within the first two weeks?
- How might we make role expectations so clear that a new hire could describe their 90-day goals to a stranger?
- How might we help managers support onboarding without adding significant time to their schedule?
- How might we make the first day feel meaningful rather than administrative?

## Step 2 — Run empathy research (Day 1)

**Sample prompt:**

> "Design the Day 1 empathy research session for our onboarding sprint. We have 8 hours and access to 6 employees who joined in the last 6 months."

**Generated Day 1 plan:**

| Time | Activity | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 9:00-9:30 | Sprint kickoff — brief the team on design thinking process and goals | Shared understanding of the sprint mission |
| 9:30-10:00 | Review existing data — exit interviews, engagement survey comments, 30-day check-in themes | Research brief: what we already know |
| 10:00-12:00 | Employee empathy interviews — 6 x 20-minute interviews, 2 interviewers per session | Raw interview notes |
| 12:00-13:00 | Lunch + individual reflection — each team member reviews their notes alone first | Individual insights |
| 13:00-15:00 | Affinity mapping — team synthesizes interview notes into themes on a virtual board | Insight clusters |
| 15:00-16:00 | Journey mapping — map the new hire experience from offer acceptance through day 60, marking emotional highs and lows | Current-state journey map |
| 16:00-17:00 | Insight framing — convert themes into design insights and finalize "How might we" questions for Day 2 | 5-7 prioritized design opportunities |

**Empathy interview guide:**

> "Tell me about your first week here. Walk me through what actually happened — not what was supposed to happen."
> "When did you first feel like you understood what was expected of you in your role?"
> "Describe a moment in your first 60 days where you felt most connected to the team."
> "What was the most confusing or frustrating part of getting started here?"
> "If you could change one thing about how new people are brought in, what would it be?"

## Step 3 — Ideate solutions (Day 2)

**Sample prompt:**

> "We found that new hires feel like the first day is administrative and they don't meet their skip-level manager until week 3. Generate ideation prompts and help us structure the ideation session."

**Generated Day 2 ideation session:**

**Warm-up (15 minutes):**

Rapid-fire: every person writes one idea per sticky note for 8 minutes — no filtering. Share in round-robin.

**Ideation round 1 — Connection (30 minutes):**

How might we help new hires feel meaningfully connected to their team in the first 5 days?

Idea starters:
- What if the new hire's first meeting was a skip-level conversation about the team's mission?
- What if every new hire was paired with a "first-month buddy" who had joined 6 months before them?
- What if the team published a "what you'll love about working with us and what might frustrate you" document before Day 1?

**Ideation round 2 — Clarity (30 minutes):**

How might we make role expectations concrete enough that the new hire could describe their goals to someone outside the company?

Idea starters:
- What if the hiring manager wrote a "30-60-90 day success story" before the new hire's first day?
- What if new hires had a structured Week 1 conversation with their manager that only covered the three things that matter most in the first 90 days?
- What if role clarity was assessed in a 2-week check-in — not assumed?

**Dot voting:**

Each team member places 3 votes on the ideas they find most feasible and impactful. Top-voted ideas move to prototyping.

## Step 4 — Prototype (Day 3 morning)

**Sample prompt:**

> "We've selected 3 ideas to prototype: a skip-level welcome conversation, a 30-60-90 day success story written by the manager, and a first-month buddy program. Help me design low-fidelity prototypes for each."

**Generated prototypes:**

**Prototype 1 — Skip-level welcome conversation:**

Create a 30-minute agenda template for a skip-level manager to use in week 1:
- What are you most excited to work on here? (5 min)
- Here is what the team is trying to accomplish this quarter and how your role connects (10 min)
- What questions do you have that you might not want to ask your direct manager yet? (10 min)
- Open questions (5 min)

Test: Schedule one skip-level welcome for 5 new hires joining in the next 2 weeks. Measure: did they rate "I understand how my role connects to team goals" higher at the 30-day check-in?

**Prototype 2 — Manager success story:**

Write a one-page template managers complete before a new hire's first day:
- The 3 things I most want you to accomplish in your first 90 days
- How I'll know you're succeeding at the 30-day mark
- The one mistake new people make that I want you to avoid
- The best way to work with me

Test: Ask 5 managers to complete the template for their next hire. Collect new hire feedback at week 2 on whether expectations felt clear from day one.

**Prototype 3 — First-month buddy:**

Match 5 new hires with a buddy (6-18 months tenure) using a 3-question compatibility survey. Provide the buddy with a conversation guide covering: what to share in week 1, week 2, and week 4. Measure: connection scores at 30-day check-in versus baseline.

## Step 5 — Test and measure

**Sample prompt:**

> "We're about to run the three pilots. Help me design the measurement approach so we know whether to scale, iterate, or drop each prototype."

**Generated measurement plan:**

| Prototype | Success metric | Measurement method | Decision threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Skip-level welcome | 30-day "clarity on team goals" score | 2-question pulse added to existing check-in | 20% improvement over baseline |
| Manager success story | 14-day "clear on what's expected" score | Single-question pulse at day 14 | 25% improvement over baseline |
| First-month buddy | 30-day "connected to team" score | Existing 30-day survey question | 15% improvement over baseline |

**Decision criteria:**

- Scale: metric exceeds threshold + qualitative feedback is positive
- Iterate: metric is directionally positive but below threshold — refine and test again
- Drop: metric shows no improvement or negative signal — return to ideation

## Summary

Use `hr-design-thinking` to frame HR problems as human-centered design challenges, run structured sprints that surface employee insights before building solutions, prototype at low cost with real employees, and measure prototype effectiveness before committing to full-scale implementation.
