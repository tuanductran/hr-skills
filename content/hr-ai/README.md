# HR AI: Understanding Artificial Intelligence for Modern Hiring

## Overview

> Related example: [Senior AI Engineer hiring workflow](../../examples/ai/hiring-a-senior-ai-engineer.md).

Artificial Intelligence (AI) in hiring is no longer limited to automation tools or keyword-based screening systems. In modern organizations, AI spans across machine learning systems, generative AI applications, large language models (LLMs), and production-grade AI infrastructure.

For HR professionals and recruiters, understanding AI is not about becoming engineers. It is about developing the ability to evaluate candidates, interpret technical claims, and make informed hiring decisions in a rapidly evolving technical landscape.

## What AI Engineering Actually Means

AI engineering in modern systems typically includes:

- Building applications powered by large language models (LLMs)
- Designing retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems
- Integrating AI into real product workflows
- Deploying models into production environments
- Monitoring model performance and reliability
- Working with vector databases and embeddings
- Orchestrating AI agents and automation pipelines

AI engineering today is less about training models from scratch and more about **system design around AI capabilities**.

## Core AI Concepts HR Should Understand

### Large Language Models (LLMs)

LLMs are deep learning models trained on large-scale text data to generate and understand human-like language. They are the foundation of most modern generative AI systems.

### Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

RAG combines:

- A retrieval system (search over external data)
- A generative model (LLM)

This allows AI systems to produce more accurate and context-aware responses.

### Embeddings and Vector Databases

Embeddings are numerical representations of data (text, images, etc.). They are stored in vector databases to enable semantic search.

### AI Agents

AI agents are systems that:

- Can reason over tasks
- Use tools and APIs
- Execute multi-step workflows autonomously

## How AI Systems Are Built in Practice

Modern AI products typically follow this architecture pattern:

1. User input is received via application layer
2. Input is converted into embeddings
3. Relevant context is retrieved from a vector database
4. Context is passed into a large language model
5. Model generates a response
6. Output is post-processed and returned to the user
7. System logs and monitors performance

This pipeline is often combined with:

- API orchestration layers
- caching systems
- evaluation frameworks
- observability tools

## What HR Should Look For in AI Candidates

### Strong Signals

- Demonstrated experience with production AI systems
- Understanding of system design, not just models
- Experience with LLM APIs or RAG systems
- Ability to explain trade-offs in architecture
- Familiarity with deployment and monitoring

### Weak Signals

- Only building chatbot demos without architecture depth
- Overuse of buzzwords without implementation detail
- No understanding of evaluation or production constraints
- No evidence of real system integration

## Common Misunderstandings in AI Hiring

### AI Engineering is not just Prompt Engineering

Prompting is only one small part of interacting with LLMs. Real AI systems require architecture, retrieval systems, evaluation pipelines, and infrastructure.

### Data Science is not AI Engineering

Data science focuses on:

- Analysis
- Statistics
- Experimentation

AI engineering focuses on:

- Building production systems
- Integrating models into products
- Scaling AI services

### Generative AI is not the entire AI field

Other important areas include:

- Computer vision
- Speech recognition
- Recommendation systems
- Predictive analytics
- Robotics and control systems

## Modern Hiring Reality

AI teams today are expected to:

- Build AI-powered product features
- Integrate LLMs into workflows
- Maintain model performance in production
- Design scalable AI architectures
- Evaluate AI output quality systematically

Hiring AI talent now requires understanding both:

- software engineering fundamentals
- and AI system design principles
